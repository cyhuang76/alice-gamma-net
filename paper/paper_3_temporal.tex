\begin{abstract}
Papers~1 and~2 of this series established the spatial
architecture of impedance-minimising networks
(relay-node emergence, fractal scaling, Murray's Law,
dual neural--vascular organ health
$H = (1-\Gamma_n^2)(1-\Gamma_v^2)$).
Here we develop the full \emph{temporal} theory in two
complementary parts.

\textbf{Part~A} introduces \emph{impedance debt}
$D_Z = \int |\Gamma|^2 P_{\mathrm{in}}\,dt$:
the time-integrated mismatch energy that accumulates
during wakefulness.
We show that sleep is the minimum-action mechanism
for discharging $D_Z$---a thermodynamic necessity,
not a behavioural choice.
Decomposing $D_Z$ into microscopic (synaptic drift)
and thermal (waste heat) components explains why
jellyfish sleep without a brain
($D_{\mathrm{thermal}}$ removed by ocean)
and why the water-to-land transition imposes a physical
constraint favouring centralised neural architecture
($D_{\mathrm{thermal}}$ must be managed internally).
A dual-field extension ($Z$-field + material field $\rho$)
unifies Wolff's law (bone), Davis's law (muscle),
and coefficient update (neurons) as parameter
instances of a single impedance-matching PDE,
with embryogenesis recapitulating the thermodynamic
causal chain (heart $\to$ neural tube $\to$ brain).

\textbf{Part~B} derives the \emph{Lifecycle Equation},
\begin{equation*}
  \frac{d(\Sigma\Gamma^2)}{dt}
    = \underbrace{-\eta\,\Sigma\Gamma^2}_{\text{learning}}
    + \underbrace{\gamma\,\Gamma_{\mathrm{env}}(t)}_{\text{novelty}}
    + \underbrace{\delta\,D(t)}_{\text{aging}}\,,
\end{equation*}
governing the complete cognitive trajectory from birth to
senescence as a three-force competition on the dual
neural--vascular network.
The resulting \emph{bathtub curve} of $\Sigma\Gamma^2$
reproduces the known developmental trajectory without
any fitted function.
Emotion, reward, stress, curiosity, and boredom emerge as
\emph{readouts} of $\Gamma$ dynamics at different time scales:
dopamine encodes $-\Delta\Gamma^2$, cortisol encodes chronic
$\Gamma^2$ elevation, curiosity encodes the impedance gradient,
and boredom encodes stagnation at non-zero mismatch.
Aging is derived as Coffin--Manson fatigue accelerated by
Arrhenius stress kinetics, with
atherosclerosis as vascular fatigue and the cascade
$\Gamma_v\!\uparrow \to \rho\!\downarrow \to \Gamma_n\!\uparrow$
explaining why cardiovascular disease precedes
neurodegeneration.

\textbf{Part~C} proves that C2 is the \emph{universal}
update law: we map all twenty-nine known
tissue-update mechanisms to the single rule
$\Delta Z = -\eta\,\Gamma\,x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}$,
identify the thermodynamic boundaries of life
(ice, fire, Goldilocks 37\textdegree C), and derive the
two central controllers (cardiac pump, regulatory brain)
whose failure propagates $\Gamma$ error to every
downstream organ.
Together, Parts~A--C establish that temporal dynamics,
lifecycle, and tissue adaptation are all consequences of
$\mathcal{A}[\Gamma] \to \min$.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Paper~1~\cite{Paper1} showed that the Minimum
Reflection Action $\mathcal{A}[\Gamma] = \int |\Gamma|^2\,dt$
on an impedance-heterogeneous network forces hierarchical
relay-node emergence, with dimensional cut cost $A_{\mathrm{cut}}$
irreducible by any local rule; fractal scaling recovers
Kleiber's $B \sim M^{3/4}$ and Murray's branching exponent.
Paper~2~\cite{Paper2} extended the framework to vascular
networks and established the dual-network organ model
$H = (1-\Gamma_n^2)(1-\Gamma_v^2)$.

These results are \emph{spatial}: they describe the
equilibrium architecture of impedance networks.
But biological networks operate in time.
Action potentials flow, ion channels drift,
metabolic heat accumulates.
What is the cost of running the network?

This paper extends the Minimum Reflection Action to the
time domain.
Part~A defines impedance debt $D_Z$---the integral of
$|\Gamma|^2$ weighted by incident power---and shows that
sleep is its minimum-action discharge mechanism.
The decomposition of $D_Z$ into microscopic and thermal
components provides physical constraints consistent with
cnidarian sleep, the water-to-land neural transition,
and embryogenesis as a single thermodynamic causal chain.
Part~B derives the Lifecycle Equation---the first-order ODE
governing $\Sigma\Gamma^2$ from birth to death---and shows
that cognition, emotion, and aging are three forces competing
on the same impedance network.

\section{Physical Framework}
\label{sec:framework}

\subsection{Impedance mismatch and the three constraints}

At every signalling interface, the reflection coefficient is
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma = \frac{Z_{\mathrm{load}} - Z_{\mathrm{source}}}
               {Z_{\mathrm{load}} + Z_{\mathrm{source}}}\,.
  \label{eq:gamma}
\end{equation}
The three inviolable constraints are:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{C1 (Energy Conservation)}:
    $\Gamma^2 + T = 1$ at every interface, every tick.
  \item \textbf{C2 (Coefficient Update)}:
    $\Delta Z = -\eta\,\Gamma\,x_{\mathrm{in}}\,x_{\mathrm{out}}$.
  \item \textbf{C3 (Dimensional Relay)}:
    All inter-module signals are \texttt{ElectricalSignal}
    objects carrying $Z$-metadata.
\end{itemize}


\subsection{Neurons as transmission lines}
\label{sec:neuron-TL}

A myelinated axon has characteristic impedance
\begin{equation}
  Z_0 = \sqrt{\frac{R + j\omega L}{G + j\omega C}}\,,
  \label{eq:Z0}
\end{equation}
where $R$, $L$, $G$, $C$ are the distributed per-unit-length
resistance, inductance, conductance, and capacitance
of the neural membrane.
At the synapse, $Z_0$ meets the postsynaptic input impedance
$Z_{\mathrm{post}}$, producing a reflection coefficient
$\Gamma = (Z_{\mathrm{post}} - Z_0)/(Z_{\mathrm{post}} + Z_0)$.
Perfect transmission ($\Gamma = 0$) requires impedance matching;
any mismatch wastes energy as reflected power.

Over time, ion-channel conductances drift (fatigue,
temperature fluctuation, molecular turnover), shifting both
$Z_0$ and $Z_{\mathrm{post}}$ away from their initial values.
This drift accumulates as \emph{impedance debt}.


\subsection{Definition of impedance debt}
\label{sec:DZ-def}

Define the impedance debt at time $t$ as
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  D_Z(t) = \int_0^{t} \frac{1}{N}
    \sum_{i=1}^{N} |\Gamma_i(t')|^2\,
    P_{\mathrm{in},i}(t')\,dt'
  \;}
  \label{eq:DZ}
\end{equation}
where $N$ is the number of signalling interfaces and
$P_{\mathrm{in},i}$ is the incident power at interface~$i$.
$D_Z$ has units of energy and is monotonically
non-decreasing during wakefulness
(active signalling guarantees $P_{\mathrm{in}} > 0$).

When the impedance landscape changes slowly relative to
firing rate, $\langle|\Gamma|^2\rangle$ is approximately
constant over a wake epoch.
Then $D_Z$ grows linearly:
\begin{equation}
  D_Z(t) \approx \langle|\Gamma|^2\rangle\,
    \bar{P}_{\mathrm{in}}\,t\,.
  \label{eq:DZ-linear}
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[Impedance debt monotonicity]
\label{prop:dz-monotone}
During wakefulness ($P_{\mathrm{in}} > 0$), impedance
debt $D_Z(t)$ is monotonically non-decreasing.
Sleep ($P_{\mathrm{in}} \to 0$) is the only state in
which $D_Z$ can decrease.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By definition (Eq.~\ref{eq:DZ}),
$dD_Z/dt = N^{-1} \sum_i |\Gamma_i|^2 P_{\mathrm{in},i}
\ge 0$ whenever $P_{\mathrm{in},i} > 0$.
Equality holds only when all $P_{\mathrm{in},i} = 0$
(global decoupled state = sleep).
\end{proof}


\subsection{Adenosine as a $D_Z$ readout}
\label{sec:adenosine}

Adenosine accumulates extracellularly in proportion to
metabolic demand---specifically, to ATP hydrolysis rate,
which is proportional to $P_{\mathrm{dissipated}} =
\Gamma^2 \cdot P_{\mathrm{in}}$.
Thus extracellular adenosine concentration
$[\mathrm{Ade}](t) \propto D_Z(t)$.
This identifies adenosine as a \emph{local impedance-debt
meter}---consistent with its established role as the
primary molecular correlate of sleep pressure
(Borb\'{e}ly's Process~S~\cite{Borbely1982}).


\subsection{$D_Z$ decomposition: microscopic and thermal}
\label{sec:DZ-decomp}

The total impedance debt decomposes into two physically
distinct components:
\begin{equation}
  D_Z = D_{\mathrm{micro}} + D_{\mathrm{thermal}}\,.
  \label{eq:DZ-decomp}
\end{equation}
$D_{\mathrm{micro}}$ (synaptic impedance drift) is the
cumulative mismatch from ion-channel stochastic drift,
receptor desensitisation, and molecular turnover.
$D_{\mathrm{thermal}}$ (waste heat) is the $\Gamma^2$-derived
dissipation that raises local tissue temperature.
This decomposition has physical consequences:
organisms immersed in high-$\kappa$ media (ocean) can
passively dissipate $D_{\mathrm{thermal}}$ but must still
manage $D_{\mathrm{micro}}$ via sleep-like quiescence.


\subsection{Dual-network impedance debt}
\label{sec:dual-network-debt}

Paper~2~\cite{Paper2} established that organ health depends
on two impedance networks.
Impedance debt likewise splits:
\begin{equation}
  D_Z = D_{Z,n} + D_{Z,v}\,,
  \label{eq:DZ-dual}
\end{equation}
where $D_{Z,n}$ is neural and $D_{Z,v}$ is vascular debt.
The discharge time constants differ by orders of magnitude:
neural debt discharges on the timescale of a night's sleep
($\eta_n \sim 0.08$);
vascular debt discharges on weeks to months
($\eta_v \sim 0.001$).
This asymmetry means nightly sleep primarily services
$D_{Z,n}$, while $D_{Z,v}$ accumulates chronically
unless offset by vascular remodeling.
The coupling from Paper~2,
$I_{\mathrm{blood}} = (1-\Gamma_v^2)\,Q_0$,
means rising vascular debt ($\Gamma_v\!\uparrow$) starves
neural repair of material ($\rho\!\downarrow$),
amplifying neural debt in a positive-feedback loop.


\subsection{Sleep as impedance-debt discharge}
\label{sec:sleep}

Sleep---formally, a \emph{global decoupled dissipation
state} in which external drive is removed
($P_{\mathrm{in}} \to 0$) so that the intrinsic
restorative term $-\eta\,\Gamma^2$ dominates the
impedance ODE---serves as the system's offline
maintenance phase.
During NREM sleep, the brain activates a recalibration
mode: reduced sensory input
halts $D_Z$ accumulation, while slow oscillations drive
$Z$-map restoration via C2 coefficient update.
The discharge rate follows:
\begin{equation}
  D_Z(t) = D_Z^{\mathrm{peak}}\,
    e^{-\beta_{\mathrm{recal}}\,(t - t_{\mathrm{sleep}})}\,,
  \label{eq:DZ-decay}
\end{equation}
where $\beta_{\mathrm{recal}}$ is the N3 recalibration rate.
The organism sleeps until $D_Z < D_Z^*$ (threshold).

The maximum tolerable wake duration before $D_Z$ exceeds
a critical threshold $D_Z^*$ is:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  T_{\mathrm{wake}}^{\max}
    = \frac{D_Z^*}
           {\langle|\Gamma|^2\rangle\,\bar{P}_{\mathrm{in}}}
  \;}
  \label{eq:Twake}
\end{equation}
This explains why infants ($\langle\Gamma^2\rangle \approx 0.12$)
sleep $\sim$16\,h/day while adults
($\langle\Gamma^2\rangle \approx 0.04$) need only $\sim$8\,h:
lower average mismatch means slower debt accumulation.

\paragraph{Quantitative link to glymphatic clearance.}
The exponential $D_Z$ discharge (Eq.~\ref{eq:DZ-decay})
has a direct biophysical correlate: the glymphatic system.
Xie et al.\ showed that interstitial space expands by
$\sim$60\% during NREM sleep, accelerating
cerebrospinal-fluid (CSF) convective clearance of
solutes including amyloid-$\beta$~\cite{Xie2013}.
Fultz et al.\ subsequently demonstrated that large-amplitude
CSF oscillations are phase-locked to NREM slow
waves~\cite{Fultz2019}.
In $\Gamma$-Net language, glymphatic flow is the
\emph{physical transport mechanism} that
realises $D_Z$ discharge: reduced sensory drive
($P_{\mathrm{in}} \to 0$) allows C2 update
to dominate, while the 60\% expansion of
interstitial space lowers the effective hydraulic
impedance $Z_{\mathrm{ISF}}$, increasing the
transmission coefficient $T = 1 - \Gamma^2$ for
waste-clearance channels.
The prediction is quantitative:
$\beta_{\mathrm{recal}}$ in Eq.~\ref{eq:DZ-decay}
should scale with CSF flow rate, and any intervention
that reduces glymphatic efficiency (e.g.\ aquaporin-4
knockout~\cite{Nedergaard2013})
should prolong the time required for $D_Z < D_Z^*$,
i.e.\ increase sleep need---consistent with
observation.

\subsection{Forced awakening and the gate-leak inequality}
\label{sec:forced-awakening}

Sleep requires that all thalamic gates satisfy
$G_i \approx 0$, so the television inequality
(Paper~4~\cite{Paper4}) falls below threshold:
$Z_{\mathrm{arousal}}\sum_i G_i \cdot Q_{\text{PFC}} < \theta$.
However, a gate is an impedance attenuator, not a
perfect open circuit.  Even at $G_i \ll 1$
the \emph{leaked power} on channel~$i$ is
\begin{equation}
  P_{\mathrm{leak},i}
  \;=\;
  G_i^2\;\Gamma_i^2\;P_{\mathrm{in},i}\,.
  \label{eq:gate-leak}
\end{equation}
The organism is forced awake whenever
\emph{any single channel's leak exceeds the
Johnson--Nyquist noise floor}:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  G_i^2\;\Gamma_i^2\;P_{\mathrm{in},i}
  \;>\;
  4\,k_B T\,R_{\mathrm{eff}}\,\Delta f
  \;\equiv\;
  P_{\theta}
  \;}
  \label{eq:forced-wake}
\end{equation}
This is the \textbf{gate-leak inequality}: the
physical condition for forced awakening.
Three routes satisfy it:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{External threat}:
    $P_{\mathrm{in},i}$ spikes (loud noise, pain,
    predator vibration).
    The startle circuit
    (Paper~4~\cite{Paper4})
    drives $G_{\text{threat}} \to 1$ within
    $\sim$5\,ms, far faster than cortical processing.
    This is the ancestral survival mechanism.
  \item \textbf{Endogenous alarm}:
    $\Gamma_i^2$ is chronically elevated on an
    internal channel (anxiety, rumination,
    pain).  Even with $G_i$ nominally low
    and $P_{\mathrm{in},i}$ at resting baseline,
    the product $\Gamma_i^2 P_{\mathrm{in},i}$
    exceeds the gate's attenuation capacity.
    The channel ``bleeds through'' and
    re-satisfies the television inequality
    $\to$ \textbf{insomnia}.
  \item \textbf{Thermal discomfort}:
    $T_{\mathrm{body}}$ drifts outside the
    Arrhenius operating range
    ($T < T_c$ or fever $T > T_{\max}$),
    hypothalamic thermoreceptors fire at
    high $P_{\mathrm{in}}$, and the thermal
    channel leaks through $\to$ awakening.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
Insomnia (route~2) is therefore not a ``failure to relax''
but a \emph{C1 consequence}: a channel whose
$\Gamma_i^2$ is structurally forged at high mismatch
(trauma, chronic stress) radiates enough leaked power
to keep the consciousness loop above threshold.
The gate is doing its job---it simply cannot attenuate
a signal that strong.
Treatment must reduce $\Gamma_i^2$ itself (C2
remodelling over long time horizons---Paper~3, Remark~4)
or reduce $P_{\mathrm{in},i}$ (removing the stressor),
not ``try harder to sleep'':
gating~$G_i$ is already at its physical minimum.

\subsection{Narcolepsy and hypersomnia:
  the arousal-collapse dual}
\label{sec:narcolepsy-hypersomnia}

Insomnia is the gate-leak failure mode:
too much signal penetrates a closed gate.
Narcolepsy and hypersomnia are the \emph{dual}
failure: the arousal or gating system itself
is structurally compromised.

\paragraph{Narcolepsy (arousal anchor loss).}
The television inequality
(Paper~4~\cite{Paper4})
requires $Z_{\mathrm{arousal}} > Z_{\min}$
for consciousness.
$Z_{\mathrm{arousal}}$ is held at its operating point
by the orexin (hypocretin) system,
which acts as a \emph{structural impedance anchor}:
\begin{equation}
  Z_{\mathrm{arousal}}(t)
  \;=\;
  Z_{\mathrm{anchor}}
  + \delta Z(t)\,,
  \qquad
  |\delta Z| \ll Z_{\mathrm{anchor}}\,.
  \label{eq:arousal-anchor}
\end{equation}
When the orexin-producing neurons are destroyed
(autoimmune ablation in type-1 narcolepsy),
$Z_{\mathrm{anchor}} \to 0$ and
$Z_{\mathrm{arousal}}$ becomes a random walk:
\begin{equation}
  Z_{\mathrm{anchor}} = 0
  \;\;\Longrightarrow\;\;
  P(Z_{\mathrm{arousal}} < Z_{\min})
  \;\propto\; t^{1/2}\,.
  \label{eq:narcolepsy}
\end{equation}
The system crosses threshold at unpredictable intervals
$\to$ \textbf{sleep attacks}.
Cataplexy (sudden muscle-tone loss) is the
motor-gate analogue: an emotional $\Gamma^2$ spike
in the limbic system leaks through the
brainstem motor-gate and drives
$G_{\text{motor}} \to 0$---the same gate-leak
mechanism as Eq.~\eqref{eq:forced-wake},
but acting on the descending motor channel
rather than the ascending sensory channel.

\paragraph{Hypersomnia ($\beta_{\mathrm{recal}}$ deficit).}
In idiopathic hypersomnia (IH), the arousal anchor
is intact ($Z_{\mathrm{anchor}} > 0$) and there is
no gate-leak.
Instead, the NREM recalibration rate~$\beta_{\mathrm{recal}}$
in Eq.~\eqref{eq:DZ-decay} is structurally low:
\begin{equation}
  \beta_{\mathrm{recal}}^{\text{IH}}
  \;\ll\;
  \beta_{\mathrm{recal}}^{\text{normal}}
  \;\;\Longrightarrow\;\;
  T_{\mathrm{sleep}}^{\text{IH}}
  = \frac{1}{\beta_{\mathrm{recal}}^{\text{IH}}}
    \ln\!\frac{D_Z^{\mathrm{peak}}}{D_Z^*}
  \;\gg\; T_{\mathrm{sleep}}^{\text{normal}}\,.
  \label{eq:hypersomnia}
\end{equation}
The organism requires far longer sleep to discharge the
same $D_Z$, and waking before $D_Z < D_Z^*$
produces ``sleep inertia''---the persistent
fog of unfinished recalibration.
Excessive daytime sleepiness follows because
residual $D_Z$ raises $\langle\Gamma^2\rangle$
on all channels, so the gate-leak condition
$G_i^2\,\Gamma_i^2\,P_{\mathrm{in}} < P_\theta$
\emph{barely holds}: the smallest increase in
$P_{\mathrm{in}}$ (e.g.\ a warm room, monotonous
stimuli) tips any channel past threshold and the
system collapses back into sleep.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}llll@{}}
\hline

  & Gate-leak status \\
\hline

  & $\Gamma_i^2$ too high
  & Leak $> P_\theta$ while gates closed
  & Cannot close \\

  & $Z_{\mathrm{anchor}} \to 0$

  & Gates collapse suddenly \\

  & $\beta_{\mathrm{recal}}$ too low

  & Gates barely holding \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent
All three are \emph{structural} impedance failures
(different $Z_s$ parameters) sharing a common
C1 framework.
None is a ``psychological'' disorder;
each specifies which physical parameter must be
targeted for treatment: lower~$\Gamma_i^2$
(insomnia), restore~$Z_{\mathrm{anchor}}$
(narcolepsy, via orexin agonists),
or raise~$\beta_{\mathrm{recal}}$
(hypersomnia, via GABA$_\text{A}$ modulation or
wake-promoting agents that reduce residual~$D_Z$).


\subsection{NREM/REM partition}
\label{sec:NREM-REM}

NREM sleep discharges $D_Z$ (impedance recalibration).
REM sleep reorganises the $Z$-map topology
(adding/pruning connections).
The required REM fraction scales with topological complexity:
\begin{equation}
  T_{\mathrm{REM}} \propto N_{\mathrm{topo}}\,,
  \label{eq:REM-fraction}
\end{equation}
where $N_{\mathrm{topo}}$ is the number of active nodes
in the impedance network.
This explains the high REM fraction in neonates
($\sim$50\%, rapidly growing $N$) versus adults
($\sim$25\%, stable $N$).

\section{Dual-Field Extension: Morphogenesis}
\label{sec:dual-field}

When impedance varies in space (tissue structure)
and is coupled to a material-supply field,
the framework becomes a reaction--diffusion system:
\begin{align}
  \frac{\partial Z}{\partial t}
    &= D_Z\,\nabla^2 Z
    \underbrace{-\eta\,f(\rho)\,\Gamma\,Z}_{\text{C2 update}}
    \underbrace{-\,E(\Gamma^2)\,(-\Gamma)\,\rho}_{\text{enzyme}}
    - \lambda\,Z\,,
  \label{eq:Z-field} \\[4pt]
  \frac{\partial\rho}{\partial t}
    &= D_\rho\,\nabla^2\rho
    + I_{\mathrm{blood}}
    - c(\Gamma^2)\,\rho\,,
  \label{eq:rho-field}
\end{align}
where:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $D_Z$, $D_\rho$ are diffusion coefficients,
  \item $f(\rho) = \rho/(\rho + K_\rho)$: material-limited
    repair (Michaelis--Menten form),
  \item $E(\Gamma^2)$: Hill enzyme kinetics,
  \item $I_{\mathrm{blood}} = (1-\Gamma_v^2)\,Q_0$: vascular
    supply from Paper~2,
  \item $\lambda$: entropic degradation rate.
\end{itemize}

The factor $(-\Gamma)$ in the enzyme term ensures
bidirectional remodeling: building when $Z < Z_0$
($\Gamma < 0$, osteoblast-like) and degrading when
$Z > Z_0$ ($\Gamma > 0$, osteoclast-like)---the
mathematical expression of Wolff's law~\cite{Wolff1892}.
Under appropriate parameters, the same PDE reduces to
Davis's law (muscle) and coefficient update (neurons).

When the diffusion ratio $D_\rho / D_Z$ exceeds a
tissue-specific threshold, the uniform steady state
becomes unstable via Turing instability~\cite{Turing1952},
producing spatial patterns in the $Z$-field
(bone trabecular architecture, hepatic lobule periodicity).

The material-bottleneck limit ($\rho \to 0$) yields:
\begin{equation}
  \rho \to 0 \;\Longrightarrow\;
  \frac{\partial Z}{\partial t} \to -\lambda\,Z\,.
  \label{eq:ischemia-limit}
\end{equation}
Only entropic degradation survives---no repair is possible
without material supply.

\section{Evidence I: Cnidarian Sleep Without a Brain}
\label{sec:cnidaria}

\textit{Cassiopea} satisfies all sleep criteria
(reduced activity, increased arousal threshold,
homeostatic rebound~\cite{Nath2017}).
Appelbaum \emph{et al.}~\cite{Appelbaum2026} showed
that neuronal DNA double-strand breaks accumulate linearly
with wake duration and are repaired during sleep.

In the impedance-debt framework, DNA damage is a
\emph{downstream marker} of $D_Z$:
the causal chain is
$\Gamma^2\!\uparrow \to T\!\downarrow \to$
mitochondrial compensation $\to$ ROS $\to$ DNA damage.
Jellyfish, immersed in an ocean with volumetric heat
capacity $\sim$3\,500$\times$ that of air,
passively dissipate $D_{\mathrm{thermal}}$.
Yet $D_{\mathrm{micro}}$ (ion-channel drift) still
accumulates, requiring sleep-like quiescence for discharge.
Hence: \textbf{no brain is needed, but sleep is still required.}

\section{Physical Constraints on Neural Architecture}
\label{sec:brains}

Complex nervous systems appear independently in at least
nine metazoan lineages~\cite{Moroz2012}.
The $\Gamma$-framework does not explain this
convergence but identifies the \emph{physical constraints}
that any nervous system must satisfy.

\subsection{Impedance constraint on active foraging}

An organism that transitions from passive filter-feeding
to active predation gains a new signalling channel
with lower $\Gamma^2$, but this channel requires
coordinated impedance management across sensors,
appendages, and a controller (i.e., a nervous system).
Each additional sensory--motor loop increases the
irreducible cut cost $A_{\mathrm{cut}}$ (Paper~1).
The $\Gamma$-framework thus provides a \emph{necessary
condition}: any organism with $N$ coordinated channels
must pay at least $A_{\mathrm{cut}}(N)$ in reflected energy.
Whether this path is realised depends on
thermodynamic boundary conditions, not on $\Gamma$ alone.

\subsection{Thermal constraint on the water-to-land transition}

Organisms on land exchange seawater
($\kappa \ge 1\;\mathrm{W\,m^{-1}K^{-1}}$)
for air ($\kappa \le 0.04$).
$D_{\mathrm{thermal}}$ can no longer be passively removed.
The $\Gamma$-framework identifies three \emph{physical
necessities} for any land-dwelling organism with
significant neural complexity:
(i)~a convective heat-transport network (vasculature---Paper~2),
(ii)~a centralised impedance-debt manager (brain),
(iii)~structured sleep architecture for $D_{\mathrm{micro}}$
discharge (NREM/REM).
These are constraints, not causal explanations;
thermodynamics constrains \emph{which} lineages
actually colonise land.
Cephalopods remained marine but attained high metabolic
rates that challenge oceanic heat-sink capacity,
consistent with their distributed brain architecture
(nine ganglia)---Experiment~A7 at
$\kappa_{\mathrm{cross}} \approx 0.30$.

The tidal zone acts as a quarter-wave impedance matcher:
$Z_{\mathrm{match}} = \sqrt{Z_w Z_l}$, with a logarithmic
$\kappa$ gradient reducing the transition action
$\mathcal{A}[\Gamma]$ to 6.2\% of a direct jump
(Experiment~A6).
The framework thus predicts that tidal zones offer the
lowest-action path between aquatic and terrestrial
impedance regimes---a testable geometric prediction,
not a historical cause.

\subsection{Thermal refugia: the $D_Z$-clearance
  boundary crisis}
\label{sec:thermal-refugia}

Once on land, high-$K$ organisms face a second constraint.
To discharge accumulated impedance debt~$D_Z$, the system
must enter a low-power state (sleep), reducing metabolic
heat generation to a basal level~$P_{\text{basal}}$.
System temperature during sleep obeys
\begin{equation}
  T_{\text{sys}}
  = T_{\text{env}}
  + P_{\text{basal}}\,R_{\text{th}}\,,
  \label{eq:T-sleep-P3}
\end{equation}
where $R_{\text{th}}$ is the organism's effective thermal
resistance---a single lumped parameter integrating fur,
blubber, posture, and conspecific huddling.
The C2 boundary condition demands
$T_{\text{sys}} > T_c(K)$ at all times;
violation halts remodelling and freezes the impedance
mismatch in place.

During nocturnal or seasonal temperature drops,
$T_{\text{env}}$ may swing by tens of kelvin.
When $P_{\text{basal}}\,R_{\text{th}}$ alone cannot
guarantee the inequality, the organism must couple to
an \emph{environmental thermal low-pass filter}---any
high-heat-capacity mass whose thermal inertia damps
the external oscillation.
At depth~$z \gg \delta =
\sqrt{2\kappa/(\rho c_p\,\omega)}$, the local
temperature converges to the annual
mean~$\overline{T}_{\text{annual}}$.
Ecologically such environments (subterranean caverns,
tree hollows, snow caves, conspecific huddles) are
collectively termed \emph{thermal refugia}; physically,
they are low-pass filters that replace the volatile
$T_{\text{env}}$ with a stable baseline.

The resulting constraint chain is:
high~$K$ $\to$ high~$T_c(K)$ $\to$
sleep reduces $P_{\text{met}}$ $\to$
$P_{\text{basal}}\,R_{\text{th}}$ may be insufficient
$\to$ external thermal coupling required.
Low-$K$ organisms ($T_c \ll 0\,^\circ$C) satisfy the
inequality with biological insulation alone.
93~tests verify the full derivation from Arrhenius
$\eta(T)$ through thermal-refugium damping to
survival margin.

\begin{remark}[Recapitulation as impedance-threshold ordering]
\label{rem:recapitulation}
Haeckel's recapitulation law---``ontogeny recapitulates
phylogeny''---has long been regarded as a descriptive
observation without mechanistic explanation.
The $\Gamma$-framework supplies one.

Define the \emph{critical diffusion length}:
\begin{equation}
  \ell^* = \sqrt{\frac{2\,D_{\mathrm{O}_2}\,
      C_{\mathrm{surface}}}{\dot{m}}}\,,
  \label{eq:critical-length}
\end{equation}
where $D_{\mathrm{O}_2}$ is the oxygen diffusion
coefficient in tissue, $C_{\mathrm{surface}}$ is the
surface oxygen concentration, and $\dot{m}$ is the
volumetric metabolic rate.
When body thickness $\ell < \ell^*$, passive diffusion
maintains $\Gamma_v < 1$ at all interior points;
when $\ell > \ell^*$, interior cells reach
$\Gamma_v \to 1$ (anoxia) and a dedicated pump
(heart) becomes a thermodynamic necessity.

The thermodynamic progression is then an ordered sequence
of impedance thresholds:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Passive distribution}
    ($\ell < \ell^*$):
    sponges (Porifera, $\sim$600\,Ma)---no neurons,
    no pump.
  \item \textbf{Diffuse dual network}
    ($\ell < \ell^*$, but $\Gamma_n$ emerges for
     coordination):
    cnidarians (jellyfish, $\sim$550\,Ma)---nerve
    net, gastrovascular cavity, no centralised pump.
  \item \textbf{Active circulation}
    ($\ell > \ell^*$):
    annelids ($\sim$500\,Ma)---first dedicated hearts.
  \item \textbf{Internal thermal management}
    ($D_{\mathrm{thermal}}$ no longer passively
     dissipated):
    water-to-land transition ($\sim$375\,Ma)---demands
    centralised neural control (PFC).
\end{enumerate}

Embryogenesis replays this sequence not by historical
accident but by physical causality: layer~$k\!+\!1$ of
the impedance hierarchy requires
$\rho_k = (1 - \Gamma_k^2)\,Q_0 > 0$ from layer~$k$.
The heart \emph{must} precede the neural tube because
neural C2 requires material supply from vascular
transmission.

\textbf{Structural epochs.}
This threshold ordering partitions thermodynamic history
into \emph{structural epochs}---geological eras whose
biota share a common impedance-threshold tier:
(i)~membrane epoch ($>$2\,Ga; single-cell
$\Gamma$-interfaces),
(ii)~diffusion epoch ($\sim$600\,Ma;
multicellular, $\ell < \ell^*$),
(iii)~pump epoch ($\sim$500\,Ma;
$\ell > \ell^*$, dedicated hearts),
(iv)~endothermic epoch ($\sim$200\,Ma; internal
$D_{\mathrm{thermal}}$ management, PFC).
Mass extinctions eliminate species \emph{within}
a tier but never regress the structural level:
after the end-Cretaceous event (66\,Ma),
all surviving macrofauna retained active circulation.
Regression is physically forbidden because
at $\ell > \ell^*$ a pumpless body reaches
$\Gamma_v \to 1$ (anoxia).

\textbf{Why the heart grows first.}
Every extant vertebrate embryo develops in the
pump epoch.
Active circulation is therefore the minimum physical
condition for subsequent tissue viability:
the heart is not a historical ``choice'' but
the thermodynamic verdict of $\ell > \ell^*$.
Genes encode the \emph{execution} of this
physically mandatory sequence;
they do not \emph{choose} it.

\textbf{Falsifiable prediction.}
No extant or fossil multicellular animal with body
thickness $\ell > \ell^*$ (given its metabolic rate)
survives without some form of active circulatory
mechanism.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Thermodynamic progression across
  extinction boundaries]
\label{rem:thermodynamic-progression}
The Lifecycle Equation (Theorem~\ref{thm:lifecycle},
below) governs individual organisms,
but its structure applies equally at the population
level.

\textbf{Mass extinction as $\Gamma_{\mathrm{env}} \to 1$.}
A catastrophic environmental change
(asteroid impact, supervolcanic eruption, ocean
anoxia) is a global spike in
$\gamma\,\Gamma_{\mathrm{env}}(t)$.
When this term overwhelms the C2 correction
$-\eta\,\Sigma\Gamma^2$, the organism's total
mismatch diverges and viability collapses.
The five major mass extinctions correspond to five
episodes where $\gamma\,\Gamma_{\mathrm{env}}$
exceeded $\eta$ for the majority of extant species.

\textbf{Thermodynamic persistence.}
The organisms that persist through each extinction
are those with the smallest
$\Sigma\Gamma^2$ relative to the new environment:
(i)~high $\eta$ (rapid C2 updaters, e.g.\ bacteria
with horizontal gene transfer),
(ii)~low metabolic demand ($\ell < \ell^*$,
small body size),
or (iii)~already low $\Gamma_v$ in the new regime
(deep-sea organisms unaffected by surface
conditions).
This is not biological selection but
\emph{thermodynamic exclusion}: the
$\Gamma$-MRP does not select---it excludes.

\textbf{Post-extinction: a new era, not a replay.}
When $\Gamma_{\mathrm{env}}$ relaxes, the complex
structures of the previous era are
\emph{permanently lost}.
The survivors are thermodynamically simple organisms
---bacteria, cnidarians (jellyfish), small-bodied
species---whose low $K$ and low $\ell$ made them
robust to the spike.
From these survivors, \emph{new} structures develop
under the \emph{new} boundary conditions.
The impedance thresholds (Eq.~\ref{eq:critical-length})
still hold: if a lineage grows to $\ell > \ell^*$,
a pump becomes mandatory; if $K$ increases,
a centralised controller becomes mandatory.
But whether any lineage \emph{actually} reaches
$\ell > \ell^*$ depends on the post-extinction
environment, not on the previous era.
Each extinction births a genuinely new structural
era; what the previous era built is gone.

\textbf{Heart-first ordering: a contemporary fact.}
The statement ``the heart develops first in
embryogenesis'' (Sec.~\ref{sec:embryo}) applies to
\emph{current} organisms that already exist in the
pump epoch ($\ell > \ell^*$).
It is a thermodynamic prerequisite for
\emph{these} organisms, not a universal historical
law.
Jellyfish---survivors of multiple extinctions---have
no heart and no embryonic heart-first constraint,
because they satisfy $\ell < \ell^*$ via
gastrovascular diffusion.
\end{remark}

\section{The Lifecycle Equation}
\label{sec:lifecycle}

\subsection{Total mismatch energy}

Define the population-averaged squared reflection coefficient:
\begin{equation}
  \Sigma\Gamma^2(t) \equiv
    \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \Gamma_i^2(t)\,,
  \label{eq:sigma-gamma}
\end{equation}
with dual-network decomposition
$\Sigma\Gamma^2 = \Sigma\Gamma_n^2 + w_v\,\Sigma\Gamma_v^2$,
where $w_v = \alpha_{v\to n}$ is the vascular-to-neural
coupling weight from Paper~2.

\subsection{Three forces}

Three physically distinct mechanisms alter $\Sigma\Gamma^2$:

\emph{(i)~Learning (MRP descent).}
C2 coefficient update adapts $Z_{\mathrm{load}}$ toward
$Z_{\mathrm{source}}$:
\begin{equation}
  \left.\frac{d(\Sigma\Gamma^2)}{dt}\right|_{\mathrm{learn}}
    = -\eta(t)\,\Sigma\Gamma^2\,.
  \label{eq:learning-term}
\end{equation}

\emph{(ii)~Novelty injection.}
New stimuli introduce mismatches at untuned interfaces:
\begin{equation}
  \left.\frac{d(\Sigma\Gamma^2)}{dt}\right|_{\mathrm{novel}}
    = \gamma(t)\,\Gamma_{\mathrm{env}}(t)\,.
  \label{eq:novelty-term}
\end{equation}

\emph{(iii)~Aging (Coffin--Manson fatigue).}
Irreversible mechanical strain from Lorentz pinch
($\varepsilon_{\mathrm{pinch}} = S|I|^\alpha$)
drifts impedance permanently, with stress-accelerated
kinetics:
\begin{equation}
  \delta_{\mathrm{eff}}(t) = \delta_0\,
    \exp\!\bigl(E_a\,\sigma_{\mathrm{stress}}(t)\bigr)\,.
  \label{eq:arrhenius-aging}
\end{equation}

Combining all three, with the coefficients now
explicitly coupled to the vascular network
established in Paper~2:

\begin{theorem}[Lifecycle Equation --- dual-network form]
\label{thm:lifecycle}
The population-averaged squared reflection coefficient
$\Sigma\Gamma^2(t)$ satisfies the non-autonomous ODE
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  \frac{d(\Sigma\Gamma^2)}{dt}
    = -\eta_{\text{eff}}(t)\,\Sigma\Gamma^2
    + \gamma\,\Gamma_{\mathrm{env}}(t)
    + \delta_{\text{eff}}(t)\,D(t)
  \;}
  \label{eq:lifecycle}
\end{equation}
where the effective learning rate and aging rate
are determined by the vascular network state:
\begin{align}
  \eta_{\text{eff}}(t) &= \eta_0 \;
    f_{\text{dev}}(t) \;
    \bigl(1 - \Gamma^2_{\text{vessel}}(t)\bigr)\,,
    \label{eq:eta-eff} \\[2pt]
  \delta_{\text{eff}}(t) &=
    \frac{\delta_0\,
      \exp\!\bigl(E_a\,\sigma_{\text{stress}}(t)\bigr)}
         {C_w(t)}\,.
    \label{eq:delta-eff}
\end{align}
Here:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $f_{\text{dev}}(t)$: bell-shaped developmental
    envelope (Sec.~\ref{sec:eta-lifecycle});
  \item $(1 - \Gamma^2_{\text{vessel}})$: the
    neurovascular coupling factor
    (Paper~2, Eq.~\ref*{eq:Pin-vessel})---vascular
    health sets the ceiling on neural C2;
  \item $C_w(t)$: arterial Windkessel compliance
    (Paper~2)---its decline amplifies the aging term;
  \item $\sigma_{\text{stress}}$: cumulative allostatic
    stress.
\end{itemize}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The three terms follow from:
(i)~C2 gradient descent on
$\mathcal{A}[\Gamma]$ (Eq.~\ref{eq:learning-term}),
with $\eta$ replaced by $\eta_{\text{eff}}$ because
C2 update requires material substrate delivered by
the vascular network at rate
$P_{\text{in}} = P_{\text{basal}}\,(1 - \Gamma_v^2)$
(Paper~2);
(ii)~new stimuli introducing mismatches at untuned
interfaces (Eq.~\ref{eq:novelty-term});
(iii)~Arrhenius-driven irreversible impedance drift
(Eq.~\ref{eq:arrhenius-aging}), with $\delta$
amplified by $1/C_w$ because reduced arterial
compliance (Paper~2) increases pulse stress per
cardiac cycle.
Superposition gives the full ODE.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Positive feedback and the acceleration
  of aging]
\label{rem:aging-feedback}
As the organism ages, two simultaneous changes create
a positive feedback loop:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\Gamma^2_{\text{vessel}} \uparrow$
    $\;\Rightarrow\;$ $\eta_{\text{eff}} \downarrow$
    (learning capacity declines);
  \item $C_w \downarrow$
    $\;\Rightarrow\;$ $\delta_{\text{eff}} \uparrow$
    (aging accelerates).
\end{enumerate}
The numerator of the learning term shrinks while
the denominator of the aging term shrinks.
This is why aging is not linear but accelerating:
the system enters a regime where reduced vascular
health simultaneously impairs repair and amplifies
damage.  The bathtub curve of $\Sigma\Gamma^2$
(Fig.~\ref{fig:lifecycle}) is not a phenomenological
fit---it emerges from the multiplicative coupling
between P1 and P2.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Bilateral aging and the predicted
  neurodegenerative disease order]
\label{rem:bilateral-aging-order}
The positive feedback of the preceding remark acts
\emph{differentially} across bilateral brain
structures, because each structure has distinct
topological protection.
A ``triple-hit'' simulation
(\texttt{exp\_bilateral\_aging.py}) confirms this:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Hit~1: $\eta$ decay} (dopamine
    depletion).
    Not all structures lose dopamine equally:
    the basal ganglia (substantia nigra) decay
    $1.6\times$ faster than cortex.
  \item \textbf{Hit~2: Asymmetric $Z$ drift.}
    Left and right hemispheres receive
    \emph{different contralateral stimuli},
    so C2 adapts each side to its own demand
    ---this \emph{increases} the
    $\Gamma^2_{L \leftrightarrow R}$ mismatch.
  \item \textbf{Hit~3: Relay degradation}
    (white-matter atrophy).
    Midline commissures (CC, AC, HC) lose

\end{enumerate}
The critical observation: the basal ganglia
have \textbf{no commissure}
(Paper~1, Remark~\ref*{rem:bilateral-topology})
and therefore receive zero C3 relay correction
from birth.
As $\eta$ decays and $Z$ drifts asymmetrically,
$\Gamma^2_{L \leftrightarrow R}$ in the basal
ganglia rises unchecked---crossing the disease
threshold \emph{first} among all structures.

\subsection{Developmental modulation of $\eta_{\text{eff}}(t)$}
\label{sec:eta-lifecycle}

The effective learning rate
$\eta_{\text{eff}} = \eta_0\,f_{\text{dev}}\,
(1 - \Gamma^2_{\text{vessel}})$
(Eq.~\ref{eq:eta-eff}) has three factors:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\eta_0$: the intrinsic biochemical C2 rate;
  \item $f_{\text{dev}}(t)$: a bell-shaped
    developmental envelope (high in infancy, plateau
    in adolescence, monotone decline thereafter)
    reflecting myelination, synaptic density, and
    neurotrophin availability;
  \item $(1 - \Gamma^2_{\text{vessel}})$: the
    vascular supply ceiling (Paper~2,
    neurovascular coupling).
\end{enumerate}
Note that the Arrhenius factor previously included
in $\eta$ is now absorbed into
$\Gamma^2_{\text{vessel}}$: arterial stiffening raises
$\Gamma^2_{\text{vessel}}$ (Windkessel
$C_w \downarrow$), which reduces
$(1 - \Gamma^2_{\text{vessel}})$ and thereby
$\eta_{\text{eff}}$---the same physics, but now
derived from P2 rather than postulated.

The cumulative impedance change for a skill chain $s$
acquired at time $t_s$ is:
\begin{equation}
  \Delta Z_s
    = \int_{t_s}^{\infty}
      \eta(\tau)\,\Gamma_s(\tau)\,
      x_{\mathrm{in}}(\tau)\,x_{\mathrm{out}}(\tau)\;d\tau\,.
  \label{eq:skill-depth}
\end{equation}
Because $\eta(t_s)$ is largest in childhood and because
early-acquired skills accumulate over a longer integration
window, childhood-acquired skills (native language, early
motor sequences) achieve the deepest $\Delta Z_s$ and
lowest $\Gamma_s$.

\begin{proposition}[Skill-decay anti-chronology]
\label{prop:skill-decay}
Under monotone decline of $\eta(t)$ and uniform aging
damage $D(t)$, the order of functional loss inverts the
order of acquisition:
\[
  t_{s_1} < t_{s_2}
  \;\Longrightarrow\;
  t_{\mathrm{fail},s_1} > t_{\mathrm{fail},s_2}\,.
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
A deeper $\Delta Z_s$ corresponds to a lower $\Gamma_s$.
Aging drives $\Gamma_s$ back up at a rate independent
of initial depth.
The skill with the deepest $\Delta Z_s$
(earliest $t_s$, highest $\eta$) requires the most damage
to cross the failure threshold $\Gamma_{\mathrm{crit}}$.
\end{proof}

The latest-acquired skill fails first; the childhood
skill survives longest---consistent with the clinical
observation that dementia erases adult-learned second
languages before the native tongue, and that elderly
pianists who ``forgot'' they could play can still perform
when placed at the keyboard.

\begin{remark}[Three mechanisms of impedance fixation]
\label{rem:fixation-mechanisms}
The $\eta(t)$ lifecycle and C2 dynamics identify three
routes by which a $Z$-pattern becomes functionally
immutable---i.e., migrates into the effective null space
$\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$ (Paper~4, Remark~4):
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Critical-period forging.}
    Patterns repeatedly reinforced during the
    $\eta$-peak window (Eq.~\ref{eq:eta-lifecycle})
    accumulate the deepest $\Delta Z_s$
    (Eq.~\ref{eq:skill-depth}).
    Childhood-imprinted values, fears, and attachment
    styles belong to this class.
  \item \textbf{Impulsive $\Gamma^2$ spike.}
    A single event with extreme mismatch---trauma, acute
    humiliation, overwhelming success---produces a large
    instantaneous
    $\Delta Z = -\eta\,\Gamma\,x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}$
    because both $\Gamma$ and $x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}$
    are momentarily very large.
    The resulting $Z$-shift can exceed what years of
    moderate stimuli would achieve, making it
    correspondingly harder to reverse with small corrective
    signals.
  \item \textbf{Confirmatory basin trapping.}
    If the post-acquisition environment consistently
    reinforces the same $Z$-configuration, C2 keeps
    the trajectory inside the original attractor basin.
    By the time $\eta(t)$ has fallen to near-zero
    (Arrhenius aging), no practicable stimulus can
    escape the basin.
\end{enumerate}
All three routes share a common endpoint: the adult who
``knows the belief is irrational but cannot change it''
has correctly diagnosed that
$\eta\,\Gamma\,x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}\approx 0$
on the relevant sub-network.
This is a \emph{physical} constraint, not a failure of will.
Therapeutic change is possible but requires sustained,
high-fidelity counter-stimuli over long time horizons to
accumulate a measurable $\Delta Z$ despite the diminished
$\eta$---analogous to cold-working a tempered steel:
possible, but far slower and more limited than the original
hot forging.
\end{remark}


\subsection{The Equilibrium Equation}

Impedance mismatch dissipates heat.
Thermal balance gives the steady-state temperature:

  T_{\mathrm{steady}} = T_{\mathrm{env}}
    + \frac{\alpha}{\beta}\,\Sigma\Gamma^2\,.
  \label{eq:equilibrium}
\end{equation}
``Adulthood'' has a physical definition:
the thermal steady state where $d(\Sigma\Gamma^2)/dt \approx 0$
---the point at which the world can no longer burn you.

\subsection{Dopamine: the $-\Delta\Gamma^2$ signal}

\begin{equation}
  \delta_{\mathrm{RPE}} = -\bigl(\Sigma\Gamma^2_{t+1}

Dopamine fires when mismatch decreases \emph{more than
expected}---the impedance network matched faster

\subsection{Cortisol: the slow $\Gamma^2$ integrator}

Stress is the time-averaged mismatch:
\begin{equation}
  \sigma_{\mathrm{stress}}(t) =
    \frac{1}{\tau_s} \int_{t-\tau_s}^{t}
    \Sigma\Gamma^2(t')\,dt'\,,
  \label{eq:cortisol}
\end{equation}
where $\tau_s \sim$ hours--days.
Acute challenges are stimulating; prolonged elevation
activates the cortisol response.

\subsection{The Yerkes--Dodson curve}

Arousal increases both $\eta$ (learning) and $\gamma$
(novelty intake).
The optimum occurs at:
\begin{equation}
  \eta^*\,\Sigma\Gamma^2
    = \gamma^*\,\Gamma_{\mathrm{env}}
  \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
  \text{arousal}^* \propto
    \frac{\Sigma\Gamma^2}{\Gamma_{\mathrm{env}}}\,,
  \label{eq:yerkes-dodson}
\end{equation}
predicting that optimal arousal depends on task difficulty
and current skill level---the Yerkes--Dodson
observation~\cite{YerkesDodson1908}.

\subsection{Curiosity and boredom}

Curiosity is the impedance gradient:
$\mathcal{C} = -\partial(\Sigma\Gamma^2)/\partial(\text{exploration})$,

Boredom arises when $|d\Gamma^2/dt| < \epsilon$ at
non-zero mismatch---a thermodynamic imperative to seek
new gradients for action reduction.

\subsection{Metacognition}

The thinking impedance
$\Gamma_{\mathrm{think}} = \sum_i w_i\Gamma_i / \sum_i w_i$
defines dual-process switching~\cite{Kahneman2011}:
System~1 (fast, automatic) when
$\Gamma_{\mathrm{think}} < \Gamma_{\mathrm{S2}}$;
System~2 (slow, deliberate) when
$\Gamma_{\mathrm{think}} > \Gamma_{\mathrm{S2}}$.

\begin{remark}[Thirst-sensation distortion as sensory-channel
  $\Gamma^2$ drift]
\label{rem:thirst-distortion}
Osmoreceptors in the hypothalamus encode hydration
status via $\Gamma_{\text{osmo}}$.
Chronic metabolic overload (insulin resistance,
hypernatraemia, high visceral adiposity) elevates
vascular $\Gamma_v^2$ and metabolic $\Gamma_m^2$,
which couple into the osmoreceptor channel via
$C_{kj}$:
$|\Gamma_{\text{osmo}}^{\text{eff}}|
= |\Gamma_{\text{osmo}}|
+ \varepsilon\,\Gamma_m^2
+ \varepsilon\,\Gamma_v^2$.
When $\Gamma_{\text{osmo}}^{\text{eff}}$ is
chronically elevated, C2 shifts the receptor
set-point upward: the system recalibrates
``normal'' to a dehydrated baseline.
The body is objectively underhydrated,
but the subjective thirst signal is absent---water
intake ``feels'' insufficient because the delivery
channel ($Z_{\text{vasc}} \to Z_{\text{cell}}$)
itself is high-$\Gamma^2$.
This is not a behavioural choice but a
\emph{sensory-channel impedance drift}, identical
in physics to baroreceptor resetting in chronic
hypertension (Paper~2).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Reward-misalignment trap:
  local $\Gamma^2$ minimum, global maximum]
\label{rem:sugar-trap}
The reward signal is
$R = -\Delta\Gamma^2_{\text{local}}$
(dopamine as negative prediction error).
Sugar ingestion produces a rapid, large
$R > 0$ via transient blood-glucose normalisation,
while plain water yields a smaller, slower $R$
distributed across many channels.

C2 strengthens the pathway with the steepest
$R$-gradient:
$\Delta Z_{\text{sugar}} = -\eta\,\Gamma\,
x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}} < 0$,
making the sugar pathway progressively
lower-impedance (preferred).
Water---whose $x_{\text{out}}$ produces
smaller instantaneous $R$---is not reinforced,
and its pathway drifts to higher impedance.

The trap closes when global metabolic
$\Gamma^2_m$ rises from chronic sugar load,
requiring ever-larger sugar boluses:
$\text{sugar}\uparrow \to
\Gamma^2_m\!\uparrow \to
R_{\text{next}}\!\downarrow \to
\text{dose}\uparrow \to \cdots$

The neural system has found a \emph{local}
impedance minimum that is a \emph{global}
maximum.
Resolution requires \emph{external} gradient
injection (fasting, exercise, social support)
to break the closed loop---identical in physics
to the autonomous degradation loop
(Paper~4).
\end{remark}

\section{The Bathtub Curve of Cognition}

The Lifecycle Equation predicts six developmental phases:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Birth}: $\Sigma\Gamma^2 \approx 0.8$--$1.0$.
    Random initialisation; Novelty $\gg$ Learning $\gg$ Aging.
  \item \textbf{Infancy}: Learning dominates.
    $\eta$ maximal (critical period); $\Sigma\Gamma^2$ drops rapidly.
  \item \textbf{Childhood}: Learning $\approx$ Novelty.

  \item \textbf{Adulthood}: $d(\Sigma\Gamma^2)/dt \approx 0$.

\end{enumerate}

reliability \emph{bathtub curve}~\cite{Klutke2003}---not
analogy but derivation from the same physics.

%  SECTION 10 — STRESS, FATIGUE, AND AGING
% ============================================================
\section{Stress, Fatigue, and the Physics of Aging}
\label{sec:aging}

\subsection{Coffin--Manson fatigue}

Action potentials generate Lorentz forces; cumulative
strain follows the Coffin--Manson law~\cite{Coffin1954}:
$N_f = C\,(\Delta\varepsilon_p)^{-\beta}$.
``Failure'' is irreversible impedance drift.

\subsection{Vascular Coffin--Manson: atherosclerosis}

$\sim$3 billion cardiac cycles impose pulsatile strain.
Cumulative vascular damage $D_v(t)$ drifts $Z_v$ from
Murray's-Law optimum (Paper~2), with plaque as the
macroscopic manifestation:
a 50\% stenosis raises $Z_v$ by $16\times$ via
$r^{-4}$ Poiseuille scaling.

\subsection{The vascular--neural aging cascade}

\begin{equation}
  D_v\!\uparrow \;\to\;
    \Gamma_v\!\uparrow \;\to\;
    \rho\!\downarrow \;\to\;
    \eta_{n,\mathrm{eff}}\!\downarrow \;\to\;
    \Gamma_n\!\uparrow\,.
  \label{eq:aging-cascade}
\end{equation}
This explains:
(i)~cardiovascular disease precedes dementia by 10--20 years,
(ii)~blood-pressure control in midlife reduces dementia risk
(SPRINT-MIND~\cite{sprint2019}),
(iii)~metabolic syndrome as dual-network failure.

\subsection{Elastic versus plastic deformation}

Each current pulse produces $\varepsilon = \varepsilon_{\mathrm{elastic}}
+ \varepsilon_{\mathrm{plastic}}$.
Sleep repairs the elastic component (daily $D_Z$ discharge);
the plastic component is \emph{irreversible}---it accumulates
over a lifetime.
Sleep restores daily performance but cannot prevent
long-term aging~\cite{Walker2017}.

\subsection{Death as positive-feedback collapse}

When $\Sigma\Gamma^2$ exceeds a critical threshold:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{d^2(\Sigma\Gamma^2)}{dt^2} > 0
  \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
  \text{runaway failure}\,.
  \label{eq:runaway}
\end{equation}
Compensatory current increases through remaining channels
accelerate fatigue in a positive-feedback loop,
amplified by the dual-network cascade.

\subsection{Sealed shutdown: the alternative endpoint}
\label{sec:sealed-death}

Runaway collapse (Eq.~\ref{eq:runaway}) is not the only
terminal trajectory.
If, by the time life-sustaining channels approach failure,
most of the individual's functional impedance has already
migrated into the effective null space
$\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}(t)$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:eta-lifecycle},
Paper~4 Remark~4), then:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the \emph{fraction} of the network that can
    participate in a positive-feedback cascade is small;
  \item the remaining ``live'' channels carry low current
    at low $\Gamma$, so the Coffin--Manson fatigue rate
    is minimal;
  \item shutdown proceeds as a gentle, low-$\Gamma^2$
    fade rather than a high-$\Gamma^2$ explosion.
\end{enumerate}
We define the \emph{sealing ratio}:
\begin{equation}
  R_{\text{seal}}(t) \equiv
    \frac{\dim\,\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}(t)}
         {\dim\,Z_{\text{total}}}
  \;\in [0,1]\,.
  \label{eq:seal-ratio}
\end{equation}
A high $R_{\text{seal}}$ at end-of-life predicts a low
peak $\Sigma\Gamma^2$ during the dying process
(``peaceful death''), whereas a low $R_{\text{seal}}$
leaves many critical channels in the vulnerable
high-$\Gamma$, low-$\eta$ regime where positive feedback
can ignite.

Clinically: individuals with deeply established life
skills, stable temperament, and long sleep-health history
(all proxies for high $R_{\text{seal}}$) should, on
average, experience less terminal agitation and lower
end-of-life pain burden than individuals with extensive
late-acquired, poorly consolidated functional demands.

\section{Sleep Across the Lifecycle}
\label{sec:sleep-lifecycle}

\emph{Infant sleep:}
$\Sigma\Gamma^2 \approx 0.8$; daily $D_Z$ is enormous.
14--17 hours of sleep required.
REM $\sim$50\% because $N_{\mathrm{topo}}$ is rapidly growing.

\emph{Adult sleep:}
$\Sigma\Gamma^2$ near minimum; 7--9 hours suffice.

\emph{Elderly sleep:}
Irreversible (plastic) drift is undischargeable by sleep.
Rising vascular $\Gamma_v$ (atherosclerosis) reduces
material supply $\rho$, slowing neural repair.
Sleep is doubly impaired: plastic baseline rises
\emph{and} vascular-mediated repair
declines~\cite{Mander2017}.

% ============================================================
\section{Developmental Design Principles}
\label{sec:developmental}

\subsection{Infant soft tissue as impedance buffer}

Neonatal soft tissues (cartilage, fat, elastic skin) form
\emph{mechanical quarter-wave transformers}:
$Z_{\text{infant}} \ll Z_{\text{adult}}$,
attenuating impact signals before they reach nociceptors.
With $\Sigma\Gamma^2 \approx 0.8$ at birth,
unattenuated pain would overwhelm C2 calibration.

\subsection{Social referencing as external $\Gamma$ calibration}

When an infant falls, it looks at the caregiver's face
before deciding to cry~\cite{Sorce1985}.
The caregiver's emotional signal provides
$\Gamma_{\mathrm{social}}$ superimposed on the
mechanical signal:
$\Gamma_{\mathrm{perceived}} =
\Gamma_{\mathrm{mechanical}} + \eta_{\mathrm{social}}\,
\Gamma_{\mathrm{social}}$,
where $\eta_{\mathrm{social}} \gg 1$ in infancy
and $\to 0$ in adulthood (self-calibrated system).
Each fall-and-look episode is a C2 calibration step:
$\Delta Z_{\text{pain}} = -\eta\,\Gamma_{\text{social}}\,
x_{\mathrm{in}}\,x_{\mathrm{out}}$.

Both strategies---structural buffering and behavioural
calibration---are temporary scaffolding: bones harden
and social coupling decreases as the neural network
completes self-calibration.


\subsection{Aging pain immunity}
\label{sec:aging-pain}

If impedance drifts upward with age, why no chronic pain?
For slow drift with healthy C2 ($\eta > 0$),
the coefficient update continuously re-matches:
$\Gamma^2 \sim (\Delta Z / 2\bar{Z})^2 \lesssim 10^{-6}$.
Pain triggers only in three regimes:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Acute injury} ($dZ/dt \gg \eta$):
    instantaneous discontinuity.
  \item \textbf{C2 failure} ($\eta \to 0$):
    avascular tissues (cartilage) cannot remodel
    $\to$ osteoarthritis (Paper~5~\cite{Paper5}).
  \item \textbf{Late senescence}
    ($\delta D(t) > \eta\Sigma\Gamma^2$):
    Coffin--Manson damage exceeds repair capacity.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Scar Tissue as Permanent Impedance Debt}
\label{sec:scar-debt}

A wound creates acute localised impedance debt:
$D_{Z,\text{wound}} = \int_0^{T_{\text{repair}}}
\Gamma_{\text{wound}}^2\,P_{\text{in}}\,dt$.
Repair progressively reduces $|\Gamma_{\text{wound}}|$,
but scar tissue never achieves $\Gamma = 0$:
the residual mismatch $\Gamma_{\text{scar}}^2 > 0$
is a \emph{permanent, non-dischargeable impedance debt}
analogous to the irreducible $A_{\text{cut}}$ of Paper~1.

Clinical correlates: scar tenderness (persistent $\Gamma^2$),
contracture (ongoing minimisation), keloid formation
(overshoot to opposite-sign mismatch),
and cumulative surgical debt (raised $\Sigma\Gamma^2$ floor).

\begin{remark}[Scar debt and the bathtub curve]
An individual accumulating many scars shifts baseline
$\Sigma\Gamma^2$ upward, raising the bathtub-curve floor
---a mechanistic link between cumulative trauma and
accelerated aging.
\end{remark}

% ============================================================
\section{PTSD and Empathic Load as Impedance Physics}
\label{sec:ptsd}

\subsection{PTSD: anatomy of an undamped spike}
\label{sec:ptsd-anatomy}

The prediction ``PTSD = undamped $\Gamma^2$ spike''
can now be decomposed
into three necessary and jointly sufficient conditions
using the $\eta(t)$ lifecycle (Eq.~\ref{eq:eta-lifecycle})
and the fixation mechanisms (Remark~\ref{rem:fixation-mechanisms}).

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Impulsive mismatch.}
    The traumatic event generates a Dirac-like $\Gamma^2$
    spike---a sensory/emotional input that is ``completely
    beyond expectation.''
    The instantaneous C2 update
    $\Delta Z = -\eta\,\Gamma\,x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}$
    is very large because both $\Gamma$ and
    $x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}$ are momentarily extreme.
  \item \textbf{High effective $\eta$ at time of event.}
    If the event occurs during a developmental $\eta$-peak
    (childhood abuse) or during acute hyper-arousal
    (combat, sudden catastrophe), the effective learning
    rate is elevated, deepening the $\Delta Z_s$ well
    beyond what normal experience would produce.
    This is forced tempering: the impedance pattern is
    forged in a single stroke.
  \item \textbf{Absent C2 resolution path.}
    In the aftermath, no subsequent experience provides
    a sustained $(x_{\text{in}},\,x_{\text{out}})$
    trajectory that could guide $\Gamma^2$ back down.
    The traumatic $Z$-pattern sits at a local minimum
    of $\mathcal{A}[\Gamma]$ surrounded by a high-energy
    basin wall; small perturbations return the system
    to the same attractor.
    As $\eta(t)$ continues to decline with age, the
    pattern slides toward
    $\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$---simultaneously
    high-$\Gamma^2$ and low-plasticity.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
The two hallmark symptoms follow directly:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \emph{Re-experiencing / flashback}:
    the high-$\Gamma^2$ attractor has a wide basin of
    attraction; any stimulus near the basin boundary
    pulls the network into the full traumatic
    $Z$-configuration.
  \item \emph{``I know it's irrational but I can't stop it''}:
    $\eta\,\Gamma\,x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}} \approx 0$
    on the traumatic sub-network---the same physics as
    any deep fixation
    (Remark~\ref{rem:fixation-mechanisms}), but here
    the attractor carries pathologically high $\Gamma^2$
    rather than the low $\Gamma^2$ of a well-matched skill.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Therapeutic physics}
\label{sec:ptsd-therapy}

If PTSD is a high-$\Gamma^2$ pattern trapped in a deep
basin with declining $\eta$, effective therapy must
manipulate the three factors in C2:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Pharmacotherapy} (SSRIs, propranolol,
    MDMA-assisted therapy):
    modulates the effective $\eta$ and noise floor,
    lowering the instantaneous $\Gamma^2 \times P_{\text{in}}$
    at each re-activation so that the system does not
    re-traumatise (i.e., does not deposit additional
    $D_Z$ on each trigger).
    In impedance language: drugs widen the basin boundary
    and reduce the peak reflection per activation.
  \item \textbf{Exposure therapy / trauma-focused CBT}:
    provides repeated, safe, titrated
    $(x_{\text{in}},\,x_{\text{out}})$ pairs that traverse
    the basin interior under controlled conditions,
    allowing many small, convergent C2 updates to
    gradually reshape the local $Z$-landscape.
    Each session contributes a small $\Delta Z$ in the
    corrective direction; over months, the accumulated
    integral (Eq.~\ref{eq:skill-depth} in reverse)
    can lower the attractor's $\Gamma^2$ floor.
  \item \textbf{Combined treatment}:
    pharmacotherapy adjusts parameters ($\eta$, noise,
    activation threshold) to keep the system in
    the convergent C2 regime;
    psychotherapy provides the gradient.
    Neither alone is sufficient: drugs without new
    trajectories cannot reshape $Z$; new trajectories
    without parameter control risk re-traumatisation.
\end{itemize}

\noindent
This is not ``suppressing the memory'' but physically
reshaping the impedance sub-network---cold-working a
tempered structure, slow and bounded, but real.

\subsection{Empathic load and caregiver burnout}
\label{sec:empathic-load}

Empathy, in $\Gamma$-Net terms, is the temporary
imposition of another agent's high-$\Gamma^2$ field
onto one's own impedance network.
When a clinician or caregiver engages with a trauma
survivor, their own network must process the incoming
$\Gamma^2$ pattern:
\begin{equation}
  D_{Z,\text{care}}(t)
    = \int_0^t \Gamma_{\text{other}}^2(\tau)\;
      P_{\text{in,care}}(\tau)\,d\tau\,,
  \label{eq:caregiver-debt}
\end{equation}
where $\Gamma_{\text{other}}^2$ is the patient's
transmitted mismatch and $P_{\text{in,care}}$ is
the caregiver's coupling strength (empathic sensitivity
$\propto \eta_{\text{social}}$).

Chronic exposure without adequate discharge
(sleep, boundaries, peer support) raises the caregiver's
own $\Sigma\Gamma^2$ time average
(Eq.~\ref{eq:cortisol}), activating the same cortisol--
allostatic load pathway as any chronic stressor.
The lifecycle equation for the caregiver becomes:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{d(\Sigma\Gamma^2_{\text{care}})}{dt}
    = -\eta\,\Sigma\Gamma^2_{\text{care}}
      + \gamma\,\Gamma_{\text{env}}
      + \delta\,D(t)
      + \underbrace{\lambda\,\Gamma_{\text{other}}^2
        }_{\text{empathic load}}\,,
  \label{eq:caregiver-lifecycle}
\end{equation}
where $\lambda$ parameterises coupling strength.
High-$\eta$ individuals (high empathic sensitivity)
absorb more of the patient's $\Gamma^2$ per unit
time---precisely the trait that makes them effective
caregivers also makes them vulnerable to burnout.

\begin{remark}[Burnout is not weakness]
\label{rem:burnout}
Caregiver burnout---emotional exhaustion, depersonalisation,
reduced efficacy---is the predictable endpoint of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:caregiver-lifecycle} when the empathic-load
term chronically exceeds the combined learning and
sleep-discharge capacity.
It is an energy-balance constraint, not a character
deficiency.
Institutional policies that ignore $D_{Z,\text{care}}$
are, in impedance terms, running a network past its
Coffin--Manson fatigue limit.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Moral blame as $\Gamma^2$-injection]
\label{rem:moral-load}
The preceding analysis reveals a sharp criterion for
evaluating social responses to fixation, trauma, and
addiction.
Any intervention---moral, institutional, or
interpersonal---that addresses an individual's
high-$\Gamma^2$ pattern can be classified by its
effect on the C2 landscape:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Therapeutic}:
    provides safe $(x_{\text{in}},\,x_{\text{out}})$
    trajectories, raises effective $\eta$
    (pharmacologically or via trust), or lowers
    $\Gamma_{\text{env}}$ \;$\Rightarrow$\;
    $\Sigma\Gamma^2$ decreases over time.
  \item \textbf{Iatrogenic}:
    injects additional mismatch (shame, stigma,
    social exclusion) without supplying any
    corrective gradient
    \;$\Rightarrow$\;
    $\Sigma\Gamma^2$ increases;
    the impedance debt deepens.
\end{itemize}
Much of everyday ``moralising'' about trauma responses
and stubborn habits falls into the second category:
it adds $\Gamma_{\text{blame}}^2$ on top of the
original $\Gamma_{\text{fixation}}^2$, deposits
additional $D_Z$, and offers no C2 path for repair.
In the impedance framework, such blame is
physically indistinguishable from secondary injury
(Paper~4, Remark~4).
\end{remark}


\subsection{From psychological load to somatic collapse}
\label{sec:somatic-cascade}

The empathic-load equation
(Eq.~\ref{eq:caregiver-lifecycle}) feeds directly into
the dual-network organ model of
Paper~2: $H = (1-\Gamma_n^2)(1-\Gamma_v^2)$.
The causal chain is:
\begin{equation}
  \underbrace{\Sigma\Gamma^2_{\text{care}}\!\uparrow
    }_{\text{chronic stress}}
  \;\xrightarrow{\text{cortisol, Eq.~(\ref{eq:cortisol})}}
  \;\Gamma_n\!\uparrow
  \;\xrightarrow{\text{autonomic}\downarrow}
  \;\Gamma_v\!\uparrow
  \;\xrightarrow{\rho\downarrow}
  \;\eta_{\text{eff}}\!\downarrow
  \;\Longrightarrow
  \;H\!\downarrow\,.
  \label{eq:psych-somatic}
\end{equation}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Neural overload.}
    Chronic high $\Sigma\Gamma^2$ elevates cortisol
    (Eq.~\ref{eq:cortisol}), accelerating
    Coffin--Manson fatigue on neural channels and
    raising $\Gamma_n$.
  \item \textbf{Autonomic spill-over.}
    Rising $\Gamma_n$ impairs sympathetic--parasympathetic
    balance, drifting the brainstem setpoint
    $Z_{\text{setpoint}}$ upward
    (allostatic load; Paper~5).
    This increases vascular $\Gamma_v$ across
    all downstream organs (Paper~2,
    Eq.~(\ref{eq:aging-cascade})).
  \item \textbf{Material starvation.}
    Higher $\Gamma_v$ reduces perfusion $\rho$,
    starving C2 repair in every tissue---including
    immune (affinity maturation) and metabolic
    (insulin, hepatic, erythropoietic) networks
    that Paper~5 identifies as C2 subsystems.
  \item \textbf{Peripheral collapse.}
    Organs lowest in the perfusion hierarchy (skin, hair,
    nails) are the first to show visible degradation:
    sallow complexion, slow wound healing, brittle
    integument.
    These are not cosmetic complaints; they are the
    earliest macroscopic indicators of a system-wide
    $H\!\downarrow$.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
The complete chain reads:
\[
  \text{PTSD / empathic load}
  \;\to\; \Sigma\Gamma^2\!\uparrow
  \;\to\; \text{Cortisol / allostatic load}
  \;\to\;(\Gamma_n,\Gamma_v)\!\uparrow
  \;\to\; \text{Immune + metabolic C2 failure}
  \;\to\; \text{Multi-organ disease.}
\]
Chronic high-$\Gamma^2$ caregiving and unresolved trauma
do not stay ``psychological'': they accelerate
dual-network aging, push immune and metabolic C2 past
their repair capacity, and manifest as the familiar
clinical picture of lowered immunity, metabolic syndrome,
and accelerated somatic aging.

\begin{remark}[Visual health perception as impedance tomography]
\label{rem:visual-tomography}
The everyday judgment ``this person looks exhausted''
is, in $\Gamma$-Net terms, an unconscious
\emph{impedance tomography}.
The observer's visual system integrates multiple
peripheral indicators---skin perfusion (microvascular
$\Gamma_v$), muscle tone (neuromuscular $\Gamma_n$),
facial micro-expression latency (cortical $\Gamma_n$),
wound-healing state (collagen C2 output)---and compresses
them into a single scalar estimate of the subject's
aggregate $H = (1-\Gamma_n^2)(1-\Gamma_v^2)$.
``Looking well'' corresponds to low $\Gamma$ across
visible channels; ``looking haggard'' corresponds to
elevated $\Gamma$ read out through the perfusion
hierarchy's lowest-priority organs.
The intuition is not metaphor: the observer is literally
reading reflection coefficients from the subject's
skin, posture, and responsiveness, and arriving at a
population-calibrated prior on their dual-network health.
\end{remark}


\subsection{Chronic stress as slow-forged PTSD:
  integral-path equivalence}
\label{sec:chronic-stress-ptsd}

The three conditions for PTSD identified in
Sec.~\ref{sec:ptsd-anatomy}---(1)~impulsive mismatch,
(2)~high $\eta$ at the moment of trauma,
(3)~absent C2 resolution---define a sufficient but
not unique route to structural $Z_s$ fixation.
A second route reaches the same attractor through
temporal integration rather than instantaneous shock.
Recall the impedance debt
(Eq.~\ref{eq:DZ}):
\begin{equation}
  D_Z = \int_0^T \Gamma^2(t)\,P_{\text{in}}(t)\,dt
  \;> D_Z^*
  \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
  Z_s \;\text{locked.}
  \label{eq:chronic-dz}
\end{equation}
The integral is indifferent to the shape of its
integrand: a Dirac-like spike and a months-long
plateau yield identical $D_Z$ if their areas match.
When $\Gamma^2(t)$ is chronically elevated at
sub-traumatic amplitude---interview anxiety,
occupational uncertainty, prolonged social
adversity---the integral crosses the irreversibility
threshold $D_Z^*$ on a timescale of months.
The accumulated C2 update is
\begin{equation}
  \Delta Z_s
    = \int_0^{T_{\text{chronic}}}
      \eta(\tau)\,\Gamma(\tau)\,
      x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}\,d\tau\,,
  \label{eq:chronic-dz-update}
\end{equation}
bypassing conditions (1) and (2) while satisfying
(3): there is no corrective trajectory because the
stressor is \emph{ambient and unresolvable}
(e.g.\ awaiting a hiring decision with no feedback,
sustaining living costs with no income certainty).
In this regime $Z_{\text{load}}$ is perpetually
undefined---the brain cannot compute a gradient
because the target impedance fluctuates between
mutually exclusive outcomes---and C2 stalls while
$D_Z$ continues to grow.

The terminal state is identical to classic PTSD:
$Z_s$ locked in a high-$\Gamma^2$ attractor with
$\eta\,|\,\Gamma\,x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}|\approx 0$
on the affected sub-network.
The downstream somatic cascade
(Eq.~\ref{eq:psych-somatic}) proceeds unchanged:
\[
  \Sigma\Gamma^2\!\uparrow
  \;\to\; \text{Cortisol}\!\uparrow
  \;\to\; (\Gamma_n,\Gamma_v)\!\uparrow
  \;\to\; \text{Immune C2 failure}
  \;\to\; \text{Multi-organ disease.}
\]

\noindent
Two further consequences merit emphasis:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Hallucination from thermal noise.}
    As $\Gamma_i \to 1$ on sensory channels,
    $T_i \to 0$ (C1).
    Johnson--Nyquist noise
    ($P_\theta = 4k_BT\,R_{\text{eff}}\,\Delta f$)
    that would normally transmit harmlessly is instead
    reflected back into the processing loop and
    amplified---the brain interprets its own thermal
    noise as external signal.
    This is the same physics as the
    flashback mechanism (Sec.~\ref{sec:ptsd-anatomy}),
    but triggered by noise rather than a stimulus.
    Paper~4~\cite{Paper4} unifies this with
    sensory-deprivation hallucination: removing

  \quad \forall\, i \in \mathcal{T}

where $\Gamma_i = (Z_{\text{load},i} - Z_{\text{source},i})

\textbf{Step 1: Existence of $\varphi_i$.}

effort variable to a flow variable

and the update $\Delta Q_i$ maps to $\Delta Z_i$ via
a sign-preserving linear relation
($\Delta Z \propto -\Delta w$ for synapses;
$\Delta Z \propto +\Delta\rho$ for bone; etc.).
The mapping $\varphi_i$ is therefore constructible
explicitly for every row of Table~\ref{tab:twentynine};

Section~\ref{sec:tissue-proofs}.

\textbf{Step 2: Algebraic equivalence.}
Under $\varphi_i$, each $\mathcal{L}_i$ reduces to

\section{Central Controllers}
\label{sec:controllers}

The preceding tissue networks describe \emph{distributed}
C2---local impedance matching along branches.
Two systems operate at a higher hierarchical level,
setting $Z_{\text{source}}$ or $Z_{\text{setpoint}}$
for entire network trees.

\subsection{Cardiac pump: Laplace--Starling remodeling}

The heart is the voltage source whose output impedance
determines the pressure waveform for the entire arterial
tree.  C2 mapping:
$\Delta Z_{\text{heart}} = -\eta\,\Gamma_{\text{aortic}}
\,\sigma\,\varepsilon$,
where $\sigma$ is wall stress (Laplace:
$\sigma = Pr/2h$) and $\varepsilon$ is myocardial strain.

Two hypertrophy modes:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Concentric} (pressure overload,
    $\Gamma > 0$): wall thickness $h$ increases,
    $Z_{\text{heart}}$ rises toward $Z_{\text{aorta}}$.
  \item \textbf{Eccentric} (volume overload):
    chamber dilates, reducing mismatch from a
    different direction.
\end{itemize}
Heart failure is $\Gamma_{\text{aortic}} \to 1$:
ventricular impedance can no longer match the
arterial tree.  Controller failure propagates to
all downstream vascular branches.

\subsection{Regulatory brain: baroreceptor resetting and
allostatic load}

The brainstem sets $Z_{\text{setpoint}}$ for the
entire autonomic tree via the baroreflex arc.
C2 mapping:
$\Delta Z_{\text{setpoint}} = -\eta\,\Gamma_{\text{reg}}
\,(\text{sensory input})\,(\text{autonomic output})$.

Baroreceptor resetting in chronic
hypertension~\cite{Chapleau1991}:
the brainstem adapts its pressure setpoint upward
($Z_{\text{setpoint}} \uparrow$), reducing local
$\Gamma_{\text{reg}}$ but creating downstream mismatch
in every organ.

Allostatic load~\cite{McEwen1998},
mathematically the \emph{cumulative exogenous
set-point forcing}:
\begin{equation}
  L_{\text{allostatic}} = \int_0^T
  |\Delta Z_{\text{setpoint}}(t)|\,dt\,,
  \label{eq:allostatic}
\end{equation}
i.e.\ the time-integrated drift of the regulatory
controller's impedance set-point.
Chronic stress causes multi-organ disease because
the \emph{controller's} $\Gamma$ error propagates
to all downstream networks.

\begin{remark}[The full chain: trauma $\to$ multi-organ disease]
\label{rem:trauma-somatic-ctrl}
Part~B (Sec.~\ref{sec:empathic-load}) derives a caregiver
lifecycle equation
with an explicit empathic-load term
$+\lambda\,\Gamma_{\text{other}}^2$.
When this term is chronically elevated (unresolved PTSD,
long-term caregiving), the brainstem controller
accumulates allostatic load, drifting
$Z_{\text{setpoint}}$ upward and raising
$\Gamma_v$ across every downstream organ.
Because immune memory and metabolic homeostasis are
themselves C2 networks
(Table~\ref{tab:twentynine}),
the resulting $\rho\!\downarrow$ starves their
repair loops:
immune affinity maturation stalls
($\eta_{\text{immune}}\!\downarrow$),
insulin and hepatic regulation lose their corrective
gradient, and erythropoiesis underperforms.
The outward signs---lowered immunity, metabolic syndrome,
accelerated skin and hair aging---are not metaphors for
``stress''; they are quantitative consequences of
$H = (1-\Gamma_n^2)(1-\Gamma_v^2)\!\downarrow$
propagating from the controller through every C2
branch of the network.
\end{remark}

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{Methodological caveat}

All computational experiments (Paper~5) use modules
built from the $\Gamma$-Net axioms.
When the simulators reproduce the theory's predictions,
this confirms code--equation consistency, not empirical
truth.
True validation requires longitudinal impedance measurements
(EEG coherence from birth to senescence), comparative
polysomnography across phyla, and prospective clinical tests.

\subsection{The brain as thermal manager}

The $\Gamma$-framework identifies the brain as a
centralised thermal manager for impedance-debt
dissipation, necessitated by the loss of the oceanic
heat sink.
Cognition, memory, and consciousness exploit
infrastructure whose \emph{physical} raison d'\^etre is
thermodynamic housekeeping~\cite{Torday2016}.
This is consistent with the preoptic area controlling
\emph{both} sleep and thermoregulation:
thermal management is the brain's primary physical
function; higher cognition operates on the same
impedance substrate.

\subsection{$\Gamma^2 \!\leftrightarrow\! T$: the self-consistent loop}
\label{sec:gamma-T-loop}

The relationship between impedance mismatch and body
temperature is not a one-way dependency; it forms a
closed, self-consistent loop.  We now make this explicit.

\paragraph{Step 1: Computation $\to$ heat.}
C1 (Paper~0, Eq.~1) guarantees that every interface
with $\Gamma_i \neq 0$ dissipates $\Gamma_i^2
P_{\mathrm{in},i}$ as heat.  Summed over $N$ active
interfaces:
\begin{equation}
  P_{\mathrm{heat}} = \sum_{i=1}^{N} \Gamma_i^2\, P_{\mathrm{in},i}
  = \langle \Gamma^2 \rangle \cdot P_{\mathrm{total}}\,.
  \label{eq:loop-step1}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Step 2: Heat $\to$ temperature.}
In thermal steady state:
\begin{equation}
  T_{\mathrm{body}} = T_{\mathrm{env}}
  + \frac{P_{\mathrm{heat}}}{h_c A}
  = T_{\mathrm{env}}
  + \frac{\langle \Gamma^2 \rangle \, P_{\mathrm{total}}}{h_c A}\,,
  \label{eq:loop-step2}
\end{equation}
where $h_c A$ is the effective thermal conductance
(primarily cerebral blood flow, Paper~2~\cite{Paper2}).

\paragraph{Step 3: Temperature $\to$ impedance.}
Blood viscosity sets the vascular bus impedance
$Z_{\mathrm{bus}}(T)$ via the Arrhenius relation,
and C2 repair rate scales as
$\tilde{\eta}_{\mathrm{learn}}(T)
= \exp[\beta(T_0^{-1} - T^{-1})]$.
Therefore:
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma_i(T) = \frac{Z_{\mathrm{organ},i} - Z_{\mathrm{bus}}(T)}
  {Z_{\mathrm{organ},i} + Z_{\mathrm{bus}}(T)}\,.
  \label{eq:loop-step3}
\end{equation}

\paragraph{Closure: the self-consistency condition.}
Substituting Eq.~(\ref{eq:loop-step3}) into
Eq.~(\ref{eq:loop-step1}) and Eq.~(\ref{eq:loop-step2})
yields a fixed-point equation:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{T^* = T_{\mathrm{env}}
  + \frac{P_{\mathrm{total}}}{h_c A}
  \sum_{i=1}^{N} \Gamma_i^2(T^*)\,.}
  \label{eq:loop-closure}
\end{equation}
The operating point $T^* \approx 310$\,K (37$\,^\circ$C)
is the unique stable fixed point of this equation
for terrestrial mammals.
It is \emph{not} a set-point chosen by history---it is
the self-consistent solution where impedance-driven
heat production balances convective heat loss.

\paragraph{Stability and failure modes.}
The loop is \emph{stable} because $\partial P_{\mathrm{heat}}/
\partial T < h_c A$ at $T^*$:
a small temperature rise shifts $Z_{\mathrm{bus}}$,
reducing some $\Gamma_i^2$ (better vascular matching at
lower viscosity), which reduces heat production.
The loop \emph{fails} in two directions:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Hypothermia} ($T \ll T^*$):
    viscosity rises $\to$ $\Gamma^2\!\uparrow$ globally
    $\to$ C2 repair slows (Arrhenius)
    $\to$ impedance debt accumulates
    $\to$ positive feedback $\to$ coma.
  \item \textbf{Hyperthermia} ($T \gg T^*$):
    thermal noise $\propto k_B T$ overwhelms
    high-$K$ channels (Paper~4~\cite{Paper4})
    $\to$ $\delta\Gamma/\Gamma \propto K^{3/2}$
    $\to$ PFC collapses first $\to$ delirium.
\end{itemize}
The 37$\,^\circ$C operating point is
the narrow basin of attraction between these two
catastrophes.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have developed the temporal theory of impedance networks
and proved the universality of the C2 update law
in three complementary parts.

\textbf{Part~A:}
Impedance debt $D_Z = \int |\Gamma|^2 P_{\mathrm{in}}\,dt$
accumulates during wakefulness and is discharged by sleep.
Its decomposition into $D_{\mathrm{micro}}$ and
$D_{\mathrm{thermal}}$ is consistent with:
cnidarian sleep without a brain (ocean removes
$D_{\mathrm{thermal}}$);
the physical necessity of centralised neural architecture
on land (loss of passive heat sink);
the tidal zone as the lowest-action impedance transition
($\mathcal{A}[\Gamma]$ reduced 93.8\%);
and embryogenesis as thermodynamic causal chain.
A dual-field PDE unifies four tissue-remodeling laws.

\textbf{Part~B:}
The Lifecycle Equation
(Eq.~\eqref{eq:lifecycle})
reduces the entire cognitive trajectory to three forces:
learning, novelty, aging.
Emotion is impedance-mismatch readout at different time scales.
The bathtub curve emerges without fitted functions.
Aging is Coffin--Manson fatigue with Arrhenius acceleration;
atherosclerosis is vascular fatigue;
death is positive-feedback collapse.
Adulthood has a physical definition via the
Equilibrium Equation~\eqref{eq:equilibrium}.
PTSD, empathic load, and chronic stress are derived as
instances of a single impedance-debt integral with
different time-scale parameters.

\textbf{Part~C:}
The C2 update rule
$\Delta Z = -\eta\,\Gamma\,x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}$
is algebraically equivalent to all twenty-nine known
tissue-remodeling laws
(Table~\ref{tab:twentynine}).
Life is thermodynamically bounded between
ice ($D_Z = 0$, C2 halted) and fire ($Z \to \infty$,
substrate destroyed), with 37\textdegree C as the
self-consistent fixed point
(Eq.~\eqref{eq:loop-closure}).
The cardiac pump and regulatory brain are central
controllers whose impedance errors propagate to every
downstream organ; allostatic load
(Eq.~\eqref{eq:allostatic})
is the time-integrated drift of the controller set-point.
Cartilage ($\eta \approx 0$) predicts
osteoarthritis as the null case of remodeling.

Together with Papers~1--2, this paper completes a
multi-layer description of biological intelligence:
spatial architecture, vascular topology,
daily maintenance, the full arc of a life,
and the universal tissue catalogue
---all from $\mathcal{A}[\Gamma] \to \min$.
Testable predictions and computational verification
are consolidated in Paper~5~\cite{Paper5}.
