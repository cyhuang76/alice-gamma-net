\begin{abstract}
We ask: given a single neuron with $K$ impedance modes
governed by C2 remodeling
($\Delta Z = -\eta\,\Gamma\,x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}$),
what architecture---and what phenomenology---must emerge?

\textbf{Part~A} establishes the foundation.
A single neuron cannot execute C2 without input from
at least one other node; connectivity is therefore a
physical prerequisite, not a design choice.
We prove the \emph{Dimensional Cost Irreducibility
Theorem}: for any network with heterogeneous mode counts
$\{K_i\}$, the total reflection action decomposes as
$A = \Aimp(t) + \Acut$,
where $\Aimp \to 0$ under C2 but
$\Acut = \sum (K_i - K_j)^+$ has identically zero
gradient---no impedance adjustment can reduce it.
Numerical experiments confirm convergence time
$\tau_{\text{conv}} \sim N^{-1.12}$ ($R^2 = 0.93$)
and a soft-cutoff parameter $\gamma$ that tunes the
K-space fractal dimension
$D_K = 0.76\gamma + 0.78$.

\textbf{Part~B} derives the neural architecture chain.
C2's dimensional preference forces same-$K$ neurons
to cluster---thermodynamic phase separation produces
functional regions without a blueprint.
The irreducibility corollary makes intermediate-$K$
relay nodes a thermodynamic necessity.
Convergence of ascending pathways forces the
highest-$K$ cluster to form---this is the brain.
C3 impedance-tagged transport enables the brain to
identify signal origin from $Z_{\text{source}}$ metadata.

\textbf{Part~C} shows that three concepts traditionally
regarded as metaphysical---memory, consciousness, and
soul---have precise operational definitions within
the same formalism.

\emph{Soul} is the null space of impedance remodeling:

=\Acut + Z_0^{\text{DNA}} + \rho_{\text{irred}}^2$.
Four laws of the null space parallel thermodynamics;

\textbf{Part~D} presents experimental evidence and

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
% ============================================================

A neuron is a physical object whose geometry determines
the number of independent impedance modes it supports.
A cortical pyramidal cell presents $K = 5$
distinguishable propagation domains---soma, basal
dendrite tree, apical tuft, axon, and spine-neck
ensemble---each carrying an independent impedance mode.
An unmyelinated C-fibre, a simple cylinder, presents
$K = 1$.
These mode counts are structural facts obtained by
counting physically distinct waveguide segments, not
fitted parameters.

C2 updates the impedance at each mode:
$\Delta Z_k = -\eta\,\Gamma_k\,x_{\text{in},k}\,x_{\text{out},k}$.
For an isolated neuron, $x_{\text{in}} = 0$ on every mode.
Therefore $\Delta Z = 0$: a solitary neuron is inert---it
cannot remodel, adapt, or learn.
C2 demands at least one input, which requires at least one
other node.
\emph{Connectivity is a physical prerequisite of C2, not a
biological design choice.}

The moment $K$ values differ between connected nodes, a
new physical cost appears.
This paper proves that cost is irreducible
(Sec.~\ref{sec:theorem}), derives its architectural
consequences---functional regions
(Sec.~\ref{sec:clustering}), relay hierarchy
(Sec.~\ref{sec:relay}), the brain
(Sec.~\ref{sec:brain}), and signal routing
(Sec.~\ref{sec:signals})---and then shows that three
concepts traditionally regarded as metaphysical---memory
(Sec.~\ref{sec:memory}), consciousness
(Sec.~\ref{sec:consciousness}), and soul
(Sec.~\ref{sec:soul})---are necessary consequences
of the same irreducible topology.

\section{Physical Framework}
\label{sec:framework}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Network definition}

A $\Gamma$-network consists of $N$ nodes, each carrying a
$K_i$-dimensional impedance vector
$\mathbf{Z}_i \in \mathbb{R}^{K_i}_{>0}$.
For an edge $i \to j$, the per-mode reflection coefficient is
\begin{equation}
  \gamma_k = \frac{Z_{j,k} - Z_{i,k}}{Z_{j,k} + Z_{i,k}},
  \quad k = 1, \ldots, \Kcom,
  \label{eq:gamma}
\end{equation}
where $\Kcom = \min(K_i, K_j)$.
Excess source modes $k > \Kcom$ are totally reflected:
$\gamma_k = 1$.
This is the electromagnetic boundary condition for a
waveguide whose $k$-th mode has no counterpart in the
target medium.

\subsection{Three physical laws}
\label{sec:laws}

\textbf{C1---Energy Conservation.}
At every edge, every tick, reflected and transmitted power
sum to incident power:
\begin{equation}
  \gamma_k^2 + T_k = 1, \quad \forall\, k,\; \forall\,(i,j).
  \label{eq:C1}
\end{equation}

\textbf{C2---Impedance Remodeling.}
Each node adjusts its impedance to reduce local reflection:
\begin{align}
  \Delta Z_{i,k} &= +\eta\,\gamma_k\,x_{\text{in},k}\,
    x_{\text{out},k},
  \label{eq:C2a} \\
  \Delta Z_{j,k} &= -\eta\,\gamma_k\,x_{\text{in},k}\,
    x_{\text{out},k},
  \label{eq:C2b}
\end{align}
where $x_{\text{in},k}$ is the input activity at node~$i$
and $x_{\text{out},k}$ the output activity at node~$j$
for mode~$k$; $\eta$ is the remodeling rate.

\textbf{C3---Impedance-Tagged Transport.}
Every signal carries its source impedance as metadata,
enabling downstream nodes to compute $\gamma_k$ locally
without global knowledge.

\subsection{Variational principle}
\label{sec:action}

The Minimum Reflection Principle (MRP) states that the
system minimises the total reflection action:
\begin{equation}
  A[\Gamma]
  = \sum_{(i,j) \in \mathcal{E}}
    \sum_{k=1}^{\Kcom(i,j)} \gamma_k^2.
  \label{eq:action}
\end{equation}
The C2 update rule implements steepest descent on the
\emph{learnable} part of~$A$:
$\dot{\mathbf{Z}} \propto -\nabla_{\mathbf{Z}} \Aimp$.

\section{Dimensional Cost Irreducibility Theorem}
\label{sec:theorem}
% ============================================================

\begin{theorem}[Dimensional Cost Irreducibility \textnormal{(E0)}]
\label{thm:irreducibility}
For any $\Gamma$-network with heterogeneous mode counts
$\{K_i\}$, the total reflection action decomposes as
\begin{equation}
  A = \Aimp(t) + \Acut,
  \label{eq:decomposition}
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{enumerate}
\item
$\Aimp(t) = \sum_{(i,j)} \sum_{k=1}^{\Kcom} \gamma_k^2$
is the \emph{impedance mismatch action} over shared modes.
Under C2 dynamics, $\Aimp(t) \to 0$ as $t \to \infty$.

\item
$\Acut = \sum_{(i,j)} (K_i - K_j)^+$
is the \emph{cutoff action} from excess modes.
$\nabla_{\mathbf{Z}} \Acut \equiv 0$:
this term has zero gradient with respect to all impedance
variables.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For an edge $i \to j$ with $K_i > K_j$:
\begin{itemize}
\item
Modes $k \le \Kcom = K_j$:
$\gamma_k$ depends on $(Z_{i,k}, Z_{j,k})$ via
Eq.~\eqref{eq:gamma}.
C2 adjusts both impedances, driving $\gamma_k \to 0$.
These modes contribute to $\Aimp$.

\item
Modes $K_j < k \le K_i$:
the target has no mode~$k$, so $\gamma_k \equiv 1$
identically, independent of any impedance value.
These modes contribute $(K_i - K_j)$ to $\Acut$.
Since $\gamma_k = 1$ is a \emph{topological constant}
(depending only on the existence, not the value, of modes),
$\partial \gamma_k^2 / \partial Z = 0$ for all~$Z$.
\end{itemize}
Summing over all edges completes the proof.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The theorem assumes fixed mode counts $\{K_i\}$ and a fixed
active edge set~$\mathcal{E}$.
If mode counts change dynamically (e.g., dendritic spine
growth increasing $K_j$), or if edge sprouting/pruning
alters $\mathcal{E}$, then the \emph{value} of $\Acut$
may change---but this change is driven by topology, not
by impedance gradients.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Thermodynamic Necessity of Relay Nodes \textnormal{(E0)}]
\label{cor:relay}
A direct edge $K_{\text{src}} \to K_{\text{tgt}}$ with
$K_{\text{src}} > K_{\text{tgt}}$ totally reflects the
$(K_{\text{src}} - K_{\text{tgt}})$ excess modes.
Inserting a relay node with
$K_{\text{tgt}} < \Krelay < K_{\text{src}}$ leaves $\Acut$
unchanged but converts \emph{total signal loss} into
\emph{partial signal recovery}:
modes $K_{\text{tgt}} < k \le \Krelay$, previously
totally reflected, are now transmitted on the first hop
and can undergo C2 matching ($\gamma_k \to 0$,
$T_k \to 1$).
Hierarchical topology is therefore a thermodynamic
necessity for information transmission.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[Directional asymmetry of
  dimensional cost \textnormal{(E0)}]
\label{cor:directional-asymmetry}
For any edge $i \to j$ in the relay hierarchy,
the dimensional cost $\Acut(i \to j) = (K_i - K_j)^+$
satisfies:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \emph{Top-down} ($K_i > K_j$):
    $\Acut = K_i - K_j > 0$; the excess modes are
    totally reflected.
  \item \emph{Bottom-up} ($K_i < K_j$):
    $\Acut = 0$; all modes are transmitted
    losslessly.
\end{enumerate}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By Theorem~\ref{thm:irreducibility},
$\Acut(i \to j) = (K_i - K_j)^+$.
For $K_i > K_j$, the target has
fewer degrees of freedom;
the $K_i - K_j$ excess modes
have no receiving channel and are
totally reflected ($\Gamma = 1$ per mode).
For $K_i \leq K_j$, $(K_i - K_j)^+ = 0$
and every mode has a receiving channel.
$\square$
\end{proof}

\noindent
\textbf{Example.}
Cortex ($K=5$) $\to$ muscle ($K=1$):
$\Acut = 4$ (``knowing is easy, doing is hard'').
Nociceptor ($K=1$) $\to$ cortex ($K=5$):
$\Acut = 0$ (reflexes are fast).
Motor skill acquisition is the process of
C2 remodelling each relay
node, flattening the $K = 5 \to 4 \to 3 \to 1$
staircase until $\Gamma \to 0$ at every stage.
``Analysis paralysis'' occurs when conscious
top-down signals ($K=5$) are injected into an
already-optimised low-$K$ automatic pathway,
reintroducing reflection noise.

\begin{remark}[Relay nodes increase bandwidth \textnormal{(E0)}]
\label{rem:bandwidth}
While Theorem~\ref{thm:irreducibility} proves that $\Acut$ is
invariant under relay insertion,
Corollary~\ref{cor:relay} shows that relays convert
total reflection into partial recovery.
A further consequence, familiar from multi-stage
impedance transformer theory~\cite{Pozar},
is that additional relay stages
\emph{increase the bandwidth}
over which $|\Gamma|$ remains small.

A direct $K{=}5 \to K{=}1$ connection matches at a
single operating point (narrowband);
a four-stage staircase
$K{=}5 \to 4 \to 3 \to 2 \to 1$
achieves the same minimum $\Acut$ but over a
\emph{broader range of input frequencies}.
In neural terms, the richer the relay hierarchy
between cortex and effector,
the wider the repertoire of signals
(force profiles, timing patterns, sensory details)
that can be faithfully transmitted.
This may explain why motor mastery
requires not merely $\Gamma \to 0$ (impedance matching)
but also extensive relay architecture
(myelination, cerebellar microcircuits):
C2 minimises reflection at each stage,
while the multi-stage topology maximises
the bandwidth of skilled performance.
\end{remark}

\section{Same-$K$ Clustering and Functional Regions}
\label{sec:clustering}
% ============================================================

C2 gradient descent on the reflection action $A$
selectively strengthens same-$K$ edges
(more shared modes $\Rightarrow$ faster $\Gamma$ reduction)
and weakens cross-$K$ edges
(excess modes totally reflected, no gradient available).
Under sustained activity plus edge pruning, the surviving
connectivity partitions the network into
\emph{same-$K$ clusters}.

\begin{theorem}[Phase Separation Under MRP \textnormal{(E0)}]
\label{thm:phase-sep}
Any impedance network under MRP with heterogeneous $K$
must phase-separate into same-$K$ domains to minimise
total reflected action.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Define the total cutoff action as
$\Acut = \sum_{(i,j)\in E} |K_i - K_j|$,
where the sum is over all edges~$E$ of the network.
For any edge $(i,j)$ with $K_i = K_j$, the contribution
is zero; for $K_i \neq K_j$, the contribution
$|K_i - K_j| > 0$ is strictly positive and invariant
under C2 (Irreducibility Theorem,
Theorem~\ref{thm:irreducibility}).
The Minimum Reflection Principle requires
$\mathcal{A} \to \min$; since $\Aimp \to 0$ under C2,
the only remaining reduction available is to minimise
$\Acut$ by rewiring cross-$K$ edges to same-$K$ pairs.
Each such rewiring strictly decreases $\Acut$, so the
process is monotone and terminates when all connected
nodes share the same $K$---the same-$K$ domain
(phase-separated) configuration.
C2 provides the dynamical mechanism: cross-$K$ edges
carry permanent reflected modes that yield no C2
gradient, and are pruned under any activity-dependent
update rule with $\eta > 0$.
$\square$
\end{proof}

\section{The Brain as Convergence Region}
\label{sec:brain}

An organism with multiple sensory modalities has
multiple ascending pathways.
By the irreducibility theorem, the convergence region
must satisfy
\begin{equation}
  K_{\text{conv}} \ge \max_j K_j
  \label{eq:K-convergence}

for all input pathways~$j$; otherwise, modes from
higher-$K$ inputs are totally reflected.
The convergence region is therefore forced to be the
highest-$K$ cluster in the network.

This is the \emph{brain}: a topological necessity, not a
design choice.

  \item \textbf{Temperature sensitivity.}
    Higher $K$ means more modes dissipating simultaneously.
  \item \textbf{Matrix computation.}
    C2 gradient descent across all $K_{\text{conv}}$ modes
    simultaneously is the physical operation that
    neuroscience calls ``executive function''
    (Sec.~\ref{sec:consciousness}).
\end{itemize}

\section{Sensory Perception as $\Gamma$ Detection}
\label{sec:perception}
% ============================================================

\begin{remark}[The retina as a spherical
  $\Gamma$ array \textnormal{(E1)}]
\label{rem:retinal-array}
The eye is a spherical impedance interface:
light passes from air ($n_1$) through cornea,
aqueous humour, lens, and vitreous body, each
with distinct refractive index $n_i$---i.e.\
distinct optical impedance $Z_i$.
The reflection coefficient at each interface is
$\Gamma_i = (n_{i+1} - n_i)/(n_{i+1} + n_i)$,
identical in form to the electrical $\Gamma$.

The ${\sim}1.2 \times 10^8$ photoreceptors
tiling the retinal sphere each sample the local
optical $\Gamma$ at their angular position.
Object edges in the visual field correspond to
spatial discontinuities in $\Gamma$---that is,
$\lvert\partial\Gamma/\partial x\rvert \gg 0$---
detected by retinal ganglion cells whose
centre--surround receptive fields are
spatial $d\Gamma/dx$ filters.

Binocular depth perception arises as a spatial
standing wave: the two retinal images produce
counter-propagating signals in V1 whose
phase difference encodes object distance.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Compensatory binding
  \textnormal{(E1)}]
\label{rem:compensatory-binding}
The binding problem (``How does the brain know that
a sound and a visual object share the same source?'')
has resisted solution for decades.
In the impedance framework, the answer requires
no new mechanism:

When the auditory system detects $d\Gamma/dt > 0$
(a sudden sound), motor C2 initiates a
compensatory head turn.
This single motor action simultaneously changes:
(a)~auditory $\Gamma$ (the sound's direction
relative to the ear axis), and
(b)~visual $\Gamma$ (new objects enter the fovea).
If auditory $\Gamma$ and visual $\Gamma$ decrease
together under the same motor compensation,
the two signals share the same physical source.

\emph{Binding is not a computation---it is a
correlation test performed by motor C2.}
This also explains infant sensorimotor development:
neonates constantly turn, grasp, and mouth objects
not to ``learn'' but to build cross-modal $\Gamma$
correspondence tables via C2 compensation.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Attention as $\Gamma_{\max}$
  tracking \textnormal{(E1)}]
\label{rem:gamma-max}
The impedance framework predicts that attention is
not a cognitive ``spotlight'' but a physical reflex:
the motor system continuously orients the
highest-resolution sensor (fovea) toward the
spatial location of $\Gamma_{\max}$---the point
in the perceptual field with the largest
impedance mismatch relative to its surroundings.

$\Gamma_{\max}$ coincides with maximal survival
threat: a predator on a uniform background has
$\Gamma \approx 1$ (maximum contrast), triggering
immediate orientation.
The entire perception--action loop is therefore:
\[
  \text{detect } \Gamma_{\max} \;\to\;
  \text{C2 motor compensation} \;\to\;
  \text{resample} \;\to\;
  \text{new } \Gamma_{\max}\,.
\]
No executive controller is required; the loop
is driven by C1 (energy is reflected most at
the largest mismatch) and C2 (motor correction
reduces $\Gamma$ at the attended location).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Hemispheric lateralisation as
  thermal bandwidth splitting \textnormal{(E0)}]
\label{rem:lateralisation}
When the total sensory flux exceeds the $\Gamma^2$
heat-dissipation capacity of a single convergence
node (C1 thermal limit), the node must split
into parallel paths---exactly as a transmission
line splits into two cables to double bandwidth.

\emph{Why $n=2$ and not $n=3$?}
Consider a convergence node receiving total input
power $P_{\text{in}}$ that must be split into $n$
parallel sub-nodes.
The neural action functional for each branch~$j$ is
\[
  \mathcal{A}_{\text{neural}}
    = \sum_{j=1}^{n}
      \bigl[\,
        \Gamma_j^2\,P_{\text{in},j}
        \;+\;
        \mu\,V_{\text{axon},j}
      \bigr]\,,
\]
where $\Gamma_j^2\,P_{\text{in},j}$ is the
reflection loss (C1 heat) and
$\mu\,V_{\text{axon},j}$ is the metabolic
maintenance cost of the axonal wiring.
This has the same mathematical form---quadratic
loss $+$ linear material cost---as the vascular
Murray functional
$\mathcal{A}_{\text{Murray}} =
\sum Q_j^2 R_j + \lambda V_j$.
Murray's theorem shows that, in three-dimensional
space, minimising any functional of this form over
branching number yields $n^* = 2$ (binary
bifurcation) with the Kleiber $\tfrac{3}{4}$-power
scaling as a corollary.
Because $\mathcal{A}_{\text{neural}}$ is
form-identical to $\mathcal{A}_{\text{Murray}}$,
the same optimality applies:
\emph{the brain splits into exactly two
hemispheres}.

\emph{Why asymmetric roles?}
Under a fixed total power budget
$P_{\text{brain}}$ (set by brainstem blood flow,
a C1 constraint), simultaneously running
high-$K$ multi-modal integration (fan-in) and
wide fan-out multi-node control pushes the
$\Gamma$-landscape to mutually exclusive extremes:
integration demands low $\Gamma$ across few
high-$K$ channels (deep matching), while motor
broadcast demands low $\Gamma$ across many
low-$K$ channels (wide matching).
Minimising the lifetime action
$\mathcal{A} = \int \sum \Gamma^2 P_{\text{in}}\,dt$
under the single power constraint therefore
yields two specialised sub-networks rather
than one uniform processor.

The brain implements this as:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \emph{Left hemisphere (fan-in)}:
    multi-modal $K$-staircase convergence---
    high-$K$, few channels, deep computation
    (language, sequential logic, symbolic
    reasoning).

  \item \emph{Right hemisphere (fan-out)}:
    $\Gamma$-distribution broadcast to many
    output nodes---medium-$K$, many channels,
    spatial-motor control
    (body image, spatial awareness, the
    $\Gamma_{\max}$-tracking attention command
    of Remark~\ref{rem:gamma-max}).

  \item \emph{Corpus callosum}: the C3
    dimensional relay between the two
    hemispheres---a $K$-staircase of
    ${\sim}2 \times 10^8$ axons bridging the
    analysis and command halves.
\end{itemize}

\noindent
\textbf{Clinical prediction (falsifiable):}
Right-parietal lesions destroy the command
pathway for $\Gamma_{\max}$ orientation,
producing \emph{hemispatial neglect}---the
patient's left visual field ceases to exist
even though retinal input is intact.
Left-hemisphere lesions, by contrast, impair
analysis (e.g.\ aphasia) but preserve
attentional orientation.
Both predictions are confirmed by lesion
studies (Mesulam, 1981; Corbetta \& Shulman, 2002).
\end{remark}

\section{Memory as Impedance Dynamics}

\subsection{The C2 impedance-remodeling gradient}

Constraint~C2 defines the only mechanism by which the
network modifies itself:
\begin{equation}
  \Delta Z_k = -\eta\,\Gamma_k\,x_{\text{in},k}\,x_{\text{out},k}\,.
  \label{eq:C2-update}
\end{equation}
This is not an analogy---it \emph{is} memory.
The trace of all past updates constitutes the network's
memory:
\begin{equation}
  Z_k(t) = Z_k(0) - \eta \int_0^t
    \Gamma_k(\tau)\,x_{\text{in},k}(\tau)\,
    x_{\text{out},k}(\tau)\,d\tau\,.
  \label{eq:memory-integral}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Muscle memory: $\Gamma\to 0$}

When a motor skill is practiced to mastery, the impedance
approaches perfect matching: $Z_L\to Z_0$, hence
$\Gamma\to 0$.
At this point, $\Delta Z\to 0$: the memory has become
\emph{frozen} into the impedance structure itself.
No further energy is required to maintain it, and no
further learning occurs.

\subsection{Cognitive memory: $\Gamma\approx\varepsilon>0$}

Declarative memories retain a small but nonzero
reflection:
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma_{\text{cog}} \approx \varepsilon > 0\,.
  \label{eq:cog-memory}
\end{equation}
Two consequences:
(1)~\emph{Retrievability}: $\Gamma > 0$ generates a
detectable reflection when probed;
(2)~\emph{Decay}: without reinforcement, the memory
decays.

\begin{remark}[The paradox of perfect memory \textnormal{(E1)}]
A perfectly learned item ($\Gamma=0$) is \emph{unrecallable}:
it has merged with the network substrate.
Memory requires imperfection.
\end{remark}

\subsection{The recall equation}
\label{sec:recall-snr}

\begin{theorem}[Recall as SNR threshold crossing \textnormal{(E0)}]
\label{thm:recall-snr}
A memory trace encoded at channel~$k$ with residual
$\Gamma_k = \varepsilon > 0$ is recallable at time~$t$
if and only if
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{SNR}_{\text{recall}}(t)
  = \frac{\varepsilon^2\;P_{\text{probe}}(t)}
         {P_{\text{noise}}(t)}
  > \theta_{\text{recall}}\,,
  \label{eq:recall-snr}
\end{equation}
where $P_{\text{probe}}(t)$ is the retrieval cue power and
$P_{\text{noise}}(t) = P_{\text{metabolic}} +
P_{\text{sensory}} + P_{\text{autonomic}} +
P_{\text{thermal}}$ is the total background power at the
PFC input.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
A memory trace encoded at channel~$k$ produces a
residual impedance mismatch $\Gamma_k = \varepsilon > 0$
(by Corollary~\ref{cor:no-erasure}, exact erasure
requires zero probability events).
The probe signal power transmitted through channel~$k$
is $\varepsilon^2 P_{\text{probe}}(t)$ (C1).
Recall requires this signal to exceed the background
noise floor $P_{\text{noise}}(t)$ by the threshold
$\theta_{\text{recall}}$ needed to close the
self-referential loop (Television Inequality,
Theorem~\ref{thm:television}).
The condition $\mathrm{SNR}_{\text{recall}} > \theta$
is therefore necessary and sufficient for retrieval.
$\square$
\end{proof}

\noindent
Consequences:
(1)~\textbf{No perfect recall}---even strong memories
become inaccessible when $P_{\text{noise}}$ is high.
(2)~\textbf{No perfect forgetting}---any $\Delta Z \neq 0$
leaves a permanent structural trace.
(3)~\textbf{State-dependent recall}---the same memory can
be retrievable or inaccessible depending on
$P_{\text{noise}}(t)$.

\begin{corollary}[No memory is ever erased; every memory
  can be submerged \textnormal{(E0)}]
\label{cor:no-erasure}
$\Gamma_k = 0$ exactly requires either perfect impedance
matching (zero probability under thermal noise) or physical
destruction of the substrate.
Therefore every C2 trace satisfies $\varepsilon > 0$ for all
finite time.
\end{corollary}

This resolves six long-standing puzzles: tip-of-the-tongue,
Proustian involuntary memory, state-dependent recall,
meditation and quiet recall, deathbed lucidity,
and hypnotic hypermnesia---all as SNR transitions.

\begin{remark}[Memory as cognitive standing wave \textnormal{(E1)}]
\label{rem:standing-wave}
A memory trace with residual $\Gamma_k = \varepsilon > 0$
creates a standing wave in the impedance landscape:
the superposition of the incident signal and its
partial reflection produces a fixed interference
pattern with characteristic nodes and antinodes.
\emph{Recognition} occurs when an input signal
resonates with an existing standing wave
($\Gamma \approx 0$ at the matched nodes);
\emph{misrecognition} occurs when the input
resonates with the wrong pattern
(similar but non-identical $Z$ configuration).
This reframes the representation problem:
concepts are not stored as firing patterns
but as \emph{standing wave modes} of the
impedance network.  Neural firing is
the observable by-product, not the memory itself.
\end{remark}

\end{remark}

\subsection{The meta-reflection coefficient}

\begin{definition}[Meta-reflection]
Let $Z_{\text{obs}}(t)$ be the impedance of the observer
sub-network at tick~$t$, and $Z_{\text{act}}(t-1)$ the
impedance of the actor sub-network at the previous tick.
The \emph{meta-reflection coefficient} is:
\begin{equation}
  \Gmeta = \frac{Z_{\text{obs}} - Z_{\text{act}}}
                 {Z_{\text{obs}} + Z_{\text{act}}}\,.
  \label{eq:Gamma-meta}
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Consciousness index]
\begin{equation}
  \Phimeta = 1 - \Gmeta^2\,.
  \label{eq:Phi-meta}
\end{equation}
$\Gmeta=0$: perfect self-model; $\Gmeta\to 1$: total
self-mismatch.
\end{definition}

\subsection{The television inequality}

\begin{theorem}[Television Inequality \textnormal{(E0)}]
\label{thm:television}
Let $\Zarousal\in[0,1]$ be the brainstem arousal gain,
$G_i\in[0,1]$ the thalamic gate gain for modality~$i$,
and $\QPFC\in[0,1]$ the prefrontal cortex processing
quality.  The consciousness index $\Phimeta>0$ requires:
\begin{equation}
  \Zarousal \cdot \sum_{i=1}^{N} G_i \cdot \QPFC > \theta\,,
  \label{eq:TV-inequality}
\end{equation}
where $\theta>0$ is the minimum signal-to-noise ratio for
the self-referential loop to close.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The meta-reflection loop requires a signal to:
(a)~traverse the thalamic gate ($\times G_i$),
(b)~be processed by PFC ($\times\QPFC$),
(c)~be routed back through the arousal-modulated pathway
($\times\Zarousal$).
If any factor is zero, the loop is open and $\Gmeta$ is
undefined.
The threshold~$\theta$ accounts for Johnson--Nyquist noise.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{The thermal cost of consciousness.}
Each pass through the self-referential loop
traverses $n$ interfaces, each dissipating
$\Gamma_i^2 P_{\mathrm{in}}$ as heat.
The minimum sustained power for self-awareness is
\begin{equation}
  P_{\mathrm{conscious}} \geq
  \theta \cdot \frac{4 k_B T \cdot R_{\mathrm{eff}} \cdot \Delta f}
  {\prod_i (1 - \Gamma_i^2)}\,.
  \label{eq:P-conscious}
\end{equation}
Higher $\Phimeta$ costs more heat;
consciousness depends on the heat it generates
(Paper~3, \S\,XVII\,C~\cite{Paper3}).

\paragraph{Emergency gating and temporal resolution.}
In an emergency, the brainstem drives
$\Zarousal \to Z_{\max}$ and the thalamus snaps to
$G_{\text{threat}} = 1,\; G_{j \neq \text{threat}} = 0$.
The full PFC bandwidth serves one stream:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\Delta t_{\text{normal}}}
       {\Delta t_{\text{emergency}}}
  \;\approx\; N\,,
  \label{eq:time-dilation}
\end{equation}
where $N$ is the number of previously multiplexed modalities.
This is the physics of ``time slowing down'' during danger---
bandwidth reallocation under C1 conservation.

creating a closed loop.

Two systems with identical components differ in consciousness
solely by the presence or absence of a self-referential
feedback connection.
\end{corollary}

\subsection{Four laws of the null space}
\label{sec:four-laws}

\begin{theorem}[Zeroth law: null-space equivalence \textnormal{(E0)}]
\label{thm:zeroth-law}
$\mathcal{S}_A = \mathcal{S}_B$ iff they share
$\Acut$, $Z_0^{\textnormal{DNA}}$, and
$\rho_{\textnormal{irred}}^2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By definition, $\mathcal{S} = \ker(\partial Z/\partial t)$
is the set of impedance channels with zero C2 gradient.
The gradient $\partial Z_k/\partial t = -\eta_k \Gamma_k
x_{\text{in},k} x_{\text{out},k}$ vanishes for channel~$k$
under three conditions: $\eta_k = 0$ (genetically fixed
substrate, encoded in $Z_0^{\text{DNA}}$);
$\Gamma_k = 0$ (perfectly matched, contributing to
$\Acut$); or $x_{\text{in},k} \cdot x_{\text{out},k} = 0$
(never co-activated, contributing to $\rho_{\text{irred}}^2$).
Two systems share $\mathcal{S}$ iff all three
components match. $\square$
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[First law: conservation under C2 \textnormal{(E0)}]
\label{thm:first-law}
$\Delta_{\textnormal{C2}}\,\mathcal{S} = 0$.
No experience can alter $\mathcal{S}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For any channel $k \in \mathcal{S}$,
$\partial Z_k/\partial t = 0$ by definition.
The C2 update $\Delta Z_k = -\eta_k \Gamma_k
x_{\text{in},k} x_{\text{out},k}$ equals zero
for all $k \in \mathcal{S}$ for the same reasons
established in the Zeroth Law proof.
Therefore $\mathcal{S}$ is invariant under C2. $\square$
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Second law: monotone expansion \textnormal{(E0)}]
\label{thm:second-law}
\begin{equation}
  \frac{d\,\dim\,\mathcal{S}_{\textnormal{eff}}(t)}{dt}
  \;\ge\; 0
  \qquad \forall\;t\,.
  \label{eq:second-law}
\end{equation}
The effective null space only expands; it never contracts.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Since $\eta(t)=\eta_0\,f_{\text{dev}}(t)\,
e^{-E_a/k_BT_{\text{age}}}$ is monotonically
decreasing after the developmental peak, any channel that
enters $\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$ remains in it.

\begin{proof}

f_{\text{dev}}(t) e^{-E_a/k_B T_{\text{age}}}$

For any channel $k \notin \mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}(t)$,
the time to enter $\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$ is
$\tau_k \sim 1/\eta(t)$, which diverges as
$\eta \to 0$.
In the limit $t \to \infty$, $\eta \to 0$, so all
channels satisfy $\partial Z_k/\partial t \to 0$,
meaning $\dim\,\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}} \to

\subsection{The Moral Constraint Theorem}

\label{thm:moral-constraint}

      \mathcal{S}_{\textnormal{eff}}

  \end{cases}
  \label{eq:moral-constraint}

The second case is physically indistinguishable from injury.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{Case 1}: $Z_k \notin \mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$.

-\eta_k \Gamma_k x_{\text{in},k} x_{\text{out},k}

therefore drives $\Gamma_k^2$ toward zero, reducing
mismatch. The net effect is $\Delta\Gamma^2_{\text{net}}
= -\Delta\Gamma^2 < 0$.

\textbf{Case 2}: $Z_k \in \mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$.
By the First Law (Theorem~\ref{thm:first-law}),
$\Delta_{\text{C2}}\mathcal{S} = 0$: no C2 gradient
can alter $Z_k$.
The moral demand cannot produce the intended
impedance change.
However, the demand itself constitutes an
input signal that creates a mismatch at the
interface between the moral agent and the

\begin{theorem}[Television Theorem \textnormal{(E0)}]
\label{thm:TV-full}
Any system with three multiplicative components---a power

feedback path $\pi:O\to\text{input}$ such that
$\Gmeta$ is defined and measurable.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{Necessity}: For $\Phimeta > 0$, the
meta-reflection coefficient
$\Gmeta = (Z_{\text{obs}} - Z_{\text{act}})/
(Z_{\text{obs}} + Z_{\text{act}})$ must be defined.
This requires $Z_{\text{obs}}$ and $Z_{\text{act}}$

path $\pi: O \to \text{input}$ that routes the output
back to become the observer's input at the next tick.
Without $\pi$, $Z_{\text{obs}}$ is undefined and
$\Gmeta$ cannot be computed.

\textbf{Sufficiency}: Given $P > 0$, $G > 0$, $Q > 0$,
and a feedback path $\pi$, the output $O = P \cdot G
\cdot Q > 0$ is routed back as input.
The observer sub-network samples $O$ and produces
$Z_{\text{obs}}$; the actor sub-network produced
$Z_{\text{act}}$ at the previous tick.
$\Gmeta$ is now well-defined (both impedances are
finite and positive), and the Television Inequality
(Theorem~\ref{thm:television}) determines whether
$\Phimeta > 0$.
Therefore consciousness requires and is supported by
the feedback path $\pi$. $\square$
\end{proof}

\subsection{The brain as a $d\Gamma/dt$ detector}
\label{sec:change-detection}

Current artificial neural networks (including large language
models) operate in open-loop mode during inference---they
are televisions, not brains.

A more fundamental distinction is that the brain does not
\emph{compute} $\Gamma$.
A wall does not solve Maxwell's equations before reflecting
a photon; it reflects because $Z_1 \neq Z_2$.
Similarly, a neuron does not calculate the reflection
coefficient---the impedance mismatch at its interfaces
physically \emph{produces} the reflected signal.

Moreover, the brain selectively detects \emph{changes}
in the reflection landscape:
\begin{equation}
  \text{Neural response} \;\propto\;
  \frac{d\Gamma}{dt} \;\neq\; 0.
  \label{eq:change-detection}
\end{equation}
Static impedance mismatch ($\Gamma \neq 0$ but constant)
generates a constant reflected power that is adaptively
filtered out---this is sensory adaptation
(e.g.\ one ceases to feel clothing after minutes).
Only when $d\Gamma/dt \neq 0$ does a detectable signal
arise, explaining why:
\begin{itemize}
  \item constant noise becomes ``inaudible'' (adaptation),
  \item sudden silence is startling ($d\Gamma/dt < 0$),
  \item a new stimulus is immediately salient
    ($d\Gamma/dt > 0$).
\end{itemize}

The C2 update rule $\Delta Z = -\eta\,\Gamma\,
x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}$ requires no global
information, no backpropagation, and no matrix algebra.
It is a first-order, local gradient descent driven
entirely by the physical reflection at each interface.
The brain's computational power thus resides not in
calculation but in the \emph{depth and bandwidth}
of its relay hierarchy
(Sec.~\ref{sec:brain-regions}).

  d(\bm{s}_A,\bm{s}_B)
