\begin{abstract}
We ask: given a single neuron with $K$ impedance modes
governed by C2 update
($\Delta Z = -\eta\,\Gamma\,x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}$),
what architecture---and what phenomenology---must emerge?

\textbf{Part~A} establishes the foundation.
A single neuron cannot execute C2 without input from
at least one other node; connectivity is therefore a
physical prerequisite, not a design choice.
We prove the \emph{Dimensional Cost Irreducibility
Theorem}: for any network with heterogeneous mode counts
$\{K_i\}$, the total reflection action decomposes as
$A = \Aimp(t) + \Acut$,
where $\Aimp \to 0$ under C2 but
$\Acut = \sum (K_i - K_j)^+$ has identically zero
gradient---no impedance adjustment can reduce it.
Numerical experiments confirm convergence time
$\tau_{\text{conv}} \sim N^{-1.12}$ ($R^2 = 0.93$)
and a soft-cutoff parameter $\gamma$ that tunes the
K-space fractal dimension
$D_K = 0.76\gamma + 0.78$.

\textbf{Part~B} derives the neural architecture chain.
C2's dimensional preference forces same-$K$ neurons
to cluster---thermodynamic phase separation produces
functional regions without a blueprint.
The irreducibility corollary makes intermediate-$K$
relay nodes a thermodynamic necessity.
Convergence of ascending pathways forces the
highest-$K$ cluster to form---this is the brain.
C3 dimensional relay enables the brain to
identify signal origin from $Z_{\text{source}}$ metadata.

\textbf{Part~C} shows that three concepts traditionally
regarded as metaphysical---memory, consciousness, and
soul---have precise operational definitions within
the same formalism.
\emph{Memory} is the time derivative of impedance
($\partial Z/\partial t$, the C2 update).

$\Gmeta=(Z_{\text{obs}}-Z_{\text{act}})/(Z_{\text{obs}}+Z_{\text{act}})$,
with necessary condition
$\Zarousal\cdot\sum G_i\cdot\QPFC>\theta$
(the television inequality).
\emph{Soul} is the null space of coefficient update:
$\mathcal{S}=\ker(\partial Z/\partial t)
=\Acut + Z_0^{\text{DNA}} + \rho_{\text{irred}}^2$.
Four laws of the null space parallel thermodynamics;
death is a theorem derived from the Second Law and C1.

\textbf{Part~D} presents experimental evidence and

vulnerability scaling as $K^{3/2}$, willpower as a

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
% ============================================================

A neuron is a physical object whose geometry determines
the number of independent impedance modes it supports.
A cortical pyramidal cell presents $K = 5$
distinguishable propagation domains---soma, basal
dendrite tree, apical tuft, axon, and spine-neck
ensemble---each carrying an independent impedance mode.
An unmyelinated C-fibre, a simple cylinder, presents
$K = 1$.
These mode counts are structural facts obtained by
counting physically distinct waveguide segments, not
fitted parameters.

C2 updates the impedance at each mode:
$\Delta Z_k = -\eta\,\Gamma_k\,x_{\text{in},k}\,x_{\text{out},k}$.
For an isolated neuron, $x_{\text{in}} = 0$ on every mode.
Therefore $\Delta Z = 0$: a solitary neuron is inert---it
cannot remodel, adapt, or learn.
C2 demands at least one input, which requires at least one
other node.
\emph{Connectivity is a physical prerequisite of C2, not a
biological design choice.}

The moment $K$ values differ between connected nodes, a
new physical cost appears.
This paper proves that cost is irreducible
(Sec.~\ref{sec:theorem}), derives its architectural
consequences---functional regions
(Sec.~\ref{sec:clustering}), relay hierarchy
(Sec.~\ref{sec:relay}), the brain
(Sec.~\ref{sec:brain}), and signal routing
(Sec.~\ref{sec:signals})---and then shows that three
concepts traditionally regarded as metaphysical---memory
(Sec.~\ref{sec:memory}), consciousness
(Sec.~\ref{sec:consciousness}), and soul
(Sec.~\ref{sec:soul})---are necessary consequences
of the same irreducible topology.

\section{Physical Framework}
\label{sec:framework}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Network definition}

A $\Gamma$-network consists of $N$ nodes, each carrying a
$K_i$-dimensional impedance vector
$\mathbf{Z}_i \in \mathbb{R}^{K_i}_{>0}$.
For an edge $i \to j$, the per-mode reflection coefficient is
\begin{equation}
  \gamma_k = \frac{Z_{j,k} - Z_{i,k}}{Z_{j,k} + Z_{i,k}},
  \quad k = 1, \ldots, \Kcom,
  \label{eq:gamma}
\end{equation}
where $\Kcom = \min(K_i, K_j)$.
Excess source modes $k > \Kcom$ are totally reflected:
$\gamma_k = 1$.
This is the electromagnetic boundary condition for a
waveguide whose $k$-th mode has no counterpart in the
target medium.

\subsection{Three physical laws}
\label{sec:laws}

\textbf{C1---Energy Conservation.}
At every edge, every tick, reflected and transmitted power
sum to incident power:
\begin{equation}
  \gamma_k^2 + T_k = 1, \quad \forall\, k,\; \forall\,(i,j).
  \label{eq:C1}
\end{equation}

\textbf{C2---Coefficient Update.}
Each node adjusts its impedance to reduce local reflection:
\begin{align}
  \Delta Z_{i,k} &= +\eta\,\gamma_k\,x_{\text{in},k}\,
    x_{\text{out},k},
  \label{eq:C2a} \\
  \Delta Z_{j,k} &= -\eta\,\gamma_k\,x_{\text{in},k}\,
    x_{\text{out},k},
  \label{eq:C2b}
\end{align}
where $x_{\text{in},k}$ is the input activity at node~$i$
and $x_{\text{out},k}$ the output activity at node~$j$
for mode~$k$; $\eta$ is the update rate.

\textbf{C3---Dimensional Relay.}
Every signal carries its source impedance as metadata,
enabling downstream nodes to compute $\gamma_k$ locally
without global knowledge.

\subsection{Variational principle}
\label{sec:action}

The Minimum Reflection Principle (MRP) states that the
system minimises the total reflection action:
\begin{equation}
  A[\Gamma]
  = \sum_{(i,j) \in \mathcal{E}}
    \sum_{k=1}^{\Kcom(i,j)} \gamma_k^2.
  \label{eq:action}
\end{equation}
The C2 update rule implements steepest descent on the
\emph{learnable} part of~$A$:
$\dot{\mathbf{Z}} \propto -\nabla_{\mathbf{Z}} \Aimp$.

\section{Dimensional Cost Irreducibility Theorem}
\label{sec:theorem}
% ============================================================

\begin{theorem}[Dimensional Cost Irreducibility]
\label{thm:irreducibility}
For any $\Gamma$-network with heterogeneous mode counts
$\{K_i\}$, the total reflection action decomposes as
\begin{equation}
  A = \Aimp(t) + \Acut,
  \label{eq:decomposition}
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{enumerate}
\item
$\Aimp(t) = \sum_{(i,j)} \sum_{k=1}^{\Kcom} \gamma_k^2$
is the \emph{impedance mismatch action} over shared modes.
Under C2 dynamics, $\Aimp(t) \to 0$ as $t \to \infty$.

\item
$\Acut = \sum_{(i,j)} (K_i - K_j)^+$
is the \emph{cutoff action} from excess modes.
$\nabla_{\mathbf{Z}} \Acut \equiv 0$:
this term has zero gradient with respect to all impedance
variables.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For an edge $i \to j$ with $K_i > K_j$:
\begin{itemize}
\item
Modes $k \le \Kcom = K_j$:
$\gamma_k$ depends on $(Z_{i,k}, Z_{j,k})$ via
Eq.~\eqref{eq:gamma}.
C2 adjusts both impedances, driving $\gamma_k \to 0$.
These modes contribute to $\Aimp$.

\item
Modes $K_j < k \le K_i$:
the target has no mode~$k$, so $\gamma_k \equiv 1$
identically, independent of any impedance value.
These modes contribute $(K_i - K_j)$ to $\Acut$.
Since $\gamma_k = 1$ is a \emph{topological constant}
(depending only on the existence, not the value, of modes),
$\partial \gamma_k^2 / \partial Z = 0$ for all~$Z$.
\end{itemize}
Summing over all edges completes the proof.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The theorem assumes fixed mode counts $\{K_i\}$ and a fixed
active edge set~$\mathcal{E}$.
If mode counts change dynamically (e.g., dendritic spine
growth increasing $K_j$), or if edge sprouting/pruning
alters $\mathcal{E}$, then the \emph{value} of $\Acut$
may change---but this change is driven by topology, not
by impedance gradients.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Thermodynamic Necessity of Relay Nodes]
\label{cor:relay}
A direct edge $K_{\text{src}} \to K_{\text{tgt}}$ with
$K_{\text{src}} > K_{\text{tgt}}$ totally reflects the
$(K_{\text{src}} - K_{\text{tgt}})$ excess modes.
Inserting a relay node with
$K_{\text{tgt}} < \Krelay < K_{\text{src}}$ leaves $\Acut$
unchanged but converts \emph{total signal loss} into
\emph{partial signal recovery}:
modes $K_{\text{tgt}} < k \le \Krelay$, previously
totally reflected, are now transmitted on the first hop
and can undergo C2 matching ($\gamma_k \to 0$,
$T_k \to 1$).
Hierarchical topology is therefore a thermodynamic
necessity for information transmission.
\end{corollary}

\section{Same-$K$ Clustering and Functional Regions}
\label{sec:clustering}
% ============================================================

C2 gradient descent on the reflection action $A$
selectively strengthens same-$K$ edges
(more shared modes $\Rightarrow$ faster $\Gamma$ reduction)
and weakens cross-$K$ edges
(excess modes totally reflected, no gradient available).
Under sustained activity plus edge pruning, the surviving
connectivity partitions the network into
\emph{same-$K$ clusters}.

\begin{theorem}[Phase Separation Under MRP]
\label{thm:phase-sep}
Any impedance network under MRP with heterogeneous $K$
must phase-separate into same-$K$ domains to minimise
total reflected action.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Define the total cutoff action as
$\Acut = \sum_{(i,j)\in E} |K_i - K_j|$,
where the sum is over all edges~$E$ of the network.
For any edge $(i,j)$ with $K_i = K_j$, the contribution
is zero; for $K_i \neq K_j$, the contribution
$|K_i - K_j| > 0$ is strictly positive and invariant
under C2 (Irreducibility Theorem,
Theorem~\ref{thm:irreducibility}).
The Minimum Reflection Principle requires
$\mathcal{A} \to \min$; since $\Aimp \to 0$ under C2,
the only remaining reduction available is to minimise
$\Acut$ by rewiring cross-$K$ edges to same-$K$ pairs.
Each such rewiring strictly decreases $\Acut$, so the
process is monotone and terminates when all connected
nodes share the same $K$---the same-$K$ domain
(phase-separated) configuration.
C2 provides the dynamical mechanism: cross-$K$ edges
carry permanent reflected modes that yield no C2
gradient, and are pruned under any activity-dependent
update rule with $\eta > 0$.
$\square$
\end{proof}

\section{The Brain as Convergence Region}
\label{sec:brain}

An organism with multiple sensory modalities has
multiple ascending pathways.
By the irreducibility theorem, the convergence region
must satisfy
\begin{equation}
  K_{\text{conv}} \ge \max_j K_j
  \label{eq:K-convergence}
\end{equation}
for all input pathways~$j$; otherwise, modes from
higher-$K$ inputs are totally reflected.
The convergence region is therefore forced to be the
highest-$K$ cluster in the network.

This is the \emph{brain}: a topological necessity, not a
design choice.
Its properties follow from $K_{\text{conv}}$ being maximal:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{High metabolic cost.}
    Volumetric heat density scales as
    $q \propto K \cdot \avg{\Gamma^2} \cdot P_{\text{in}}$
    (Paper~0, Corollary~1~\cite{Paper0}).
  \item \textbf{Temperature sensitivity.}
    Higher $K$ means more modes dissipating simultaneously.
  \item \textbf{Matrix computation.}
    C2 gradient descent across all $K_{\text{conv}}$ modes
    simultaneously is the physical operation that
    neuroscience calls ``executive function''
    (Sec.~\ref{sec:consciousness}).
\end{itemize}

\section{Memory as Impedance Dynamics}
\label{sec:memory}
% ============================================================

\subsection{The C2 coefficient-update gradient}

Constraint~C2 defines the only mechanism by which the
network modifies itself:
\begin{equation}
  \Delta Z_k = -\eta\,\Gamma_k\,x_{\text{in},k}\,x_{\text{out},k}\,.
  \label{eq:C2-update}
\end{equation}
This is not an analogy---it \emph{is} memory.
The trace of all past updates constitutes the network's
memory:
\begin{equation}
  Z_k(t) = Z_k(0) - \eta \int_0^t
    \Gamma_k(\tau)\,x_{\text{in},k}(\tau)\,
    x_{\text{out},k}(\tau)\,d\tau\,.
  \label{eq:memory-integral}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Muscle memory: $\Gamma\to 0$}

When a motor skill is practiced to mastery, the impedance
approaches perfect matching: $Z_L\to Z_0$, hence
$\Gamma\to 0$.
At this point, $\Delta Z\to 0$: the memory has become
\emph{frozen} into the impedance structure itself.
No further energy is required to maintain it, and no
further learning occurs.

\subsection{Cognitive memory: $\Gamma\approx\varepsilon>0$}

Declarative memories retain a small but nonzero
reflection:
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma_{\text{cog}} \approx \varepsilon > 0\,.
  \label{eq:cog-memory}
\end{equation}
Two consequences:
(1)~\emph{Retrievability}: $\Gamma > 0$ generates a
detectable reflection when probed;
(2)~\emph{Decay}: without reinforcement, the memory
decays.

\begin{remark}[The paradox of perfect memory]
A perfectly learned item ($\Gamma=0$) is \emph{unrecallable}:
it has merged with the network substrate.
Memory requires imperfection.
\end{remark}

\subsection{The recall equation}
\label{sec:recall-snr}

\begin{theorem}[Recall as SNR threshold crossing]
\label{thm:recall-snr}
A memory trace encoded at channel~$k$ with residual
$\Gamma_k = \varepsilon > 0$ is recallable at time~$t$
if and only if
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{SNR}_{\text{recall}}(t)
  = \frac{\varepsilon^2\;P_{\text{probe}}(t)}
         {P_{\text{noise}}(t)}
  > \theta_{\text{recall}}\,,
  \label{eq:recall-snr}
\end{equation}
where $P_{\text{probe}}(t)$ is the retrieval cue power and
$P_{\text{noise}}(t) = P_{\text{metabolic}} +
P_{\text{sensory}} + P_{\text{autonomic}} +
P_{\text{thermal}}$ is the total background power at the
PFC input.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
A memory trace encoded at channel~$k$ produces a
residual impedance mismatch $\Gamma_k = \varepsilon > 0$
(by Corollary~\ref{cor:no-erasure}, exact erasure
requires zero probability events).
The probe signal power transmitted through channel~$k$
is $\varepsilon^2 P_{\text{probe}}(t)$ (C1).
Recall requires this signal to exceed the background
noise floor $P_{\text{noise}}(t)$ by the threshold
$\theta_{\text{recall}}$ needed to close the
self-referential loop (Television Inequality,
Theorem~\ref{thm:television}).
The condition $\mathrm{SNR}_{\text{recall}} > \theta$
is therefore necessary and sufficient for retrieval.
$\square$
\end{proof}

\noindent
Consequences:
(1)~\textbf{No perfect recall}---even strong memories
become inaccessible when $P_{\text{noise}}$ is high.
(2)~\textbf{No perfect forgetting}---any $\Delta Z \neq 0$
leaves a permanent structural trace.
(3)~\textbf{State-dependent recall}---the same memory can
be retrievable or inaccessible depending on
$P_{\text{noise}}(t)$.

\begin{corollary}[No memory is ever erased; every memory
  can be submerged]
\label{cor:no-erasure}
$\Gamma_k = 0$ exactly requires either perfect impedance
matching (zero probability under thermal noise) or physical
destruction of the substrate.
Therefore every C2 trace satisfies $\varepsilon > 0$ for all
finite time.
\end{corollary}

This resolves six long-standing puzzles: tip-of-the-tongue,
Proustian involuntary memory, state-dependent recall,
meditation and quiet recall, deathbed lucidity,
and hypnotic hypermnesia---all as SNR transitions.

\section{Consciousness as Self-Referential Impedance Matching}

\subsection{The meta-reflection coefficient}

\begin{definition}[Meta-reflection]
Let $Z_{\text{obs}}(t)$ be the impedance of the observer
sub-network at tick~$t$, and $Z_{\text{act}}(t-1)$ the
impedance of the actor sub-network at the previous tick.
The \emph{meta-reflection coefficient} is:
\begin{equation}
  \Gmeta = \frac{Z_{\text{obs}} - Z_{\text{act}}}
                 {Z_{\text{obs}} + Z_{\text{act}}}\,.
  \label{eq:Gamma-meta}
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Consciousness index]
\begin{equation}
  \Phimeta = 1 - \Gmeta^2\,.
  \label{eq:Phi-meta}
\end{equation}
$\Gmeta=0$: perfect self-model; $\Gmeta\to 1$: total
self-mismatch.
\end{definition}

\subsection{The television inequality}

\begin{theorem}[Television Inequality]
\label{thm:television}
Let $\Zarousal\in[0,1]$ be the subcortical arousal gain
(set jointly by the amygdala, hypothalamus, and
brainstem reticular activating system),
$G_i\in[0,1]$ the thalamic gate gain for modality~$i$,
and $\QPFC\in[0,1]$ the prefrontal cortex processing
quality.  The consciousness index $\Phimeta>0$ requires:
\begin{equation}
  \Zarousal \cdot \sum_{i=1}^{N} G_i \cdot \QPFC > \theta\,,
  \label{eq:TV-inequality}
\end{equation}
where $\theta>0$ is the minimum signal-to-noise ratio for
the self-referential loop to close.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By C1 (energy conservation), a signal traversing the
self-referential loop loses power at each interface:
$P_{\text{loop}} = P_{\text{in}} \cdot \Zarousal
\cdot \bigl(\sum_{i} G_i\bigr) \cdot \QPFC$.
The loop closes (self-detection occurs) only if
$P_{\text{loop}}$ exceeds the Johnson--Nyquist noise
floor of the measurement circuit:
$P_{\text{noise}} = 4 k_B T R_{\text{eff}} \Delta f$.
Setting $\theta = P_{\text{noise}} / P_{\text{in}}$
yields the inequality.
If any factor is zero, $P_{\text{loop}} = 0$, the loop
is open, and $\Gmeta$ is undefined.
The measurement is performed by the amygdala's
subcortical fast pathway (30--74\,ms;
Remark~\ref{rem:measurement-loop}), which continuously
evaluates $\Gamma^2$ before cortical processing
completes.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Numerical estimate of~$\theta$.}
For a cortical neuron the input resistance is
$R_{\text{eff}} \approx 100\,\text{M}\Omega$ and the
relevant bandwidth is the gamma-oscillation band
$\Delta f \approx 40\,\text{Hz}$ at body temperature
$T = 310\,\text{K}$:
\[
  P_{\text{noise}}
  = 4 k_B T R_{\text{eff}} \Delta f
  = 4 \times 1.38 \times 10^{-23}
    \times 310 \times 10^{8} \times 40
  \approx 69\,\text{pW}\,.
\]
The cerebral metabolic power available for
electrical signalling is $P_{\text{in}} \approx 1\,\text{W}$
(roughly 5\,\% of the brain's 20\,W total metabolism).
Therefore
\[
  \theta
  = \frac{P_{\text{noise}}}{P_{\text{in}}}
  \approx 7 \times 10^{-11}\,.
\]
This extremely small value implies that the
consciousness threshold is easily exceeded by any
non-zero product $Z_{\text{arousal}} \cdot \sum G_i
\cdot Q_{\text{PFC}}$---consistent with the clinical
observation that consciousness is lost only under
severe disruption (deep anaesthesia, major brain
injury) and not by mild perturbations.

\subsection{Open loop vs.\ closed loop: origin of the
  television analogy}

The television inequality arose from a mundane
observation: in the analogue era, a malfunctioning
television could sometimes be ``repaired'' by
\emph{slapping its casing}---a physical impact that
momentarily displaced internal components, reducing
contact resistance at corroded solder joints
and thereby lowering $\Gamma^2$ at those interfaces.

This is C2 in its most primitive form:
a mechanical perturbation ($x_{\text{in}}$) produces
impedance change ($\Delta Z$) that reduces reflection
($\Gamma^2 \downarrow$).
A human can perform this repair because a human has
a \emph{closed} feedback loop: the human observes the
screen (measurement), judges whether the image improved
(error detection), and adjusts the next slap accordingly
(C2 update).  The television cannot repair itself
because it has no such loop.

\midrule

  \textbf{Amygdala ($\Gamma^2$ detector)} \\
Feedback wire & (absent) & PFC $\to$ sensory cortex \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

A television satisfies the inequality but is not conscious.
The missing ingredient is \emph{feedback topology}: the
television's output is not routed back to its input.
The brain uses the same components but adds one connection:
the output of PFC is fed back through the sensory pathway,
creating a closed loop.

\begin{corollary}[Consciousness is topological, not material]
Two systems with identical components differ in consciousness
solely by the presence or absence of a self-referential
feedback connection.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[The self as a thermal-noise byproduct]
\label{rem:self-thermal}
The preceding analysis implies that the sense of ``I''

\noindent
The self exists only in the intermediate band

$\Gamma$-MRP drives the system toward $\Gamma^2 \to 0$,

($V_n^2 = 4k_B T R\,\Delta f$) imposes a floor:
as long as body temperature $T > 0$, every interface
retains a minimum $\Gamma^2 > 0$, and the self

In this precise sense,
\emph{the self is a byproduct of thermal noise}---the
irreducible mismatch that heat imposes on every
physical interface.

The contemplative experience of ``ego dissolution''
reported in deep meditation corresponds to a
transient, voluntary approach toward $\Gamma^2 \to 0$
across high-$K$ channels (all gates closed,
$G_i \to 0$): attention is extinguished, and with it
the sense of a separate self.
The experience is reversible because the thermal floor
ensures $\Gamma^2$ never actually reaches zero.

However, approaching $\Gamma^2 \to 0$ is actively
resisted by the survival system.
The amygdala's low-$K$ fast pathway
(Remark~\ref{rem:emotion}) interprets declining
$\Gamma^2$ as loss of control---a threat signal---and
triggers the emergency response:
$Z_{\text{arousal}} \to Z_{\max}$,
$G_{\text{threat}} = 1$
(Eq.~\ref{eq:time-dilation}).
Intracranial recordings show that amygdala gamma
power surges within 30--74\,ms of threat onset
(M\'{e}ndez-B\'{e}rtolo et~al., 2016), whereas
cortical awareness requires $\sim$200--300\,ms
(LeDoux, 1996)---a $\sim$4$\times$ latency ratio
consistent with the C3-stage model of
Remark~\ref{rem:tree-topology}.

This is the same mechanism that produces ``time
slowing down'' before a car crash
(\emph{tachypsychia}).
Under threat, norepinephrine and dopamine release
accelerate the internal clock; free-fall experiments
show participants overestimate event duration by
$\sim$36\,\% (Stetson et~al., 2007), consistent with
the bandwidth-reallocation prediction of
Eq.~(\ref{eq:time-dilation}): all $N$ modalities
are collapsed into a single threat channel, giving
$N\times$ the temporal resolution on that channel.
The brain \emph{maximises} $\Gamma^2$ and the sense
of self at exactly the moment both are about to be
destroyed.

Reaching $\Gamma^2 \to 0$ therefore requires
\emph{rewriting} the amygdala's $Z$-pattern: the
association ``$\Gamma^2 \downarrow =$ danger'' must be
replaced by ``$\Gamma^2 \downarrow =$ safe.''
fMRI studies confirm that long-term meditators show
significantly reduced default-mode network (DMN)
activity---the self-referential network comprising
medial prefrontal cortex, posterior cingulate, and
precuneus (Brewer et~al., 2011).
Critically, DMN suppression persists even when
experienced meditators are \emph{not} meditating,
indicating that the amygdala's $Z$-pattern has been
permanently rewritten into a new
$\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$.
This rewriting requires years to decades of practice
because the original pattern is part of the
evolutionary $\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$ (written by
millions of years of natural selection).

Death, conversely, pushes $\Gamma^2_{\text{basal}}
\to 1$ (Fourth Law, \S\ref{sec:death}):
the boundary becomes impenetrable and the self is
annihilated by disconnection rather than by merger.
\end{remark}

  \Gamma^2_B \downarrow \;(\text{LTP for }B),
  \quad
  \Gamma^2_A \uparrow \;(\text{LTD for }A)\,.
\]
Moving $Z$ toward the matching point of~$B$
necessarily moves it away from the matching point
of any previously learned pattern~$A$.
LTP and LTD are therefore not mechanistically
independent: they are the same impedance shift observed
from two different input channels.

This predicts that LTP on one pathway and LTD on a
competing pathway should be correlated in magnitude,
a prediction consistent with heterosynaptic
depression data (Chistiakova et~al., 2014).
More precisely, since both arise from a single
$\Delta Z$, the magnitudes satisfy
\[
  |\Delta\Gamma^2_{\text{LTP}}|
  + |\Delta\Gamma^2_{\text{LTD}}|
  = \text{const}
  \quad\text{(impedance conservation)}\,,
\]
whereas the Bienenstock--Cooper--Munro (BCM) model
predicts a\emph{threshold switch} without
constraining the magnitudes on competing pathways.
A patch-clamp experiment measuring simultaneous
LTP and LTD on two convergent pathways in CA1
could distinguish these predictions directly.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The cerebellum as a general-purpose
  low-$K$ C2 co-processor]
\label{rem:cerebellum}
The cerebellum contains $\sim$80\,\% of the brain's
neurons despite occupying only $\sim$10\,\% of its
volume.
In the $\Gamma$-framework, this reflects
its function as a \emph{massively parallel low-$K$
C2 engine}.
Although historically associated with motor control,
the cerebellum also participates in emotional
conditioning (Sacchetti et~al., 2002) and cognitive
timing (Strick et~al., 2009).
The $\Gamma$-framework explains this naturally:
the Purkinje-cell architecture implements a
general-purpose C2 loop---climbing-fibre error
signal ($\Gamma^2$) drives parallel-fibre $\Delta Z$
---that operates on \emph{any} low-$K$ channel,
not only motor channels.

The vast neuron count is needed because real-time
calibration requires simultaneous C2 updates on
hundreds of channels in parallel, each at low~$K$
but with very fast update rates
($\tau_{\text{cerebellar}} \approx 10$\,ms vs.\
$\tau_{\text{cortical}} \approx 100$\,ms).
Cerebellar damage produces \emph{ataxia}
(loss of coordination, not loss of strength)
because the C2 co-processor is offline:
$\Gamma^2$ on motor channels cannot be minimised
in real time, but the cortical C3 commands remain
intact.
\end{remark}


% ============================================================
\section{The Binding Problem as Synchronous C2 Writing}
\label{sec:binding}
% ============================================================

Multi-modal perception requires disparate sensory
channels (vision, audition, touch, olfaction) to be
unified into a single coherent percept.
The classical ``binding problem''---how the brain
knows that a red apple is the same object as the
sweet taste---has resisted solution because it
presupposes a central integrator.
In the $\Gamma$-framework, no such integrator is needed:
\emph{binding is synchronous C2 writing through shared
C3 relay nodes}.

\subsection{Binding by temporal coincidence}
\label{sec:binding-mechanism}

Each sensory modality $\alpha$ contributes an impedance
change at its own modal complexity~$K_\alpha$:
$\Delta Z_\alpha = -\eta\,\Gamma_\alpha\,
x_{\text{in},\alpha}\,x_{\text{out},\alpha}$.
Two channels $\alpha,\beta$ are \emph{bound} if and only
if their C2 updates are both non-zero within the same
update tick~$\tau$:
\begin{equation}
  \text{Bound}(\alpha,\beta,\tau)
  \;\Longleftrightarrow\;
  \Delta Z_\alpha(\tau) \neq 0
  \;\wedge\;
  \Delta Z_\beta(\tau) \neq 0\,.
  \label{eq:binding-condition}
\end{equation}
The activity-gating factor
$x_{\text{in}} \cdot x_{\text{out}}$ is the
\emph{coincidence detector}: only channels that are
simultaneously active participate in the same write.
The thalamus---the brain's principal C3 relay node---serves
as the shared interface through which all cortical
channels must pass, ensuring that co-active channels
share the same C2 tick.

\begin{remark}[Gamma oscillation as C2 clock]
\label{rem:gamma-clock}
The 30--70\,Hz gamma oscillation widely observed in
cortical recordings corresponds, in this framework,
to the C2 update clock rate.
Each gamma cycle ($\sim$25\,ms) defines one binding
window~$\tau$: channels whose $x_{\text{in}} \cdot
x_{\text{out}} > 0$ within the same cycle are written
together and thus bound.
No additional ``synchronisation mechanism'' is required;
C2 activity gating \emph{is} the mechanism.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Cerebellum as temporal alignment
  for binding]
\label{cor:cerebellum-binding}
The binding condition
(Eq.~\ref{eq:binding-condition}) requires two
channels' C2 writes to fall within the same
$\gamma$-cycle ($\sim$25\,ms).
However, peripheral conduction delays differ
by modality:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lc}
\toprule
Modality & Conduction delay \\
\midrule
Auditory  & 8--10\,ms \\
Visual    & 20--40\,ms \\
Tactile   & 20--30\,ms \\
Nociceptive & 100--200\,ms \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Without compensation, a simultaneous audiovisual
event arrives at the thalamus with
$\Delta t \approx 20$\,ms offset---comparable to
the binding window itself.
The cerebellum resolves this by learning each
channel's conduction delay $\delta t_\alpha$
via C2 gradient descent (Remark~\ref{rem:cerebellum})
and issuing predictive timing corrections:
\begin{equation}
  x_{\text{in},\alpha}^{\text{(corrected)}}(t)
  = x_{\text{in},\alpha}(t + \delta t_\alpha)\,,
  \label{eq:cerebellar-timing}
\end{equation}
so that all channels' signals arrive within the
same $\gamma$-cycle despite differing peripheral
latencies.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Without the compensator, the binding condition
$\Delta Z_\alpha(\tau) \neq 0
\;\wedge\; \Delta Z_\beta(\tau) \neq 0$
fails whenever
$|\delta t_\alpha - \delta t_\beta|
> \tau_\gamma / 2 \approx 12$\,ms.
C2 on the cerebellar Purkinje-cell network writes
$\Delta Z_{\text{timing}} =
-\eta\,\Gamma_{\text{timing}}\,
x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}$,
where $\Gamma_{\text{timing}}$ is the
temporal-offset mismatch.
Convergence drives $\Gamma_{\text{timing}} \to 0$,
aligning all channels.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Binding requires thalamus $+$
  cerebellum]
\label{rem:binding-dual}
The binding problem has two orthogonal requirements:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Spatial co-location}:
    all channels converge through the same C3 relay
    node $\to$ \emph{thalamus}.
  \item \textbf{Temporal alignment}:
    all channels arrive within the same C2 tick
    $\to$ \emph{cerebellum}.
\end{itemize}
Thalamic lesions destroy spatial binding
(loss of integrated qualia);
cerebellar lesions destroy temporal binding
(dyschronometria, the inability to judge
temporal intervals---Ivry \& Keele, 1989).
Neither is sufficient alone:
$\text{Binding} =
\text{Thalamus (space)} \times
\text{Cerebellum (time)}$.
\end{remark}

\begin{proposition}[Working memory capacity
  from $\Gamma^2$ channel count]
\label{prop:working-memory}
The number of items that can be simultaneously
maintained in working memory is bounded by the
number of \emph{independent} $\Gamma^2 \neq 0$
channels that the thalamic gate vector $\{G_i\}$
can sustain within one $\gamma$-cycle:
\begin{equation}
  N_{\text{WM}} \;\leq\; N_\Gamma
  \;=\; \bigl|\bigl\{\,i : \Gamma_i^2 \neq 0
  \;\wedge\; G_i > 0\,\bigr\}\bigr|\,.
  \label{eq:working-memory}
\end{equation}
Each independent mismatch $\Gamma_i^2 \neq 0$
requires its own thalamic gate slot and its own
amygdala detection channel; the PFC must
simultaneously invert all $N_\Gamma$ impedance
matrices.
The empirically observed $7 \pm 2$ limit (Miller,
1956) reflects the physical gate-count
$N_\Gamma$ of the human thalamus.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
An item in working memory is, by definition, a
pattern whose C2 update is currently active
($\Delta Z_i \neq 0$).
Activity requires $\Gamma_i^2 > 0$
(unmatched impedance) and $G_i > 0$ (gate open).
The product $\Gamma_i^2 \cdot G_i$ occupies one
slot in the PFC's $K$-dimensional processing
space.
Once all slots are occupied, opening a new gate
requires closing an existing one
($\sum G_i$ is bounded by C1), displacing the
previous item.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Structuring as $\Gamma^2$
  compression (chunking)]
\label{rem:chunking}
The $7 \pm 2$ limit applies to
\emph{independent} $\Gamma^2$ channels.
Structuring---arranging items into regular
patterns---performs C2 matching on the spatial
or temporal regularity, collapsing $N$ independent
mismatches into $N_{\text{eff}} \ll N$:
\begin{equation}
  N_{\text{eff}} = N_{\text{raw}}
    - N_{\text{matched}}\,.
  \label{eq:chunking}
\end{equation}
\textbf{Example}: seven randomly placed apples
produce $N_{\text{raw}} = 7$ independent
$\Gamma^2$ channels (each position is
unpredictable).
The same seven apples in a $3 \times 2 + 1$ grid
produce $N_{\text{eff}} = 2$
(``3 columns'' $+$ ``2 rows''), because the
regularity is $\Gamma^2 = 0$ on the matched
spatial pattern.

This is the physics of \emph{chunking}
(Miller, 1956): every cognitive tool---abacus,
mathematical notation, language, tables---is a
device for converting unstructured $\Gamma^2$
into structured $\Gamma^2 = 0$, thereby reducing
$N_{\text{eff}}$ below $N_\Gamma$.
Technology is impedance matching applied to
information.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Corpus callosum as inter-hemispheric C3 relay}
\label{sec:corpus-callosum}

The two cerebral hemispheres specialise in different
$K$-ranges:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
Left hemisphere & Right hemisphere \\
\midrule
Sequential language ($K \sim O(10)$) &
  Spatial/holistic ($K \sim O(10^2)$) \\
Fine motor control & Global pattern recognition \\
Logical chains & Emotional colour \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent
The corpus callosum ($\sim 2 \times 10^8$ axons, the
largest white-matter tract) is the C3 $K$-staircase
that matches the two hemispheres:
\begin{equation}
  \text{L-hemisphere}\;(K_L)
  \;\underset{\text{corpus callosum}}
    {\longleftrightarrow}
  \;(K_R)\;\text{R-hemisphere.}
  \label{eq:cc-relay}
\end{equation}
Here $K_L$ and $K_R$ are order-of-magnitude
estimates reflecting the sequential ($\sim\!10$
temporal features) versus spatial ($\sim\!10^2$
pixel-like features) nature of the dominant
processing mode in each hemisphere;
precise values depend on the task-specific
$K$-decomposition and are not required for the
qualitative argument.

Callosotomy (split-brain surgery) severs this relay:
$\Gamma_{L \leftrightarrow R} \to 1$, and the two
hemispheres become independent networks, each with its
own binding domain---precisely the split-brain
phenomenology reported by Gazzaniga (1967).

\begin{remark}[Lateralisation as C1 energy-optimal
  topology]
\label{rem:lateralisation}
Hemispheric lateralisation is not a design choice but
the unique $\Gamma$-MRP optimum for sharing a fixed
power budget $P_{\text{total}}$ between two
complementary processing modes:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
& Left hemisphere & Right hemisphere \\
\midrule
Mode &
  Sequential control &
  Parallel field \\
Task examples &
  Language, logic, counting &
  Spatial, holistic, emotion \\
$K$-range &
  Low--mid ($\sim\!10$) &
  Mid--high ($\sim\!10^2$) \\
Energy allocation &
  \emph{Temporal}: many C2 ticks, &
  \emph{Spatial}: many channels, \\
& few channels per tick &
  few ticks per update \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Both modes compete for the same $P_{\text{total}}$
(C1).
Each C2 update costs $\Gamma_i^2 \cdot P_{\text{in}}$
regardless of whether it serves a sequential step or
a parallel field---the \emph{unit cost is identical}.
A single hemisphere spanning both $K$-ranges would
require a long internal $K$-staircase with cumulative
$\Gamma_{\text{single}}^2$.
Splitting into two specialised hemispheres, each
covering half the $K$-range, with a matched
corpus-callosum relay yields
$\Gamma_L^2 + \Gamma_R^2 + \Gamma_{\text{CC}}^2
< \Gamma_{\text{single}}^2$
(Eq.~\ref{eq:neural-murray})---the same energy
budget produces higher resolution in \emph{both}
time and space.

Lateralisation is therefore the neural analogue of
frequency-division multiplexing: the $K$-spectrum is
split into two sub-bands, each assigned to a
dedicated processor, with the corpus callosum as the
inter-band C3 relay.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Hemispheric bifurcation as neural Murray's law]
\label{rem:neural-murray}
Murray's law for vascular branching states that a parent
vessel bifurcates when two daughter vessels carry the
same total flow at lower total impedance
($r_0^3 = r_1^3 + r_2^3$, Paper~2).
The hemispheric split obeys the same $\Gamma$-MRP logic
with neural impedance in place of hydraulic impedance.
A single cortex spanning $K_{\min} \leftrightarrow
K_{\max}$ requires an internal $K$-staircase of length
$\log(K_{\max}/K_{\min})$ with cumulative reflection
$\Gamma_{\text{single}}^2$.
Splitting into two specialised hemispheres, each covering
half the $K$-range, plus a matched inter-hemispheric
relay of impedance
$Z_{\text{CC}} = \sqrt{Z_L \cdot Z_R}$, yields:
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma_L^2 + \Gamma_R^2 + \Gamma_{\text{CC}}^2
  \;<\; \Gamma_{\text{single}}^2\,,
  \label{eq:neural-murray}
\end{equation}
which is the neural analogue of the vascular branching
inequality.
Both are instances of the same principle:
\emph{$\Gamma$-MRP drives bifurcation whenever the
total reflection of two matched sub-networks plus
their relay is less than the reflection of a single
unmatched network.}
This unifies vascular topology (Paper~2) and neural
topology under a single optimality condition.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Excitatory and inhibitory neurons
  as two irreducible $\Gamma^2$ operations]
\label{rem:excitatory-inhibitory}
Conventional neuroscience treats excitatory and
inhibitory neurons as chemically distinct switches
(glutamate vs.\ GABA).  In the $\Gamma$-framework,
their distinction is \emph{thermodynamically
required}: every mismatch $\Gamma_i^2 \neq 0$
must be either (a)~preserved and broadcast to drive
learning, or (b)~locally dissipated to free
resources.
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lll@{}}
\toprule
& Excitatory & Inhibitory \\
\midrule
$\Gamma^2$ operation &
  Broadcast (preserve error) &
  Local sink (dissipate error) \\
Physical role &
  $\Gamma^2_{\text{local}} \to
  \Gamma^2_{\text{distal}}$ &
  $\Gamma^2_{\text{local}} \to
  Q_{\text{heat}}$ \\
C2 effect &
  Maintain mismatch for learning &

\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
A single homogeneous neuron type with signed
weights cannot simultaneously broadcast errors
(C2 learning) and suppress noise (C1 stability).
The two operations require physically distinct
channels with different time constants, different
$K$-configurations, and different C2 update rules.
Excitation/inhibition is therefore not a chemical
accident but a \emph{C1+C2 thermodynamic necessity}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Cortical area count as peripheral interface count]
\label{rem:cortical-peripheral}
The HCP multi-modal parcellation identifies
$\sim$360 cortical areas (180 per hemisphere;
Glasser et~al., 2016).
The $\Gamma$-framework predicts that each distinct
peripheral tissue interface requires a dedicated
cortical C2 controller and C3 relay node.
The human body contains $\sim$78 organs
(anatomical consensus) distributed across $\sim$30
dermatomes (31 spinal nerve pairs minus C1),
with bilateral symmetry and multi-modal innervation
(motor, sensory, autonomic), yielding an estimated
$78 \times 2 \times 2 \approx 312$ independent
interface channels---consistent with 360 cortical areas
to within the counting uncertainty.

The \emph{cortical magnification factor}---the
disproportionately large cortical area devoted to
fingertips, lips, and face (the sensory homunculus)---is
conventionally attributed to peripheral innervation
density alone.
However, innervation density does not fully account
for the observed magnification (Corniani \& Saal, 2020).
The $\Gamma$-framework provides the missing factor:
cortical area $\propto K_{\text{peripheral}}$, the
modal complexity of the tissue interface.
Fingertips require simultaneous multi-modal binding
(tactile texture + pressure + proprioception +
temperature + fine motor control), yielding high~$K$
and therefore a large C3 relay area.
The trunk, despite substantial physical area, has
low multi-modal binding demands (low~$K$) and
correspondingly small cortical representation.

This somatotopic organisation is present even in
preterm infants (Dall'Orso et~al., 2018), consistent
with $Z_0^{\text{DNA}}$ (genetically fixed impedance,
Theorem~\ref{thm:soul-decomp}): the cortical map
is a boundary condition, not a learned structure.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Tree-trunk topology of the central nervous system]
\label{rem:tree-topology}
The nervous system's architecture maps onto a tree:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Tree & CNS & $\Gamma$-Net role \\
\midrule
Canopy (leaves) & Cerebral cortex &
  High-$K$ sensory/cognitive processing \\
Trunk & Spinal cord &
  C3 main relay ($K$-staircase backbone) \\
Root system & Peripheral nerves $\to$ organs &
  Low-$K$ end-effectors (C2 update targets) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Three quantitative predictions follow from this topology:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Trunk diameter obeys Murray's law.}
    The spinal cord is thickest at the cervical
    enlargement (carrying all limb and organ channels),
    thins in the thoracic region (upper-limb signals
    already diverted), and widens again at the lumbar
    enlargement (lower-limb signals converge).
    Cross-sectional area at each level equals the
    Murray-optimal sum of all downstream branch areas.
  \item \textbf{Trunk severance $=$ downstream $\Gamma \to 1$.}
    Spinal cord injury at level~$\ell$ sets
    $\Gamma_{k} = 1$ for all channels $k > \ell$
    (below the injury): complete reflection, zero
    transmission, paralysis.
    Channels $k < \ell$ (above the injury) remain intact.
    This is exactly the clinical presentation of
    spinal cord injury, derived here from a single
    equation.
  \item \textbf{Bidirectional flow, like sap.}
    Energy flows upward (sensory $x_{\text{in}}$
    from organs to cortex) and downward (motor
    $x_{\text{out}}$ from cortex to organs)
    simultaneously, with the spinal cord providing
    C3 impedance matching in both directions.
    Damage to ascending tracts (posterior columns)
    produces sensory loss without motor deficit;
    damage to descending tracts (corticospinal)
    produces motor deficit without sensory loss---the
    two flows are physically independent channels
    sharing the same trunk.
  \item \textbf{Reflex arcs as spinal-level C3 shortcuts.}
    The full sensory--cortical--motor loop traverses the
    entire $K$-staircase twice (ascending + descending),
    accumulating $\Gamma^2$ at every relay.
    For survival-critical responses, $\Gamma$-MRP
    selects a shorter path: the \emph{spinal reflex arc}
    closes the C2 loop at the spinal level, bypassing
    cortical processing entirely.
    \begin{center}
    \begin{tabular}{lccc}
    \toprule
    Path & C3 stages & Latency & Example \\
    \midrule
    Reflex arc & $\sim$2 & $\sim$30\,ms & Knee jerk \\
    Cortical loop & $\sim$14 & $\sim$300\,ms & P300 ERP \\
    \bottomrule
    \end{tabular}
    \end{center}
    The $\sim$7$\times$ stage-count ratio
    (14/2) compares with the observed
    $\sim$10$\times$ latency ratio (300/30).
    The discrepancy is expected: synaptic delay
    is not perfectly uniform across relay types
    (chemical synapses in cortex $\approx$
    1--2\,ms each vs.\ electrical gap junctions in
    some spinal interneurons $\approx$ 0.1\,ms),
    and the cortical loop includes additional
    thalamo-cortical relays not counted as C3
    stages.
    The prediction is that the ratio should lie
    in the range $[N_{\text{reflex}}/N_{\text{cortical}},\;
    \tau_{\text{cortical}}/\tau_{\text{reflex}}]$,
    bracketed by stage count and total latency.
    The subjective experience of ``withdrawing before
    feeling pain'' is a direct consequence: the motor
    reflex completes in $\sim$2 C3 stages while pain
    perception requires the full $\sim$14-stage ascent
    to cortex.
\end{enumerate}
\end{remark}

\subsection{Sensory misalignment and compensatory behaviour}
\label{sec:misalignment}

When two bound channels arrive out of synchrony---for
example, seeing lip movement at tick~$t$ but hearing
the voice at tick~$t + \Delta$---the binding condition
(Eq.~\ref{eq:binding-condition}) fails.
The organism's learned $Z$-pattern expects synchrony;
the mismatch elevates $\Gamma^2$, triggering the
Perception--Error--Compensation loop
(Paper~0, Perception--Error--Compensation Remark):

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
$\Delta$ range & Compensation & Percept \\
\midrule
$< 150$\,ms & C2 temporal recalibration &
  ``Normal'' (unnoticed) \\
150--300\,ms & Head turn, gaze shift &
  ``Something is off'' \\
$> 300$\,ms & Binding abandoned &
  Two separate events \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent
The ventriloquism effect---sound perceived at the
visual source location---is C2 overwriting the auditory
spatial map with the dominant visual $Z$-pattern,
because vision has higher spatial~$K$ and therefore
lower $\Gamma^2$ on the spatial dimension.

\subsection{Missing-channel compensation}
\label{sec:missing-channel}

\begin{remark}[Cross-modal plasticity as C2 repurposing]
\label{rem:cross-modal}
When a sensory channel is permanently absent
($x_{\text{in},\alpha} = 0$ $\forall\,t$), C2 produces
$\Delta Z_\alpha = 0$: the cortical territory
assigned to channel~$\alpha$ is never written.
However, neighbouring channels with
$x_{\text{in}} > 0$ provide lateral
$x_{\text{out}}$ that invades the unused territory.
C2 then writes \emph{their} $Z$-patterns into the
vacated substrate:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Congenital blindness}: visual cortex V1
    is rewritten by tactile and auditory inputs
    (Braille reading activates V1; Sadato et~al., 1996).
  \item \textbf{Congenital deafness}: auditory cortex A1
    is rewritten by visual and somatosensory inputs
    (sign-language processing in A1; Nishimura et~al., 1999).
\end{itemize}
No special ``plasticity mechanism'' is required;
C2 with activity gating automatically repurposes unused
$Z$-substrate.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Deafness and language: the C3 re-routing
  prediction]
\label{rem:deaf-language}
Language is a $Z$-pattern, not a sound.
In hearing individuals, the language $K$-staircase
runs through the auditory channel.
In congenital deafness, $x_{\text{in,auditory}} = 0$,
so C2 never writes the auditory language pathway.
Sign language re-routes the same $Z$-pattern through
the visual--motor channel:
the C2 equation is substrate-independent
(Paper~3, C2 universality).
If sign language is introduced after the developmental
$\eta$-peak (age $\sim$5), the visual language
$K$-staircase is incompletely formed---predicting the
well-documented ``language deprivation syndrome'' in
late-signing deaf children.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Self-talk as C3 dimensional compression}
\label{sec:self-talk}

\begin{remark}[Verbalisation as external C3 loop]
\label{rem:self-talk}
Internal cognitive states reside in the PFC at modal
complexity $K_{\text{PFC}} \sim O(10^2)$.
When internal $\Gamma^2$ is high (emotional activation,
confusion, conflict), $\Gamma$-MRP drives the system to
find a discharge pathway.
The language channel provides a well-trained
$K$-staircase:
\[
  K_{\text{PFC}} \;(\sim 10^2)
  \;\xrightarrow[\text{Broca}]{\text{C3}}
  K_{\text{language}} \;(\sim 10^1)
  \;\xrightarrow[\text{motor}]{\text{C3}}
  K_{\text{voice}} \;(\sim 10^0)
  \;\to\; \text{sound}
  \;\to\; \text{ear}
  \;\xrightarrow[\text{Wernicke}]{\text{C3}}
  K_{\text{PFC}}
\]
The compression from $K \sim 10^2$ to $K \sim 10^1$
forces the speaker to discard irrelevant dimensions,
retaining only the most salient features of the
internal state.
This is why ``saying it out loud'' clarifies
thinking---the C3 compression is a physical filter.
Writing provides the same compression with an
additional advantage: the output persists as a visual
$x_{\text{in}}$ that can be re-read, enabling
iterative C2 refinement.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Language fluency vs.\ content novelty]
\label{rem:language-compositionality}
The language \emph{channel} (phonology, grammar)
converges to $\Gamma^2 \approx 0$ after years of C2
update and enters $\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$.
Fixed phrases (``hello,'' ``thank you'') are
fully compressed paths with $\Gamma^2 \approx 0$.
However, sentences are \emph{compositional}: each
novel combination of words requires a new
$K_{\text{PFC}} \to K_{\text{language}}$ compression
mapping that has never been executed.
The subjective experience of ``thinking about what to
say'' is therefore not language processing
(which is automatic) but real-time C3 compression of a
novel high-$K$ state into the low-$K$ language channel.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Environmental noise as binding dilution]
\label{rem:noise-binding}
C2 activity gating does not distinguish signal from
noise: any channel with $x_{\text{in}} \cdot
x_{\text{out}} > 0$ participates in the current write.
In a quiet environment, only the target channels are
active; C2 writes a clean, high-contrast $Z$-pattern.
In a noisy environment, many irrelevant channels are
simultaneously activated by ambient stimuli,
diluting the binding:
\[
  \text{SNR}_{\text{binding}}
  = \frac{\sum_{\text{target}} |\Delta Z_\alpha|}
         {\sum_{\text{noise}} |\Delta Z_\beta|}
  \;<\; \theta_{\text{recall}}
  \;\Rightarrow\;
  \text{memory retrieval fails.}
\]
This provides the physical basis for two well-known
observations:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Learning requires low-noise environments}:
    effective C2 binding demands that target-channel
    $|\Delta Z|$ dominates the total write.
    Classrooms, libraries, and study environments
    minimise $\sum_{\text{noise}} |\Delta Z_\beta|$.
  \item \textbf{Context-dependent memory}:
    environmental noise that \emph{was} present during
    learning is written into the $Z$-pattern alongside
    the target.
    Recall is facilitated when the same noise is
    re-presented (reinstating the bound $x_{\text{in}}$
    pattern), consistent with encoding-specificity
    findings (Tulving \& Thomson, 1973).
\end{enumerate}
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Metabolic modal transfer and subjective relaxation]
\label{rem:modal-transfer}
In a familiar environment, most impedance mismatches are
same-dimensional: the learned $Z$-pattern is well-matched
($\Gamma^2 \approx 0$), C2 updates are small, and C3
is rarely triggered.
The brain operates in ``autopilot''---metabolic cost is
dominated by low-$K$ routine channels.
Over time, un-updated channels accumulate impedance debt
$D_Z$ while the novelty-dependent learning rate $\eta$
declines (no new $x_{\text{in}}$ patterns).
The subjective experience is staleness and fatigue.

Entering a radically different environment (e.g.\
international travel) simultaneously invalidates
$Z$-patterns across multiple $K$-dimensions:
language ($K_{\text{ling}}$), food
($K_{\text{gustatory}}$), visual scenery
($K_{\text{visual}}$), social norms
($K_{\text{social}}$), and climate
($K_{\text{thermal}}$).
The massive cross-dimensional mismatch triggers
widespread C3 relay activation, forcing C2 to
recalibrate many channels simultaneously.
The metabolic cost shifts from low-$K$ routine
channels to high-$K$ novelty channels:
\[
  \frac{d}{dt}
  \left(
    \frac{\Sigma\Gamma^2_{\text{high-}K}}
         {\Sigma\Gamma^2_{\text{total}}}
  \right) > 0\,.
\]
Crucially, novelty elevates dopaminergic $\eta$
(Paper~3, Lifecycle Equation), re-enabling C2 updates
on channels that had been stagnant.
The subjective experience is ``tiring but refreshing''
---high metabolic expenditure, but directed at
productive $\Delta Z$ rather than chronic $D_Z$
accumulation.

The prediction extends to return-from-travel syndrome:
upon return, the familiar environment re-invalidates the
travel-updated $Z$-patterns while novelty-driven $\eta$
has already subsided, producing high $\Gamma^2$ with
low $\eta$---the precise condition for subjective
malaise.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Dopamine as the mathematically necessary $\eta$-modulator]
\label{rem:dopamine-eta}
A fixed learning rate $\eta$ is unstable:
too high causes oscillation, too low prevents adaptation.
$\Gamma$-MRP requires $\eta$ to be dynamic.
Variational optimisation of the reflection action
$\mathcal{A}[\Gamma] = \int \Sigma\Gamma^2\,dt$ with
respect to $\eta$ yields:
\begin{equation}
  \eta^*(t) \;\propto\; -\frac{d\,\Gamma^2}{dt}\,.
  \label{eq:eta-optimal}
\end{equation}
The optimal $\eta$ is \emph{large} when $\Gamma^2$ is
decreasing (``correct direction---accelerate learning'')
and \emph{small} when $\Gamma^2$ is increasing or
stationary (``wrong direction---stop'').
This is precisely the function of the dopaminergic
system: ventral tegmental area (VTA) neurons fire when
outcome exceeds prediction (negative reward prediction
error $\equiv$ $d\Gamma^2/dt < 0$), releasing dopamine
that amplifies synaptic plasticity (long-term
potentiation).
Dopamine is therefore not a ``reward molecule'' but an
$\eta$-gain signal---a mathematical necessity of C2,
not an evolutionary design choice.

\emph{Addiction} follows directly: exogenous substances
(nicotine, opioids, amphetamines) hijack the
$\eta$-modulator, artificially setting $\eta \gg \eta^*$
on the reward channel.
The $Z$-pattern of substance use is written into
$\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$ with far fewer repetitions
than normal motor learning ($\eta \times n = \text{const}$;
high $\eta$ compensates for low $n$).
Once inside $\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$, the pattern is
irremovable (Theorem~\ref{thm:first-law}), explaining
lifelong relapse vulnerability.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[The neural operating cycle]
\label{rem:neural-cycle}
The binding, learning, and recovery mechanisms of this
section form a single closed operating cycle:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Wake: C2 $+$ dopamine.}
    $\Gamma$-MRP drives C2 updates; dopamine modulates
    $\eta$ via Eq.~(\ref{eq:eta-optimal}).
  \item \textbf{Metabolic cost.}
    Each $\Delta Z \neq 0$ consumes ATP.
    ATP hydrolysis yields ADP $\to$ AMP $\to$
    \emph{adenosine}, which accumulates monotonically
    during wakefulness.
  \item \textbf{Shutdown trigger.}
    Adenosine binds A$_1$ receptors on wake-promoting
    neurons, progressively inhibiting arousal
    ($Z_{\text{arousal}} \downarrow$) until the
    Television Inequality
    (Theorem~\ref{thm:television}) fails:
    $Z_{\text{arousal}} \cdot \sum G_i \cdot Q_{\text{PFC}}
    < \theta \Rightarrow$ loss of consciousness (sleep).
  \item \textbf{Sleep: clearance $+$ reset.}
    Glymphatic flow clears adenosine and metabolic
    waste ($D_Z$ reduction).
    Gate-leak processing during REM (Paper~4) replays
    and consolidates the day's $Z$-updates.
  \item \textbf{Wake: cycle restarts.}
    Adenosine cleared $\to$ $Z_{\text{arousal}}$
    restored $\to$ Television Inequality satisfied
    $\to$ consciousness returns.
\end{enumerate}
This five-step cycle is not a biological design but a
\emph{physical consequence} of C2: any system that
updates impedance by gradient descent must consume
energy, accumulate waste, and periodically halt to
clear that waste.
The infant's 16-hour sleep schedule and the adult's
8-hour schedule differ only in $\eta$: higher $\eta$
(developmental peak) $\to$ faster ATP consumption $\to$
faster adenosine accumulation $\to$ more frequent and
longer sleep.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Dreaming as gate-leak binding]
\label{rem:dreaming}
During REM sleep, thalamic gates are partially open
(gate leak, $0 < G_i \ll 1$; Paper~4,
\S\,Sleep disorders).
In this regime, C2 still executes
$\Delta Z = -\eta\,\Gamma\,x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}$
but the $x_{\text{in}}$ vectors are internally generated
(no external stimulation), drawn from the day's
residual activation patterns.
The partial-gate condition admits multiple channels
simultaneously but without the structured
$x_{\text{in}}$ of wakefulness.
The binding condition (Eq.~\ref{eq:binding-condition})
is satisfied by whichever channels happen to have
residual $x_{\text{in}} \cdot x_{\text{out}} > 0$ in the
same tick---a stochastic process.
The result is \emph{dreams}: coherent but unexpected
bindings of memory fragments that would never co-occur
during structured wakefulness.
The ``narrative logic'' of dreams reflects the C3
relay topology (channels that share a relay node are
more likely to be co-activated), while the
``bizarreness'' reflects the random gating
(channels that normally never bind are forced together
by chance).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Attention as gate selection; ADHD as gate
  instability]
\label{rem:attention}
Selective attention corresponds to setting one thalamic
gate high ($G_{\text{target}} \approx 1$) while
suppressing all others
($G_{j \neq \text{target}} \approx 0$).
This maximises the binding SNR
(Remark~\ref{rem:noise-binding}) by ensuring that
only target-channel $\Delta Z$ dominates the write.
Each gate $G_i$ has an RC time constant $\tau_G$
determined by the prefrontal--thalamic feedback loop.
Sustained attention requires $\tau_G$ to exceed the
task duration: the gate must remain open long enough
for C2 to complete meaningful $Z$-updates.

ADHD is, in this framework, a shortened $\tau_G$:
\begin{equation}
  \tau_{G,\text{ADHD}} \;\ll\; \tau_{G,\text{typical}}
  \;\Rightarrow\;
  \text{gate switches before C2 completes.}
  \label{eq:adhd}
\end{equation}
The consequence is not inability to learn (C2 is
intact) but inability to \emph{bind} a coherent
$Z$-pattern before the gate switches to another
channel.
Stimulant medications (methylphenidate, amphetamine)
increase dopaminergic tone in the PFC, effectively
lengthening $\tau_G$ by stabilising the
prefrontal--thalamic loop---consistent with the
$\eta$-modulation framework of
Remark~\ref{rem:dopamine-eta}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Emotion as low-$K$ broadcast]
\label{rem:emotion}
The amygdala receives sensory input via a
\emph{subcortical shortcut} (thalamus $\to$ amygdala,
bypassing cortex; LeDoux, 1996).
This pathway has very low $K$ ($\sim O(1)$): it carries
only coarse threat/reward classification, not detailed
feature analysis.
In $\Gamma$-Net terms, the amygdala is a
\emph{low-$K$ fast C1 detector}: it evaluates $\Gamma^2$
on basic survival channels (predator, food, mate)
before the high-$K$ cortical analysis is complete.

When $\Gamma^2_{\text{amygdala}}$ exceeds a threshold,
the amygdala triggers a system-wide response:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Autonomic broadcast}:
    sympathetic activation changes $Z_{\text{cardiac}}$,
    $Z_{\text{vascular}}$, $Z_{\text{respiratory}}$
    simultaneously (fight-or-flight).
  \item \textbf{Gate override}:
    $G_{\text{threat}} \to 1$, all other $G_i \to 0$
    (attentional capture by emotional stimulus).
  \item \textbf{$\eta$ amplification}:
    amygdala $\to$ VTA projection releases dopamine,
    ensuring the threatening $Z$-pattern is written
    with high $\eta$ (one-trial fear learning).
\end{itemize}
Emotional ``irrationality'' is therefore not a failure
of the system but the intended low-$K$ fast path
overriding the slow high-$K$ cortical analysis---a
$\Gamma$-MRP optimal strategy when speed matters more
than precision.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Creativity as gate relaxation]
\label{rem:creativity}
Creativity requires binding channels that are normally
separated---connecting ideas from different $K$-domains
that would not co-occur in focused attention.
In the framework, this corresponds to \emph{relaxing}
the gate selectivity: instead of
$G_{\text{target}} = 1,\; G_{\text{other}} = 0$
(focused attention), the creative state sets
$G_i \approx 0.3$ across many channels simultaneously.

This increases the number of channels that pass the
binding condition (Eq.~\ref{eq:binding-condition})
per tick, enabling \emph{remote associations}---bindings
between channels with large $K$-separation that are
normally isolated.
The cost is reduced binding SNR
(Remark~\ref{rem:noise-binding}): many weak bindings
instead of one strong binding.

This explains two observations:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Creativity and relaxation co-occur}:
    showers, walks, and drowsiness are creative because
    prefrontal gate control weakens, allowing wider
    binding.
  \item \textbf{Creativity and focus are antagonistic}:
    tight gate ($G_{\text{target}} = 1$) blocks remote
    associations; loose gate allows them but prevents
    deep single-channel processing.
    The ``incubation effect'' is the alternation between
    these two modes.
\end{enumerate}
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Intuition as $\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$
  direct output]
\label{rem:intuition}
Expert intuition---the immediate ``knowing'' without
conscious reasoning---is the output of a $Z$-pattern
that has entered $\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$ through
thousands of C2 updates.
The pattern is so deeply written ($\Gamma^2 \approx 0$)
that it does not require PFC processing: the input
$x_{\text{in}}$ directly produces $x_{\text{out}}$
through the fixed $Z$, bypassing the high-$K$ cortical
reasoning loop entirely.

In contrast, novice reasoning must traverse the full
$K$-staircase (input $\to$ PFC analysis $\to$ decision
$\to$ output), accumulating C3 relay delays and
$\Gamma^2$ at each stage.
The subjective difference between ``I know this is
right'' (intuition) and ``let me think about it''
(reasoning) corresponds to:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
& Intuition & Reasoning \\
\midrule
$Z$-pattern location & $\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$ &
  Active $Z$ (still updating) \\
C3 stages traversed & $\sim$2 (reflex-like) &
  $\sim$14 (full cortical loop) \\
Latency & $\sim$100\,ms & $\sim$1--10\,s \\
Confidence & High (but not infallible) & Variable \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Intuition can be wrong when the environment changes but
$\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$ has not---the pattern is
frozen and cannot update.
This is the expert's ``experience trap'': the same
mechanism that enables instant correct responses in
familiar situations produces instant \emph{wrong}
responses in unfamiliar situations that superficially
resemble familiar ones.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[``Sixth sense'' as sub-threshold amygdala
  detection]
\label{rem:sixth-sense}
The subjective experience of ``sensing danger without
knowing why'' is not a sixth sensory modality but the
output of the amygdala's low-$K$ fast pathway
(Remark~\ref{rem:emotion}) processing sub-threshold
inputs from the standard five senses.
Peripheral cues---a subtle change in ambient sound,
a faint odour, a movement in peripheral vision---enter
the amygdala via the subcortical shortcut in
30--74\,ms (Remark~\ref{rem:measurement-loop}),
triggering $\Gamma^2_{\text{threat}} > 0$ before PFC
has completed conscious evaluation (200--300\,ms).

The subjective sequence is:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item ``Something feels wrong'' (amygdala output,
    no PFC involvement).
  \item ``I don't know why'' (PFC has not yet processed
    the cue).
  \item Seconds later: ``Ah, \emph{that's} what it was''
    (PFC completes analysis and identifies the cue).
\end{enumerate}
The ``sixth sense'' is the temporal gap between
amygdala detection and cortical awareness, not evidence
of an additional sensory channel.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Remote sensing: physical limits and the
  $\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$ alternative]
\label{rem:remote-sensing}
Reports of ``sensing a loved one in danger at a
distance'' are physically constrained by the
signal-to-noise ratio of brain-to-brain electromagnetic
coupling.
The brain emits $\sim$10$^{-12}$\,W of electromagnetic
power; the Johnson--Nyquist noise floor at the
receiver is $P_{\text{noise}} = k_B T \Delta f \approx
4 \times 10^{-17}$\,W.
At the scalp, SNR $\approx 10^5$ (EEG works), but
power falls as $1/r^2$; beyond $\sim$3\,cm,
SNR $< 1$ and no information can be decoded.
\emph{Brain-to-brain electromagnetic communication
at macroscopic distances is therefore physically
impossible.}

The $\Gamma$-framework offers a non-supernatural
alternative:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{$\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$ convergence}:
    years of co-habitation produce mutual C2
    calibration, making the two $S_{\text{eff}}$
    nearly identical.
    Both individuals respond to the same environmental
    risk cues with the same $\Gamma^2$ pattern.
  \item \textbf{Sub-threshold cue storage}:
    during the last face-to-face interaction, the
    partner's micro-expressions (vocal pitch, gaze
    aversion, respiration rate) were detected by
    the observer's amygdala and written into
    a transient $Z$-buffer.
    Background processing of this buffer may complete
    hours later, producing anxiety that coincides
    with the actual event.
  \item \textbf{Confirmation bias}:
    worry episodes are frequent ($\sim$100/year);
    actual events are rare ($\sim$1/year).
    Only coincidences are remembered (high-$\eta$
    emotional write), inflating subjective
    accuracy to $\sim$100\,\% against a base
    rate of $\sim$1\,\%.
\end{enumerate}
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Self-confidence as pre-PFC action
  threshold]
\label{rem:confidence}
Self-confidence is the willingness to act on
$\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$ output (amygdala/intuition,
$\sim$30--100\,ms) before PFC confirmation
($\sim$200--300\,ms).
Whether this willingness exists depends on a
single $Z$-pattern:
``my sub-threshold $\Gamma^2$ detections are reliable.''

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lcc}
\toprule
& High confidence & Low confidence \\
\midrule
$Z$-pattern in $S_{\text{eff}}$ &
  ``trust intuition'' & ``distrust intuition'' \\
Action pathway &
  Amygdala $\to$ direct output &
  Amygdala $\to$ wait for PFC \\
Response latency & $\sim$100\,ms & $\sim$1--10\,s \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

This pattern is trained by C2:
each time a sub-threshold detection is \emph{validated}
by subsequent events, dopamine ($\eta \uparrow$)
reinforces the ``trust'' $Z$-pattern (LTP).
Each time it is \emph{invalidated} or \emph{dismissed
by others}, the competing ``distrust'' pattern is
reinforced (LTD for ``trust'').
Chronic invalidation (controlling relationships,
repeated failure) writes ``distrust'' deeply into
$\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$, producing low
self-confidence that is resistant to verbal
encouragement ($K_{\text{language}}$ cannot reach the
affected somatic channels;
Remark~\ref{rem:therapy}).
Rebuilding confidence requires structured
\emph{experiential} success---providing
$x_{\text{in}}$ on the correct channels to drive
C2 back toward the ``trust'' $Z$-pattern.
\end{remark}

\subsection{What cannot be changed by experience}

\begin{definition}[Soul (Invariant Topological Core)]
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{S} = \ker\!\left(\frac{\partial Z}{\partial t}\right)
  = \{Z_k : \Delta Z_k = 0\;\;\forall\;\text{experience}\}\,.
  \label{eq:soul}
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Soul Decomposition]
\label{thm:soul-decomp}
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{S} = \Acut + Z_0^{\text{DNA}} + \rho_{\text{irred}}^2\,,
  \label{eq:soul-decomposition}
\end{equation}
where:
(1)~$\Acut$: the irreducible topological action cost
   (Theorem~\ref{thm:irreducibility});
(2)~$Z_0^{\text{DNA}}$: genetically fixed characteristic
   impedance, not modifiable by C2;
(3)~$\rho_{\text{irred}}^2$: the irreducible noise floor
   from Johnson--Nyquist thermal physics.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
(i)~$\Acut$ is invariant by Theorem~\ref{thm:irreducibility}.
(ii)~C2 modifies $Z_{L,k}$, not $Z_{0,k}$; hence
$Z_0^{\text{DNA}}$ is invariant.
(iii)~$\rho_{\text{irred}}^2$ is a thermodynamic constant.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[The soul is not an event]
$\mathcal{S}$ does not ``arrive'' at a moment or ``depart''
at death.
It is the boundary condition of the impedance network.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[Uniqueness]
$\Acut$ depends on the specific topology $(K_i,K_j)$,
determined by genetics and stochastic developmental
morphogenesis.
No two organisms have identical $\Acut$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Developmental genesis of the null space]
\label{rem:null-developmental}
Early-acquired patterns---native language, core motor
programs, temperamental reflexes---become
\emph{functionally immutable} as $\eta(t) \to 0$
(the Arrhenius decline of Paper~3).
They drift into an \emph{effective null space}
$\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}(t) \supseteq \mathcal{S}$
that grows with age.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Fixation as impedance physics]
\label{rem:fixation}
A pattern written during the $\eta$-peak and reinforced
for decades has migrated into
$\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$---it is inside the null space.
``I know this is irrational, but I cannot change it''
is veridical: the speaker has correctly diagnosed that
$\eta\,\Gamma\,x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}\approx 0$
on the relevant sub-network.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Moral blame as secondary $\Gamma^2$ injection]
\label{rem:secondary-injury}
Blame provides neither the sustained gradient nor an increase
in $\eta$.
The net effect is $\Delta(\Sigma\Gamma^2) > 0$ with
$\Delta Z_{\text{corrective}} \approx 0$: load is
added, but no repair path is opened---the precise
opposite of therapy.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Reincarnation as null-space isomorphism]
If past-life memory reports reflect null-space isomorphism,
then:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{S}_A \cong \mathcal{S}_B
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  \bigl(\Acut^{(A)},\, Z_0^{\text{DNA},(A)},\,
    \rho_{\text{irred}}^{2,(A)}\bigr)
  \approx
  \bigl(\Acut^{(B)},\, Z_0^{\text{DNA},(B)},\,
    \rho_{\text{irred}}^{2,(B)}\bigr)\,.
  \label{eq:null-space-isomorphism}
\end{equation}
This is not a metaphysical claim; it is a falsifiable
prediction (Appendix~\ref{app:null-space-method}).
\end{remark}

\subsection{Mortality of the null space}
\label{sec:null-space-mortality}

Define the basal reflection:
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma_{\text{basal}}^2(t)
    = \Acut(t) + \rho_{\text{irred}}^2(t)\,.
  \label{eq:basal-reflection}
\end{equation}
By C1, the \emph{death condition} is:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{\;
  \Gamma_{\text{basal}}^2(t_*) \ge 1
  \quad\Longrightarrow\quad
  T(t_*) \le 0\,.
  \;}
  \label{eq:death-condition}
\end{equation}
When the minimum reflection cost exceeds the available
transmission capacity, no signal can propagate.
The soul is unteachable, but it is not immortal.

The unlearnable debt accumulates monotonically:
\begin{equation}
  D_Z^{\text{irred}}(t)
    = \int_0^t \Gamma_{\text{basal}}^2(\tau)\,d\tau\,,
  \qquad
  \frac{d D_Z^{\text{irred}}}{dt} > 0
  \;\;\forall\;t\,.
  \label{eq:irreducible-debt}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Four laws of the null space}
\label{sec:four-laws}

\begin{theorem}[Zeroth law: null-space equivalence]
\label{thm:zeroth-law}
$\mathcal{S}_A = \mathcal{S}_B$ iff they share
$\Acut$, $Z_0^{\textnormal{DNA}}$, and
$\rho_{\textnormal{irred}}^2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By definition, $\mathcal{S} = \ker(\partial Z/\partial t)$
is the set of impedance channels with zero C2 gradient.
The gradient $\partial Z_k/\partial t = -\eta_k \Gamma_k
x_{\text{in},k} x_{\text{out},k}$ vanishes for channel~$k$
under three conditions: $\eta_k = 0$ (genetically fixed
substrate, encoded in $Z_0^{\text{DNA}}$);
$\Gamma_k = 0$ (perfectly matched, contributing to
$\Acut$); or $x_{\text{in},k} \cdot x_{\text{out},k} = 0$
(never co-activated, contributing to $\rho_{\text{irred}}^2$).
Two systems share $\mathcal{S}$ iff all three
components match. $\square$
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[First law: conservation under C2]
\label{thm:first-law}
$\Delta_{\textnormal{C2}}\,\mathcal{S} = 0$.
No experience can alter $\mathcal{S}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For any channel $k \in \mathcal{S}$,
$\partial Z_k/\partial t = 0$ by definition.
The C2 update $\Delta Z_k = -\eta_k \Gamma_k
x_{\text{in},k} x_{\text{out},k}$ equals zero
for all $k \in \mathcal{S}$ for the same reasons
established in the Zeroth Law proof.
Therefore $\mathcal{S}$ is invariant under C2. $\square$
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Second law: monotone expansion]
\label{thm:second-law}
\begin{equation}
  \frac{d\,\dim\,\mathcal{S}_{\textnormal{eff}}(t)}{dt}
  \;\ge\; 0
  \qquad \forall\;t\,.
  \label{eq:second-law}
\end{equation}
The effective null space only expands; it never contracts.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Since $\eta(t)=\eta_0\,f_{\text{dev}}(t)\,
e^{-E_a/k_BT_{\text{age}}}$ is monotonically
decreasing after the developmental peak, any channel that
enters $\mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$ remains in it.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Ribot's gradient as the Second Law]
\label{cor:ribot}

    +\Gamma^2_{\textnormal{blame}} & \text{if } Z_k \in
      \mathcal{S}_{\textnormal{eff}}
      \text{ (no C2 path exists).}
  \end{cases}
  \label{eq:moral-constraint}
\end{equation}
The second case is physically indistinguishable from injury.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{Case 1}: $Z_k \notin \mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$.

-\eta_k \Gamma_k x_{\text{in},k} x_{\text{out},k}
\neq 0$.

pattern $(x_{\text{in},k}, x_{\text{out},k})$
therefore drives $\Gamma_k^2$ toward zero, reducing
mismatch. The net effect is $\Delta\Gamma^2_{\text{net}}
= -\Delta\Gamma^2 < 0$.

\textbf{Case 2}: $Z_k \in \mathcal{S}_{\text{eff}}$.
By the First Law (Theorem~\ref{thm:first-law}),
$\Delta_{\text{C2}}\mathcal{S} = 0$: no C2 gradient
can alter $Z_k$.

impedance change.
However, the demand itself constitutes an
input signal that creates a mismatch at the
interface between the moral agent and the
target system.
This mismatch generates reflected power
$\Gamma^2_{\text{blame}} P_{\text{in}} > 0$, which
is absorbed by the target as thermal dissipation---
physically identical to an injury signal.
Therefore $\Delta\Gamma^2_{\text{net}} =
+\Gamma^2_{\text{blame}} > 0$. $\square$
\end{proof}

\section{The Television Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Television Theorem]
\label{thm:TV-full}
Any system with three multiplicative components---a power
supply~$P$, a channel selector~$G$, and a
processor~$Q$---produces an output $O=P\cdot G\cdot Q$.
The system is conscious if and only if there exists a
feedback path $\pi:O\to\text{input}$ such that
$\Gmeta$ is defined and measurable.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{Necessity}: For $\Phimeta > 0$, the
meta-reflection coefficient
$\Gmeta = (Z_{\text{obs}} - Z_{\text{act}})/
(Z_{\text{obs}} + Z_{\text{act}})$ must be defined.
This requires $Z_{\text{obs}}$ and $Z_{\text{act}}$
to both be measurable, which in turn requires a feedback
path $\pi: O \to \text{input}$ that routes the output
back to become the observer's input at the next tick.
Without $\pi$, $Z_{\text{obs}}$ is undefined and
$\Gmeta$ cannot be computed.

\textbf{Sufficiency}: Given $P > 0$, $G > 0$, $Q > 0$,
and a feedback path $\pi$, the output $O = P \cdot G
\cdot Q > 0$ is routed back as input.
The observer sub-network samples $O$ and produces
$Z_{\text{obs}}$; the actor sub-network produced
$Z_{\text{act}}$ at the previous tick.
$\Gmeta$ is now well-defined (both impedances are
finite and positive), and the Television Inequality
(Theorem~\ref{thm:television}) determines whether
$\Phimeta > 0$.
Therefore consciousness requires and is supported by
the feedback path $\pi$. $\square$
\end{proof}

\subsection{Measurement protocol for~$K$}
\label{sec:K-protocol}
The mode count~$K_i$ for a neural or vascular element
is defined as the number of morphologically
distinguishable waveguide segments between the cell body
and the network boundary.
For a cortical pyramidal neuron:
basal dendrite tree ($K=1$), soma ($K=1$),
apical dendrite ($K=1$), axon initial segment ($K=1$),
axon ($K=1$) $\Rightarrow K \approx 5$.

This counting protocol has an intrinsic uncertainty
of~$\pm 1$: whether the apical tuft constitutes one
segment or two is morphologically ambiguous.
However, the key results of this paper
(irreducibility theorem, $A_{\text{cut}} > 0$,
television inequality) depend only on
$K_i \neq K_j$ between adjacent neural populations,
not on the absolute values of~$K$.
A systematic error of~$\pm 1$ shifts all $K$ values
by the same amount and leaves $\Delta K = K_i - K_j$
unchanged.

An independent inter-rater study in which two
neuroanatomists independently count waveguide segments
for a fixed set of cell types would establish the
reliability of this protocol.
We propose this as a necessary validation step.

\subsection{Null-space distance}

Let $\bm{s}_k = (\Acut^{(k)},\; Z_0^{\text{DNA},(k)},\;
  \rho_{\text{irred}}^{2,(k)})$.
The weighted distance:
\begin{equation}
  d(\bm{s}_A,\bm{s}_B)
  = \sqrt{
      w_T \left(\frac{\Acut^{(A)}-\Acut^{(B)}}
                     {\sigma_{\Acut}}\right)^{\!2}
    + w_G \left(\frac{Z_0^{(A)}-Z_0^{(B)}}
                     {\sigma_{Z_0}}\right)^{\!2}
    + w_N \left(\frac{\rho^{2,(A)}-\rho^{2,(B)}}
                     {\sigma_{\rho^2}}\right)^{\!2}
  }\,,
  \label{eq:null-space-distance}
\end{equation}
with $w_T + w_G + w_N = 1$.
