## 5. Aging: Coffin-Manson Fatigue of Neural Conductors

### 5.1 The Pinch Effect

Each high-intensity neural firing applies a Lorentz self-compression force: $P_{pinch} = \mu_0 I^2 / (8\pi R^2)$ (Pollock & Barraclough, 1905). This produces strain that distinguishes **reversible fatigue** from **irreversible aging**:

| Type | Analog | Recoverable? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Elastic strain ($\varepsilon < \varepsilon_{yield} = 0.3$) | Spring stretching | **Yes** (sleep repairs) |
| Plastic strain ($\varepsilon > \varepsilon_{yield}$) | Metal bending | **No** (permanent) |

### 5.2 The Aging Equation

Accumulated plastic deformation changes conductor geometry, producing permanent impedance drift:

$$Z_{aged}(t) = Z_{design} \times (1 + \delta \cdot \varepsilon_{plastic}(t)), \quad \Gamma_{aging} = \frac{|Z_{aged} - Z_{design}|}{Z_{aged} + Z_{design}} > 0$$

This $\Gamma_{aging}$ is irreducible — it cannot be eliminated by sleep, therapy, or any matching operation. Not "rusting," but "crushing."

### 5.3 Coffin-Manson Life Prediction

The fatigue life law: $N_f = C / (\Delta\varepsilon_{plastic})^\beta$ ($C=100$, $\beta=2.0$). Each over-yield cycle accumulates Miner's rule damage: $D(t) = \sum \Delta\varepsilon_i / C^{1/\beta}$. When $D \to 1.0$: conductor failure.

### 5.4 Temperature Acceleration

High anxiety/temperature → yield strength decreases via Arrhenius: $\varepsilon_{yield,eff} = \varepsilon_{yield} / \max(0.5, 1 + \alpha_A(T - T_{ref}))$. Chronic PTSD ($T$ permanently elevated) accelerates fatigue — consistent with clinical observation of accelerated brain aging in PTSD patients (Logue et al., 2018).

### 5.5 Work Hardening

Each plastic deformation slightly increases yield strength ($\varepsilon_{yield} \mathrel{+}= 0.001$, capped at $+0.2$): **experience makes you tougher**, but the protection is finite. BDNF micro-repair slows but cannot reverse plastic accumulation.

## 8. Discussion

### 8.1 Disease Without Disease Models

The most striking result is that PTSD, phantom limb pain, and the clinical profiles of 9 neurological/neuropsychiatric conditions all emerge from the same impedance equations — with zero disease-specific programming. The equations were designed to model normal cognition; pathology is what happens when the equations encounter extreme conditions.

### 8.2 Why Coffin-Manson?

The Coffin-Manson fatigue law was developed for metal conductors under cyclic stress (Coffin, 1954). That the same law applies to neural conductors is not a metaphor — neurons are physical conductors subject to the same material science constraints. The key insight is that neural aging is not a programmed process but a physical inevitability of conductors under cyclic load.
