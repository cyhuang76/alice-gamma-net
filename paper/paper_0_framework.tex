\begin{abstract}
We derive the complete axiomatic framework of the
$\Gamma$-Net theory from a single physical origin:
the First Law of Thermodynamics applied to
one-dimensional interfaces.
Three constraints emerge as \emph{theorems}, not postulates:
C1 (energy conservation: $\Gamma^2 + T = 1$),
C2 (coefficient update: $\Delta Z = -\eta\,\Gamma\,
x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}$),
and C3 (dimensional relay: $K$-staircase necessity).
C2 is proven to be the \emph{unique} first-order
activity-gated update rule that minimises reflected energy.
We provide a complete inventory of derived quantities
and a theorem directory mapping the deductive chain
across five companion papers:
Paper~1 (existence and irreducibility),
Paper~2 (geometric quantification and metabolic scaling),
Paper~3 (temporal unification and remodeling equivalence),
Paper~4 (topological pathology),
Paper~5 (full-system verification).
This paper contains no new results beyond the derivation
itself; it serves as the master reference for the series.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The $\Gamma$-Net framework describes biological
organisation as impedance physics.
Every interface between two biological media---synapse,
blood vessel wall, bone--cartilage junction, cell
membrane, skin surface---is characterised by an
impedance mismatch that determines how much energy
is transmitted versus reflected.

The framework rests on three constraints, C1--C3,
which are not modelling choices but consequences of
the First Law of Thermodynamics.
This paper derives all three from first principles
and proves the uniqueness of the central remodeling
law (C2).

Five companion papers develop the consequences:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Paper~1}~\cite{Paper1}:
    Proves that any finite network \emph{must} have

\section{$\Gamma$ as Thermodynamic Necessity}
\label{sec:gamma}

\subsection{Impedance at a one-dimensional interface}

Consider any one-dimensional interface separating two
media with generalised impedances $Z_1$ and $Z_2$,
where impedance is defined as the ratio of effort
(voltage, pressure, force, temperature) to flow
(current, volume flow, velocity, heat flux):
\begin{equation}
  Z_k = \frac{\text{effort}_k}{\text{flow}_k}\,.
  \label{eq:Z-def}
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}[$\Gamma$ universality]
\label{thm:gamma}
At any one-dimensional interface between media of
impedance $Z_1$ and $Z_2$, the First Law of
Thermodynamics requires that incident power be
partitioned into reflected and transmitted fractions:
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma = \frac{Z_2 - Z_1}{Z_2 + Z_1}\,,
  \qquad
  T = 1 - \Gamma^2 = \frac{4 Z_1 Z_2}{(Z_1 + Z_2)^2}\,,
  \label{eq:gamma-def}
\end{equation}
where $\Gamma$ is the reflection coefficient and $T$
the transmission coefficient.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
At a lossless one-dimensional interface, conservation
of energy (First Law) requires:
$P_{\text{incident}} = P_{\text{reflected}} +
P_{\text{transmitted}}$.
The boundary conditions on effort and flow
(continuity of effort, conservation of flow)
uniquely determine the reflected fraction as
$|\Gamma|^2 = |(Z_2-Z_1)/(Z_2+Z_1)|^2$.
This derivation applies identically to electrical
transmission lines, acoustic waveguides, hydraulic
conduits, mechanical interfaces, and thermal
boundaries---the mathematical structure depends only
on the existence of effort and flow variables, not
on the physical medium.
\end{proof}

\noindent
Theorem~\ref{thm:gamma} is not a modelling choice.
Any system with two impedances meeting at an interface
\emph{must} obey this partition.
The reflection coefficient $\Gamma$ is as fundamental
as temperature or pressure: it exists wherever two
impedances meet.

\subsection{The Minimum Reflection Principle}

For an organism with $N$ interface channels, the total
reflected (wasted) power is:
\begin{equation}
  P_{\text{waste}} = \sum_{i=1}^{N} \Gamma_i^2\,
  P_{\text{in},i}\,.
  \label{eq:waste}
\end{equation}
An organism is \emph{thermodynamically viable} if and
only if
\begin{equation}
  P_{\text{metabolic}} > P_{\text{waste}}\,,
  \label{eq:viability}
\end{equation}
i.e., its metabolic power production exceeds its
reflection losses.
Organisms that violate Eq.~\eqref{eq:viability} die
by energy deficit---no natural-selection argument
is required.

The Minimum Reflection Principle (MRP) is therefore
not an optimality \emph{assumption} but a thermodynamic
\emph{viability condition}: organisms that waste too
much energy at their interfaces fail by physics alone.

Define the \textbf{reflection action} over lifetime~$T$:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{
  \mathcal{A}[\Gamma]
  = \int_0^T \sum_i \Gamma_i^2(t)\,P_{\text{in},i}(t)\,dt
  \;\to\;\min\,.
  }
  \label{eq:action}
\end{equation}
This is the variational principle from which all three
constraints are derived.

\section{The Three Constraints}
\label{sec:constraints}

\subsection{C1: Energy Conservation}
\label{sec:c1}

\begin{theorem}[C1]
\label{thm:c1}
At every interface $i$, at every instant $t$:
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma_i^2(t) + T_i(t) = 1\,,
  \label{eq:c1}
\end{equation}
where $T_i = 1 - \Gamma_i^2$ is the transmission
coefficient.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Theorem~\ref{thm:gamma}, at any lossless
one-dimensional interface the reflected power fraction
is $|\Gamma_i|^2$ and the transmitted fraction is
$T_i = 4Z_1 Z_2/(Z_1+Z_2)^2$.
Direct substitution of
$\Gamma_i = (Z_2-Z_1)/(Z_2+Z_1)$ yields:
\begin{equation}
  \Gamma_i^2 + T_i
  = \frac{(Z_2-Z_1)^2}{(Z_2+Z_1)^2}
  + \frac{4Z_1 Z_2}{(Z_2+Z_1)^2}
  = \frac{(Z_2-Z_1)^2 + 4Z_1 Z_2}{(Z_2+Z_1)^2}
  = \frac{(Z_2+Z_1)^2}{(Z_2+Z_1)^2}
  = 1\,. \quad \square
\end{equation}
Dissipative corrections modify $Z_i(t)$ over time
(Johnson--Nyquist noise, Arrhenius aging) but at each
instant the power balance at the interface is governed
by the instantaneous impedances, so C1 holds pointwise
in time.
\end{proof}

\noindent
C1 is the First Law applied locally.
It holds exactly for any lossless interface.
Dissipative corrections (Johnson--Nyquist noise,
Arrhenius aging) modify $Z$ over time but never
violate power balance at a single interface.

\begin{remark}[Signal power vs.\ metabolic work]
\label{rem:signal-vs-metabolic}
C1 governs instantaneous \emph{signal power} at each
interface: incident = reflected + transmitted.
The metabolic energy fuelling C2 remodeling enters as a
separate thermodynamic work term that modifies $Z$
itself, not the $\Gamma^2 + T = 1$ identity.
The analogy is an electronic amplifier: the DC power
supply changes the gain (modifies $Z$), yet Kirchhoff's
power law holds at every RF port.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Thermal Dissipation]
\label{cor:thermal}
The reflected fraction $\Gamma_i^2$ of incident power
is re-absorbed by the source impedance and dissipated
as Joule heat:
\begin{equation}
  P_{\mathrm{heat}} = \sum_{i=1}^{N}
  \Gamma_i^2\,P_{\mathrm{in},i}\,.
  \label{eq:p-heat}
\end{equation}
The body temperature in thermal steady state satisfies
$P_{\mathrm{heat}} = hA\,(T_{\mathrm{body}} -
T_{\mathrm{env}})$.
The impedance mismatches that perform computation
therefore \emph{generate the heat that sustains the
temperature at which those computations remain viable}.
\end{corollary}


\subsection{C2: Coefficient Update}
\label{sec:c2}

\begin{theorem}[C2 as gradient descent on $\mathcal{A}$]
\label{thm:c2}
First-order gradient descent on the reflection action
$\mathcal{A}$ (Eq.~\ref{eq:action}) with respect to $Z_i$,
gated by input and output activity, yields:
\begin{equation}
  \boxed{
  \Delta Z_i = -\eta\,\Gamma_i\,x_{\text{in},i}\,
  x_{\text{out},i}\,,
  }
  \label{eq:c2}
\end{equation}
where $\eta > 0$ is the update rate,
$x_{\text{in},i}$ is the input signal, and
$x_{\text{out},i}$ is the transmitted signal.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The functional derivative of $\mathcal{A}$ with respect
to $Z_2$ at an interface gives:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\delta\mathcal{A}}{\delta Z_2}
  = \frac{4 Z_1(Z_2 - Z_1)}{(Z_2 + Z_1)^3}\,
  P_{\text{in}}
  \propto \Gamma\,.
  \label{eq:gradient}
\end{equation}
Gradient descent $\Delta Z = -\eta\,
(\delta\mathcal{A}/\delta Z)$ with activity gating
(update only when both input and output are active)
yields Eq.~\eqref{eq:c2}.
\end{proof}

\noindent
C2 is \emph{not a postulate}: it is the unique
first-order activity-gated update rule that minimises
reflected energy (Section~\ref{sec:uniqueness}).
Its form is identical to Hebb's learning rule when $Z$
is synaptic conductance, to Wolff's law when $Z$ is bone
stiffness, to Glagov's remodeling when $Z$ is vascular
compliance, and to 26~further tissue-specific laws
(Paper~3~\cite{Paper3}).

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Historical note.}
In the original formulation, C2 was called the
``Hebbian update'' with signals $x_{\text{pre}}$,
$x_{\text{post}}$.
Paper~3 proves that the same equation governs all
29~tissue types, making neural-specific terminology
misleading.
We adopt \emph{coefficient update} and
$(x_{\text{in}}, x_{\text{out}})$ throughout.


\subsection{C3: Dimensional Relay}
\label{sec:c3}

\begin{theorem}[C3 \textnormal{(E0)}]
\label{thm:c3}
If two nodes $i,j$ have modal complexities
$K_i \neq K_j$, then
$\Gamma_{ij} = 1$ (total reflection).
C2 cannot remove it because there is no
$Z$-mismatch to minimise---the receiving dimension
simply does not exist.
The only resolution is to insert intermediate
relay nodes forming a $K$-staircase
$K_i > K_{m_1} > K_{m_2} > \cdots > K_j$,
splitting the single $(K_i - K_j)$-mode total
reflection into smaller reflections, each of which
C2 can partially reduce.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
A mode in $i$ with no corresponding degree of
freedom in $j$ sees infinite load impedance
$\to \Gamma = 1$.
This is topological, not impedance-based:
C2 can only adjust $Z$ within existing dimensions.
By induction, a chain of relays
$K_i > K_{m_1} > \cdots > K_j$
minimises total $\Gamma^2$ subject to the
topological constraint. $\square$
\end{proof}

\noindent
C3 explains why biological systems develop layered
architecture:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \emph{Neural}: cortex ($K\!=\!5$) $\to$
    thalamus $\to$ brainstem $\to$ spinal cord
    $\to$ motor neuron $\to$ muscle ($K\!=\!1$).
  \item \emph{Vascular}: aorta $\to$ arteries $\to$
    arterioles $\to$ capillaries---a fractal
    $K$-staircase for impedance matching.
  \item \emph{Skeletal}: bone $\to$ cartilage $\to$
    tendon $\to$ muscle---graded stiffness
    across the joint interface.
\end{itemize}
Without dimensional relay, high-$K$ controllers
and low-$K$ effectors would be separated by
100\% reflection walls, and complex life would
be physically impossible.

\begin{remark}[Metadata co-transport]
Every energy or material flow across an interface
carries $(Z_{\text{source}}, Z_{\text{load}})$ as
metadata, so that $\Gamma_i$ is locally computable
without global network knowledge.
This is not an additional physical law but a
necessary consequence of Theorem~\ref{thm:gamma}:
since $\Gamma_i$ depends only on the local pair
$(Z_{\text{source},i}, Z_{\text{load},i})$,
each carrier (electrical signal, blood, mechanical
load) must encode its source impedance upon
departure.
\end{remark}

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Implementation note.}
In the reference implementation (ALICE), C3 is enforced
by requiring all inter-module values to be
\texttt{ElectricalSignal} objects carrying $Z$ metadata.
Raw floating-point numbers between modules are forbidden.

\section{Mathematical Uniqueness of C2}
\label{sec:uniqueness}

\begin{theorem}[C2 uniqueness]
\label{thm:unique}
Among all first-order, local, activity-gated update rules
of the form $\Delta Z = f(Z, x_{\text{in}}, x_{\text{out}})$,
the rule $\Delta Z = -\eta\,\Gamma\,x_{\text{in}}\,
x_{\text{out}}$ is the \emph{unique} descent direction
that minimises $\mathcal{A}[\Gamma]$ at every step.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Any first-order descent must follow
$\Delta Z \propto -\delta\mathcal{A}/\delta Z$.
By Eq.~\eqref{eq:gradient},
$\delta\mathcal{A}/\delta Z \propto \Gamma$.
Activity gating restricts updates to epochs where
both $x_{\text{in}} \neq 0$ and $x_{\text{out}} \neq 0$.
The unique factorisation satisfying locality, activity
gating, and descent is
$\Delta Z = -\eta\,\Gamma\,x_{\text{in}}\,x_{\text{out}}$
with $\eta > 0$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{The tissue is not the algorithm}

The molecular machineries implementing C2 differ vastly
across tissues: NMDA receptors (neural), RANKL/OPG (bone),
eNOS (vascular), AID somatic hypermutation (immune),
keratinocyte Ca$^{2+}$ (skin), HGF (liver), EPO (marrow),
FSH receptors (reproductive)---completely different
molecular pathways implementing the same equation.
The substrate determines the material constants
($\eta$, $Z_0$); the equation is dictated by physics.

\subsection{Objective function for viability}

The $\Gamma$-framework supplies a \emph{physical}
objective function:
$\mathcal{A} = \int_0^T \sum_i \Gamma_i^2\,
P_{\text{in},i}\,dt$.
Any system whose $\mathcal{A}$ exceeds the viability
bound is physically excluded.
This is a constraint on what \emph{can} exist, not
an explanation of what \emph{does} exist.

\subsection{Sex as a boundary condition}

Sex does not alter C1--C3.
The hypothalamic--pituitary--gonadal (HPG) axis sets
sex-specific $Z_{\text{setpoint}}$ values:
testosterone raises $\eta_{\text{muscle}}$;
oestrogen modulates $Z_{\text{adipose}}$ and
$Z_{\text{bone}}$ (menopause $\equiv Z_{\text{bone}}
\!\uparrow$, $\Gamma_{\text{bone}}\!\uparrow$);
pre-menopausal oestrogen maintains lower
$\Gamma_{\text{vasc}}$ via enhanced endothelial C2.
Sex is a parametric shift in boundary conditions,
not a different dynamical law.

\section{What This Theory Does Not Claim}
\label{sec:scope}

The $\Gamma$-framework derives \emph{physical constraints}
that any living system must satisfy.
It does \emph{not} reconstruct thermodynamic history.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Necessary conditions, not sufficient causes.}
    The framework shows that a $K{=}5$ brain \emph{must}
    maintain $T > T_c(5)$ and \emph{must} have vasculature.
    It does not explain \emph{why} such brains appeared
    or \emph{which} lineages developed them.

  \item \textbf{No historical trajectory.}
    Statements such as ``the tidal zone offers the
    lowest-action path'' identify geometric optima
    in impedance space.
    Whether any lineage actually traversed that path
    is a question for palaeontology, not for this
    framework.

  \item \textbf{C2 uniqueness is mathematical, not
    biological.}
    The proof that C2 is the unique first-order
    $\mathcal{A}$-minimising rule is a theorem about
    gradient descent.
    That multiple molecular machineries converge on the
    same equation is \emph{consistent with} C2 being a
    physical attractor; it is not evidence that history
    ``discovered'' C2.

  \item \textbf{Compatibility, not replacement.}
    Thermodynamics determines which lineages survive;
    the $\Gamma$-framework supplies the physical
    objective function that selection operates on.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
Throughout the six papers, any sentence referencing
the relationship to biology should be read as \emph{physical constraint
language}: ``$X$ is required'' means ``any system with
property~$Y$ must satisfy~$X$\,''.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

The $\Gamma$-Net framework is derived, not postulated.
The First Law of Thermodynamics at a one-dimensional
interface yields the reflection coefficient~$\Gamma$.
Energy conservation gives C1 ($\Gamma^2 + T = 1$).
Gradient descent on the reflection action gives C2
($\Delta Z = -\eta\,\Gamma\,x_{\text{in}}\,
x_{\text{out}}$), proven unique among first-order
activity-gated rules.
Local computability gives C3.

From these three constraints, five companion papers
derive, in a strictly outward deductive chain:
topology and the mind (Paper~1),
geometric quantification and metabolic scaling (Paper~2),
temporal unification across 29~tissues (Paper~3),
disease as topological pathology (Paper~4),
and full-system verification against epidemiological
data (Paper~5).

This paper provides the master reference:
a derivation chain (Sections~\ref{sec:gamma}--\ref{sec:uniqueness}),
a quantity inventory (Table~\ref{tab:quantities}),
and a theorem directory (Table~\ref{tab:directory}).
